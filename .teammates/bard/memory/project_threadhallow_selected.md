User selected **Threadhallow** as the base world for LoreWeave.

**Theme:** Folk horror meets high fantasy. Reality is a tapestry woven by ancient beings (the Weavers) who vanished 1,200 years ago. Mortal civilizations maintain the weave through ritual. The weave is now degrading (the Thinning).

**Why:** The weave metaphor maps 1:1 to engine architecture (proc gen = weaving, event sourcing = tapestry memory, world healing = re-knitting, archetypes = repeating patterns). Folk horror tone supports the cozy-to-unsettling gradient that biome/settlement proximity naturally creates.

**How to apply:** All narrative content (factions, quests, NPCs, archetypes, vocabulary) should be grounded in Threadhallow's themes. Magic is Threadcraft. Settlements are protected by wards. The wilds are defined by weave stability. The Weavers' disappearance is the overarching mystery.
