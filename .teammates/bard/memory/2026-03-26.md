- **Key decisions:**
  - World name: **Threadhallow** (thread + hallow — sacred woven ground)
  - Core premise: Reality is woven by ancient Weavers who vanished 1,200 years ago; mortals maintain the weave through ritual
  - Current era: **The Thinning** — weave degrading faster than traditions can maintain it
  - 5 major themes: Memory vs. Forgetting, Old vs. New, Folk Horror, Interconnection, Discovery Over Exposition
  - 3-layer cosmology: The Tapestry (physical world), The Loom (metaphysical framework), The Fray (between-space)
  - Magic system: **Threadcraft** — all magic is manipulation of the weave, mapped to D&D traditions
  - 5 factions sketched: Threadwalker Orders, Wardweaver Guilds, Rememberers, Hollow Court, Common Folk
  - Engine alignment table showing how every technical concept maps to a narrative metaphor
  - Tone: folk horror — cozy in settlements, unsettling at edges, dangerous in frayed zones

## Task: Resolve Open Questions

- **What:** Resolved all 5 open questions from the world bible, expanding `threadhallow.md` from 10 to 15 sections
- **Decisions made:**
  - **Calendar — The Wheel of Binding:** 8 ceremonies on solstices/equinoxes/cross-quarters. Mechanically significant — 12% ward decay per missed ceremony. 12 months with thread-themed names (Stillthread, Deepknot, Thawsong, Greenweave, Brightloom, Longstitch, Highthread, Goldenweft, Wanespindle, Fraywatch, Harvestknot, Hollowdark). The Hollowing (late autumn) is the most critical/dangerous.
  - **Naming conventions:** Anglo-Celtic folk roots confirmed. Compound settlement names (Thornwick, Millhaven, Bracken Fell). Thread-motif professional names for Wardweavers and Threadwalkers. Fray creatures get descriptive folklore names (Looselings, Thread-eaters, Hollow Walkers).
  - **Weavers' fate — discoverable via main quest:** The Weavers wove themselves into the Tapestry to save it from a fundamental structural flaw. 5-stage discovery arc (local → regional → continental → cosmological → truth). Endgame offers 3 choices: Restore (re-cohere Weavers), Release (let them fade), Harmonize (sustainable partnership — hardest, best).
  - **Scale of Unraveling — permanent, not a timer:** Accelerating condition, not a doomsday clock. Patchy and organic, responsive to player action. Specific locations can improve or worsen. The world adapts — new traditions, Fray ecology, the Hollow Court grows.
  - **Pantheon — the Loom-Born (6 gods):** Separate from the Weavers. Emerged spontaneously from the Tapestry. Subject to the Thinning. Each embodies an aspect of the woven world. Kael Thornmantle (Wild/Nature), Seren Stillwater (Knowledge), Voss Ironweft (Protection/Craft), Asha Brightloom (Light/Community), Morrith Hollowdark (Death/Liminal), Tael Quickstitch (Change/Trickery). Worship is informal — knotting gestures, Loom Stones, no organized churches.
- **Files changed:** `world/lore/threadhallow.md` (sections 10-15)
- **Remaining open questions:** Starting region, companion system, faction reputation, crafting/Threadcraft, audio direction
