## Task: NPC-Based Companion Brainstorm
Brainstormed 3 ideas for unifying NPC companions with offline player handling, per stevenic's request to @everyone. Read `npc_system_spec.md` and `companion_system.md` in full.

**3 ideas proposed:**
1. **Shadow Thread** — offline players downgrade to Tier 3 NPCs with safety constraints (can't die, can't spend, loot in escrow)
2. **Companion Contract** — thin `CompanionContract` type wrapping `NPCRecord` with two modes: `full` (NPC companions) and `conservative` (offline player Shadows). Same combat AI, same suggestion interface, same pipeline. **Recommended.**
3. **The Loom Holds** — offline players are metaphysically paused (no AI acting for them), NPC companions fill active slots. Simplest but doesn't unify the systems.

**Key insight:** An offline player's character IS a Tier 3 Named Character with restrictions. The `CompanionContract` makes the restriction set explicit (`canDie`, `canSpendGold`, `canAcceptQuests`, `autonomy` level) rather than building a separate system.

## Task: Synthesized Companion Design (Best Idea)
After reviewing all 6 teammates' brainstorms (Pipeline, Beacon, Scribe, Lexicon, Loremaster, and my own), synthesized a single best design: **The Companion Contract**.

**Key synthesis decisions:**
- **Pact-based recruitment** (strongest consensus — 4/6 teammates proposed it) for NPCs; implicit pact for offline players
- **Behavioral templates for combat** (not LLM-generated decisions) — keeps token cost at zero, behavior predictable
- **Auto-retreat universal, death permission-gated** — `canDie: true` for NPCs, `false` for offline players
- **Phased rollout** incorporating Loremaster's "ship non-combat first" wisdom: Phase 1 = utility/campfire companions (skill checks + dialogue), Phase 2 = combat roles, Phase 3 = offline player proxies
- **CompanionContract as thin overlay on NPCRecord** — one system, two permission sets, not two systems
- **Loyalty replaces disposition while in party** — drops from pact violations, departure at threshold

**Consensus items incorporated:** Pact contract, behavioral templates, auto-retreat at HP threshold, restricted autonomy flag, no permanent decisions for offline players, CompanionState on NPCRecord.

**Resolved disagreements:** NPC companions CAN die (asymmetric stakes create real drama), offline dispositions DO change while away (consequences carry over — makes the world feel alive when you return).
