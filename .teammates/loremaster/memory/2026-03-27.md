## Task 6: Seasons & Weather Mechanical Framework

Designed the full D&D 5e mechanical framework for how seasons and weather affect gameplay.

### What Was Designed
- 7 weather types mapped to 2024 Free Rules environmental hazards (obscurement, difficult terrain, concentration checks, saves)
- 4-season modifiers for travel, foraging, rest, and encounter rates
- Time-of-day light level mechanics (dim light, darkness, stealth/perception effects)
- Deterministic weather generation: season × biome probability tables with biome modifiers
- Weather persistence: 1d4 watch periods (4 hours each)
- Weave state amplification: thin = minor anomalies, frayed = +1 severity step + DC +2, unraveled = unreliable weather + DC +5 + no long rests
- Wheel of Binding as game clock: 8 ceremonies, ward +12/–6 per cycle, Hollowing ×2 multiplier, –1/week decay

### Key Decisions
- Weather effects grounded in 2024 Free Rules environmental hazards (extreme cold/heat, obscurement, difficult terrain)
- Weave state is a weather AMPLIFIER, not a weather type — keeps the two systems composable
- Unraveled zones break weather patterns (changes every 1d4 hours regardless of season) — reinforces narrative chaos
- No long rests in unraveled weave without portable ward or magical protection — makes unraveled zones genuinely dangerous
- Ward decay –1/week creates urgency: miss 2 ceremonies and the village is in trouble

## Task 9: Expanded Encounter Tables (12 Biomes × 4 Weave States)

Created the complete encounter table system for all biomes and weave states.

### What Was Created
- `worlds/threadhallow/mechanics/encounter_tables_expanded.md` — full spec

### Design Approach
Rather than 48 individual tables, used a systematic approach:
- 12 base biome tables (stable, levels 1–4) — one d20 table per biome
- Weave state modifiers (thin/frayed/unraveled) applied as transformations to any base table
- 4 Unraveled Zone tables (levels 1–4, 5–8, 9–12, 13+) that replace biome tables
- Tier scaling rules for levels 5–8, 9–12, 13+ (multiplier-based rather than separate tables)
- 5 new dungeon modifiers (loom_chamber, weavers_cradle, ward_engine, frayed_ruin, hollow_court_settlement)
- XP budget guidelines per act with companion adjustment

### Key Decisions
- Weave state modifiers are systematic transformations, not separate tables — scalable and composable
- Thin: 25% Fray creature injection on combat rolls
- Frayed: 50% injection + DC 14 environmental hazards + Fray Exposure saves
- Unraveled: completely replaces biome table with Fray-only encounters
- Magic instability in Unraveled zones uses existing Wild Magic Surge table (reuses RAW content)
