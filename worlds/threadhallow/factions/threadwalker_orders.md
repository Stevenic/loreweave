# The Threadwalker Orders

*Wandering maintainers of the weave. Part ranger, part monk, part field surgeon for reality itself.*

---

## Overview

The Threadwalker Orders are the most visible defenders of the weave. Where Wardweavers protect settlements from within, Threadwalkers patrol the wilds — walking the Threadlines, mending small tears before they spread, mapping the Thinning's advance, and dealing with Fray creatures.

**Headquarters:** No central base. Each order maintains a network of **wayposts** — small, warded shelters along major Threadlines where Threadwalkers resupply and exchange reports.

**Size:** ~2,000 active Threadwalkers across all orders. Small compared to the territory they cover.

**Reputation:** Respected by common folk (they're the ones who actually show up when the forest goes wrong), tolerated by Wardweaver guilds (who consider them undisciplined), and monitored by city authorities (wanderers with power make governments nervous).

---

## The Three Orders

### The Mending Path

**Philosophy:** Restore the original patterns. The Weavers knew what they were doing; mortal innovations are patches on a masterwork.

**Methods:** Study pre-Silence Weaver techniques. Repair tears using traditional Binding Songs. Preserve Loom Stones and Weaver's Marks. Refuse to improvise — if the old method doesn't work, find the old method you haven't learned yet.

**Strengths:** Deep knowledge of the weave's original structure. Best success rate at stabilizing thin places. Trusted by the Rememberers, who share archival access.

**Weaknesses:** Inflexible. Slow to adapt to new types of Thinning. Dismissive of Common Folk traditions as "corrupted echoes."

**Internal conflict:** Some younger Mending Path walkers privately experiment with new techniques but hide it from their elders.

**Key NPC:** **Pathmaster Aldric Longstitch** — Veteran walker, 50s, scarred, speaks softly. Has walked every Threadline in the northern region. Believes the Thinning is accelerating but trusts in the old ways. The player's primary Threadwalker contact in Act II.

---

### The New Stitch

**Philosophy:** The old ways are incomplete — the Weavers left gaps. Mortals must develop new binding techniques for a world the Weavers never anticipated.

**Methods:** Experimental Threadcraft. Hybrid techniques combining Wardweaving and Threadwalking. Willing to study Fray-touched phenomena for insight. Field-test new approaches in real thin places.

**Strengths:** Adaptable. Often the first to find solutions for novel Thinning patterns. Attract younger recruits who chafe under the Mending Path's conservatism.

**Weaknesses:** Higher failure rate. Some experiments go badly — a New Stitch team once accidentally created a persistent Frayed zone by trying to "overcorrect" a tear. The Mending Path never lets them forget it.

**Internal conflict:** The line between "innovative" and "reckless" is debated constantly. Some New Stitch walkers have quietly adopted Fraytouch techniques — the forbidden tradition.

**Key NPC:** **Farweft** — Mid-30s, intense, curious. A Mending Path member who transferred to the New Stitch after her patrol partner was killed by a Fray manifestation that old techniques couldn't handle. She's the player's traveling companion in Acts II–III and a potential romance option.

---

### The Frayborn

**Philosophy:** The Fray is not the enemy — it's raw creative potential. Controlled exposure to the Fray can strengthen both the weave and the walker.

**Methods:** Deliberate, ritualized contact with Frayed zones. Study of Fray-touched creatures and Hollow Court individuals. Development of techniques that use Fray energy to reinforce (not just repair) the weave.

**Strengths:** Can operate in Frayed zones where other walkers can't survive. Understand Fray creatures better than anyone. Have the most contact with the Hollow Court.

**Weaknesses:** The other orders consider them dangerous at best, corrupted at worst. The Wardweaver guilds have formally censured them. Some Frayborn walkers *are* partially Fray-touched, raising questions about where the order ends and the Hollow Court begins.

**Internal conflict:** How much Fray exposure is too much? The order has lost members who went too deep and never fully came back. Their leader insists on strict protocols, but enforcement is difficult in the wilds.

**Key NPC:** **Thinwalker** (no other name given) — Age unclear (Fray exposure alters appearance). Calm, philosophical, slightly unsettling. Speaks about the Fray with the familiarity of someone describing a neighbor. Founded the order after surviving a Fray immersion that should have killed her. She sees things in the Threadlines that others can't — and some of what she sees frightens her.

## Inter-Order Relations

| | Mending Path | New Stitch | Frayborn |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Mending Path** | — | Tolerate, distrust their experiments | Oppose, consider them corrupted |
| **New Stitch** | Respect their knowledge, resent their rigidity | — | Cautiously study their findings from a distance |
| **Frayborn** | "They'll understand when the old ways fail them" | "Closest to the right idea, afraid to commit" | — |

All three orders share wayposts and will cooperate against immediate threats. The political friction is about *method*, not *mission*. They all want to save the weave.

## Threadwalker Traditions

- **The Walk** — A Threadwalker's patrol route. Each walker is assigned a Walk (a specific Threadline circuit) and is expected to complete it regularly. Missing your Walk is shameful.
- **Path-names** — Walkers abandon their birth names and take names from the Walk: Farweft, Longstitch, Thinwalker, Fraywatch, Knotsinger.
- **Trail Signs** — Carved symbols left at wayposts and along Threadlines: warnings, condition reports, directions. The player learns to read these.
- **The Silence Toast** — Before every meal, Threadwalkers pour a drop of water on bare earth. "For the Weavers, wherever they walk." The irony (given the truth) is powerful once the player knows.
