# The Hollow Court

*Survivors of the Fray. Changed, distrusted, and possibly the key to everything.*

---

## Overview

The Hollow Court is not an organization — it's a community of outcasts. Its members are **Fray-touched**: individuals who have been exposed to raw Fray energy and survived, altered. They are feared by the general population, distrusted by the guilds, and monitored by the Threadwalkers.

The name is their own. They chose "Hollow" because the Fray takes something from everyone it touches — a memory, a sense, a certainty. And "Court" because they refuse to be ashamed of what they've become. If the world treats them as monsters, they'll govern themselves with dignity.

**Headquarters:** No fixed base. The Court clusters in and around Frayed zones — the only places they feel fully comfortable. The largest Hollow Court settlement is in **Hollowreach**, a town that was abandoned by its original population when a Frayed zone expanded over it.

**Size:** ~500 known Fray-touched. Probably more who hide their condition.

**Reputation:** Feared. Tolerated in frontier regions where pragmatism outweighs prejudice. Banned from some cities. The Frayborn Threadwalkers are the only faction with genuine sympathy for them.

---

## What Fray-Touch Does

Exposure to the Fray changes people, but not uniformly. Common effects:

| Effect | Description | Frequency |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| **Sensory shift** | One sense operates "between" — seeing Threadlines without the Sight, hearing the Fray's hum | Very common |
| **Memory loss** | Specific memories erased — a name, a year, a relationship. Not cognitive decline, just gaps | Common |
| **Temporal perception** | Mild time-sense distortion. A Fray-touched person always knows the exact time but can't estimate durations | Common |
| **Physical alteration** | Subtle — eyes that reflect differently, skin that's slightly cool, hair that doesn't grow. Not monstrous | Uncommon |
| **Fray resonance** | Can sense Fray activity over long distances. Can enter Frayed zones safely. Can *feel* the Threadlines | Rare |
| **Pattern sight** | Sees the weave's structure directly — not just Threadlines but the full fabric of reality | Very rare |

The condition is permanent but stable. Fray-touch doesn't worsen over time (despite what the guilds claim). Additional exposure does increase effects, which is why the Frayborn Threadwalkers are controversial — they deliberately seek what others survive by accident.

## Society

The Hollow Court operates on two principles:

1. **No one is turned away.** If you're Fray-touched, you belong. Regardless of how it happened, how severe it is, or what you did before.
2. **You contribute what you can.** The Court survives because its members work. Fray-touched who can enter Frayed zones salvage artifacts and materials. Those with pattern sight serve as scouts and navigators. Those with sensory shifts work as sentries. Everyone eats.

### Internal Structure

- **The Unspoken** — The Court's informal council. Five to seven senior members who make collective decisions. Called "Unspoken" because they govern by consensus, not decree.
- **Salvagers** — Teams that enter Frayed zones to recover artifacts, materials, and sometimes people. The most dangerous role.
- **Speakers** — Fray-touched who can still pass as normal. They handle trade and diplomacy with the outside world. Exhausting, thankless work.
- **Keepers** — Those who tend to newly Fray-touched arrivals. Part therapist, part teacher. They help new members understand what's changed and what hasn't.

## The Court's Secret

The Hollow Court knows something that no other faction does: **the Fray is not empty**.

When Fray-touched individuals with pattern sight look into the Fray, they don't see void or chaos. They see *potential* — unstructured but not random. Some of them report seeing flickers of pattern in the Fray that resemble fragments of the Tapestry's weave. As if the Fray contains echoes of things that *could be* woven but haven't been yet.

The Court's most experienced members believe the Fray is what the Weavers drew from to create the Tapestry. It's not the enemy of reality — it's the *raw material* of reality. The Thinning is the Tapestry unraveling back into the Fray, not the Fray destroying the Tapestry. The distinction matters enormously.

This insight is why the Hollow Court is essential to every path in the main quest:
- **Restoration** — The Court can perceive the Weavers' distributed consciousness directly
- **Release** — The Court understands Fray energy well enough to design containment for the Ward Engines
- **Harmony** — The Court's pattern-sight practitioners can serve as interpreters between the Weavers' frequency and mortal perception

## Key NPCs

### Wren Looseweft

**Role:** De facto leader of Hollowreach. Former blacksmith.

**Personality:** Grounded, pragmatic, dry humor. She was a normal small-town blacksmith until the Frayed zone expanded over her town. Most people fled. She stayed — partly stubbornness, partly because she realized the zone wasn't killing her, just changing her. She organized the remaining residents into the first Hollow Court settlement.

**Significance:** The player meets her in Act II. She's the player's introduction to the idea that the Fray isn't simply destructive. Her firsthand experience of living inside a Frayed zone provides critical context for Act III's revelations.

**Arc:** Wren becomes a key ally in Act IV regardless of path. She doesn't care about factional politics — she cares about her people surviving. She'll support whichever path gives the Fray-touched a future.

### Glasseye

**Role:** Salvager team leader. Has the rarest form of Fray-touch: full pattern sight.

**Personality:** Quiet, intense, sees too much. Glasseye (the only name he answers to) can see the full structure of the weave at all times. He sees the world as most people see a tapestry — threads, knots, patterns, frays, the spaces between. He's learned to function in this state, but conversations with him are unsettling because he describes people as "patterns" and buildings as "knots."

**Significance:** Glasseye is the first person who can confirm the player's Act III revelation from direct perception. He's *seen* the Weavers in the Threadlines. He thought they were dead dreams caught in the weave. Learning they're alive — distributed but conscious — changes everything for him.

**Arc:** Glasseye becomes essential to the Harmony path (he can perceive the Weavers' responses during the first true Binding Song). On the Restoration path, he guides the ceremonies. On the Release path, he monitors the Weavers' dissolution to ensure it's peaceful.

## Hollow Court Traditions

- **The Naming** — Fray-touched members choose a new name when they join. Not required, but most do — it marks the boundary between who they were and who they are. Names are often descriptive: Glasseye, Looseweft, Stillhand, Halfmemory.
- **The Counting** — Every Hollowing (the most dangerous ceremony night), the Court counts its members. The number is added to a woven record — a physical thread-count that tracks the Court's growth. It's simultaneously a census and a defiance.
- **Fray Gifts** — When a new member arrives, existing members each give them something from outside the Fray — a normal apple, a letter, a coin. Reminders of what the world is like when it's whole. Simple, powerful.
