# The Rememberers

*Scholars, archivists, and lorekeepers. They don't weave — they remember.*

---

## Overview

The Rememberers are not practitioners of Threadcraft. They are scholars dedicated to recovering, preserving, and interpreting everything the Weavers left behind: inscriptions, artifacts, architectural patterns, oral traditions, linguistic fragments, and the degraded echoes of Binding Songs.

They believe that the solution to the Thinning lies in *understanding*, not power. If mortals can decode what the Weavers actually did — and why — they can find a way forward.

**Headquarters:** The **Archive of Threads** in Loomhearth — a library built around a Loom Stone cluster. The stone's resonance preserves documents that would otherwise decay from Fray exposure.

**Size:** ~300 active members. Tiny compared to the other factions, but their influence is outsized because everyone needs their knowledge.

**Reputation:** Respected by all, understood by few. The Threadwalkers value their archival work. The Wardweaver Guilds fund them (and try to control what they publish). The Hollow Court trades them Fray-sourced artifacts for analysis.

---

## Structure

The Rememberers have no hierarchy in the military sense. They organize by **specialty**:

| Division | Focus |
|----------|-------|
| **The Verse-Keepers** | Oral traditions, Binding Songs, musical notation |
| **The Stone-Readers** | Weaver's Marks, Loom Stone inscriptions, architectural patterns |
| **The Thread-Linguists** | The Weaver language (fragmentary, debated) |
| **The Field-Scholars** | On-site research in ruins, thin places, and Frayed zones |
| **The Chroniclers** | Contemporary history — tracking the Thinning's progress region by region |

### Names

Rememberers take the name of a text, artifact, or tradition they've sworn to preserve:
- "Keeper of Hollow Songs" (called **Verse** informally)
- "Reader of the Northwall Marks"
- "The One Who Holds the Reedmere Count"

This tradition means every Rememberer is personally accountable for a specific piece of knowledge surviving.

---

## The Central Tension

The Rememberers sit on the most important knowledge in the world, and they know it. But knowledge is power, and power attracts control:

- **The Wardweaver Guilds fund the Archive** — and expect publication to go through guild review. Findings that undermine guild doctrine (like evidence the Thinning is structural, not cyclical) get quietly shelved.
- **The Rememberers cooperate** — because they need the funding — but maintain private archives that the guilds don't know about. The tension between institutional compliance and scholarly integrity is a constant source of drama.
- **The Hollow Court trades freely** — they don't care about guild politics. Rememberers who deal with the Hollow Court gain access to Fray-sourced artifacts but risk guild censure.

## Key NPCs

### Keeper of the Third Verse ("Verse")

**Role:** Senior Rememberer. Specialist in Binding Songs. Political leader of the Archive (by default — someone has to negotiate with the guilds).

**Personality:** Warm, scattered, fierce when cornered. Looks like an absent-minded professor. Is actually one of the sharpest political minds in Loomhearth — she just saves it for when it matters.

**Significance:** She has a theory — unpublished, shared only with trusted colleagues — that the Binding Songs are not rituals but *conversations*. She can't prove it because the original Weaver language is too fragmentary. She's closer to the truth than anyone alive. The player's findings in Act III confirm her theory.

**Arc:** Verse becomes the intellectual architect of the Harmony path. She designs the Speaker training program and composes the first true Binding Song.

### Wynn Hollowscript

**Role:** Field-Scholar. Self-exiled to the Threadfall Expanse for twenty years.

**Personality:** Brilliant, obsessive, lonely, half-feral. Has been living alone in a tent on the grasslands, mapping Threadline vibration patterns with hand-drawn charts. Other Rememberers think she's gone mad. She's actually made the critical breakthrough — the Threadlines carry structured information.

**Significance:** Her linguistic framework is the Rosetta Stone for the Weaver language. Without her work, the Harmony path is impossible and the Restoration/Release paths are significantly harder.

**Arc:** The player finds her in Act III. Convincing her to share her work (she's protective and paranoid after years of isolation) is a significant quest. She can become a companion if the player earns her trust.

## Rememberer Traditions

- **The Recitation** — Monthly gathering where Rememberers recite their sworn texts from memory. If anyone falters, the group corrects them. This is how pre-literate traditions were preserved for centuries. It still works.
- **The Binding Obligation** — A Rememberer who discovers new knowledge must share it with the Archive within one year. Failing to do so is the only expellable offense. (This rule is why the guild-controlled publication pipeline is so frustrating — the knowledge reaches the Archive but gets bottlenecked before reaching the public.)
- **Ink and Thread** — Rememberers carry a spool of thread and a pen at all times. They write in a shorthand that uses both text and woven knot-patterns (quipu-like). Their notebooks are half-written, half-woven.
