# Act I Side Quests — The Fraying Village (Levels 1-4)

*Hand-crafted quests that introduce the world's tone and mechanics in Thornwick's immediate surroundings. Each teaches the player something about the weave, the Fray, or the people who live between them.*

---

## 1. The Scarecrow

**Location:** Greensward Farm, south of Thornwick
**Trigger:** Talking to any farmer after the first morning in Thornwick, or exploring the fields
**Tags:** investigation, folk horror, introduction to Fray
**Template basis:** Creature Problem

### Setup
A farmer named **Harlen Greensward** reports his scarecrow has moved — first to a different corner of the field, then to the fence, then to the road. His dog won't go near it. His chickens have stopped laying.

### Investigation
- Perception DC 12: The scarecrow's burlap face has faint, thread-like filaments running under the surface. They pulse faintly at dusk.
- Arcana DC 13: A minor Fray breach under the field is leaking weave energy. The scarecrow is a **Thread-Puppet** — not animated by intent, but by leaked pattern. It's mimicking its purpose (guarding the field) but extending it (guarding the road, guarding the village).
- Nature DC 11: The breach is tiny. A Wardweaver could seal it in minutes. Without one, it'll expand slowly.

### Resolution Options
1. **Unbind it** (Arcana DC 12) — Sever the Fray filaments. The scarecrow collapses. The breach remains but stops feeding. Safe, clean. Harlen is relieved.
2. **Study it** (Arcana DC 14, takes 2 hours) — Learn about Thread-Puppets and minor Fray breaches. Grants advantage on future Arcana checks involving Fray creatures of CR 1 or lower. The scarecrow is destroyed in the process.
3. **Leave it** — The scarecrow continues expanding its patrol. Within three days it reaches the village square. Causes alarm. Colm Bindstone eventually talks it down with a folk ward (demonstrating that Common Folk traditions have real power). No harm done, but the village is shaken.

### Reward
- XP appropriate for a CR 1 investigation encounter
- Lore: introduction to Fray breaches, Thread-Puppets, and the ward-circle's limits
- If studied: mechanical knowledge advantage (see above)
- Relationship: Harlen becomes a reliable information source about field conditions and weather patterns

### Design Notes
This is the player's first encounter with the Fray — it should be creepy but not dangerous. The scarecrow is folk horror distilled: the familiar made wrong. It teaches that the Fray isn't evil, just *uncontained*.

## 2. Lost Flock

**Location:** The Thinning Forest, northeast of Thornwick
**Trigger:** Talking to Tam Woolsey after the Prologue
**Tags:** exploration, moral choice, introduction to weave anomalies
**Template basis:** Missing Person (livestock variant)

### Setup
Shepherd **Tam Woolsey** lost three sheep when they wandered past the ward-circle boundary into the Thinning Forest two days ago. He's too scared to go after them. They're his livelihood — without them, his family faces a hard winter.

### Investigation
- Survival DC 11: Sheep tracks leading northeast, past the ward boundary marker (a carved stone post with faded thread-knots).
- Perception DC 13 (beyond the boundary): The forest is subtly wrong. Trees grow in slight spirals. Birdsong stops and starts in unnatural patterns. The air has a faint metallic tang. (The player is experiencing a thin place for the first time.)
- Survival DC 13: The sheep are in a small clearing about an hour past the boundary. They're alive.

### The Flock
The sheep are *changed*. Their wool has a faint luminescence — silver-white, like moonlight trapped in fiber. Their eyes are milky but they can clearly see. They don't bleat normally — the sound has a resonant, almost musical quality. They're calm. Too calm.

- Arcana DC 12: Prolonged exposure to the thin place has saturated their wool with ambient weave energy. They're not Fray creatures — they're just… infused. The effect is cosmetic and will fade over weeks if they're brought back inside the ward-circle.
- Nature DC 14: The wool itself is genuinely unusual. A Wardweaver could use it as raw material for binding work. It's not valuable in gold, but it's valuable in *thread*.

### Resolution Options
1. **Return them** — Tam is deeply grateful. The sheep normalize over 2-3 weeks as the ward-circle's stable weave bleaches out the infusion. The other sheep are spooked at first but accept them. (Demonstrates: the weave self-corrects small anomalies.)
2. **Return them and tell Sorrel** — If Sorrel has been found, she's fascinated. She harvests a small amount of the luminous wool before it fades. Gives the player a woven token that glows faintly — a minor light source and a narrative keepsake.
3. **Don't return them** — Leave them in the forest. They survive; they're adapted. Tam is devastated. Over the following weeks, other animals near the boundary start showing similar changes. The village becomes more afraid.

### Reward
- XP appropriate for a level 1-2 exploration encounter
- Lore: introduction to thin places, weave infusion, and the self-correcting nature of stable weave
- If returned with Sorrel's involvement: luminous wool token (light source, flavor item)
- Relationship: Tam's trust (or resentment, if you leave the flock)

### Design Notes
This quest is about the player's first trip outside the ward-circle. The thin place should feel *wrong* — not dangerous, just unsettling. The sheep are the payoff: the Fray doesn't just destroy, it *changes*. And sometimes the changes are beautiful.

## 3. The Peddler's Map

**Location:** Thornwick market / the surrounding wilds
**Trigger:** Old Hawthorn visits Thornwick (3-5 days after Prologue)
**Tags:** exploration, multi-site, introduction to world structure
**Template basis:** Ruin Exploration + The Stranger hybrid

### Setup
The traveling merchant **Old Hawthorn** rolls into Thornwick with his cart, selling mundane goods and dubious curiosities. Among his wares: a hand-drawn map of "safe paths through the thin places." He charges 5 gold — steep for a village economy — and insists it's "worth more than your life, but I'm feeling charitable."

### The Map
The map marks five locations in the surrounding wilds. Hawthorn claims all five are safe passages. He's half right.

| # | Location | What Hawthorn Says | What's Actually There |
|---|----------|-------------------|----------------------|
| 1 | **Miller's Crossing** | "Safe path through the eastern forest" | Accurate. A stable trail through thin territory. Good route. |
| 2 | **The Bent Oak** | "Sheltered campsite, overnight safe" | Accurate. A natural rest point where a massive oak blocks Fray drift. |
| 3 | **The Rememberer's Cache** | "Old ruin, nothing dangerous" | Partially true. A hidden Rememberer supply cache under a collapsed shrine. Trapped with a mundane tripwire (Investigation DC 12). Contains: 3 preserved scrolls of local Weaver's Marks, a ward-stone fragment, and a coded letter in Rememberer cipher. |
| 4 | **The Walker's Camp** | "Abandoned Threadwalker camp, good salvage" | True but misleading. The camp is abandoned because the Threadwalker sealed a thin place here — it's the seal the player can see. Investigation DC 14 reveals the seal is weakening. If tampered with, releases a Looseling swarm (4 Looselings, CR 1/4 each). |
| 5 | **The Still Pool** | "Beautiful spot, crystal water" | Dangerous. A Frayed pocket — a small zone where reality has properly torn. The pool reflects a different sky. Time stutters near the edge. A single Frayed One wanders the shore. Not aggressive unless approached within 15 feet. |

### Resolution
There's no single "end" — the player explores at their own pace over Act I. Each site teaches something:
- **Miller's Crossing + Bent Oak**: The world outside the ward is navigable if you know the paths
- **Rememberer's Cache**: Other factions are operating in the area, leaving traces. Seeds the Rememberer plotline.
- **Walker's Camp**: Threadwalkers maintain the weave actively. Their work has consequences.
- **The Still Pool**: The Fray is real, present, and close. First encounter with a Frayed One — the human cost of the Thinning.

### Old Hawthorn's Secret
- Insight DC 15: Hawthorn knows the map is partially dangerous. He's *testing* the player — or evaluating them.
- If confronted after visiting the Still Pool: He's evasive but impressed if the player survived. "Most people don't come back from that one asking questions. They come back asking for a refund."
- His connection to the Hollow Court (noted in his NPC profile) is NOT revealed in Act I. It's a seed for Act II.

### Reward
- Per-site XP (exploration, combat if triggered, investigation)
- The map itself becomes a useful travel aid for the rest of Act I
- Rememberer scrolls (can be translated by Sorrel for additional Loom Stone lore)
- Relationship with Old Hawthorn (complex — he's neither ally nor enemy, he's an evaluator)
- World knowledge: the player now has a mental model of what stable/thin/frayed territory feels like

### Design Notes
This is the Act I "open world sampler." It rewards exploration and teaches the biome gradient (stable → thin → frayed) through direct experience. Old Hawthorn is the player's first brush with someone who knows more than they should — foreshadowing the Hollow Court and the broader factional landscape.
