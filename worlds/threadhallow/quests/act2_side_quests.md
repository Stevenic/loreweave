# Act II Side Quests — The Threadwalker's Path (Levels 5-8)

*Hand-crafted quests that flesh out the journey from Thornwick to Loomhearth and the political landscape. Each connects to the main narrative.*

---

## 1. The Silence Toast

**Location:** Travel camp between Thornwick and Ashenmoor
**Trigger:** Traveling with Farweft as companion; first night camping
**Tags:** social, companion, lore
**Template basis:** None (unique companion quest)

### Setup
Farweft performs a quiet ritual at dusk: she sets out two cups of water, drinks one, and pours the other on the ground. If asked, she deflects. A Perception DC 14 notices her lips moving during the pour — she's whispering a name.

### Investigation
- Insight DC 13: She's grieving. This is habitual.
- Persuasion DC 15 (or relationship threshold): She explains the Silence Toast — a Mending Path tradition for honoring the lost. She's toasting **Hest**, her patrol partner who vanished in a sealed thin place two years ago. She transferred to the New Stitch order to escape the grief, but she still performs the toast.
- History DC 12: The Silence Toast is specific to the Mending Path. New Stitch walkers don't do it. She's practicing the traditions of the order she left.

### Outcome
No mechanical resolution — this is a relationship-building scene. Opens Farweft's personal quest arc ("The Walker's Silence") and plants the seed for Hest's survival reveal in Act III. If the player participates in the toast, Farweft's relationship moves toward trust.

## 2. The Drowned Ward

**Location:** Ashenmoor — the sunken ward-circle
**Trigger:** Exploring Ashenmoor after recovering the Reedmere Journal
**Tags:** exploration, mystery, combat
**Template basis:** Fray Breach + Haunting hybrid

### Setup
The Reedmere ward-circle is half-submerged. The standing stones lean at angles. Thread-Puppets have made nests in the ruins — scarecrows assembled from Reedmere's debris: fence posts, thatch, a child's doll.

### Stages
1. **Approach** — 4 Thread-Puppets shambling through the shallows. Combat or Stealth DC 14 to bypass.
2. **The Ward-Circle** — The stones are still faintly active. Arcana DC 15: the ward isn't dead, it's in a feedback loop — trying to activate without a Wardweaver to direct it, cycling on and off.
3. **The Echo** — Near the central stone, a Frayed One manifests. This is the weave-echo of **Liss Threadhollow** (the murdered Wardweaver). She's not truly Liss — she's a pattern repeating. She reaches for the central stone, tries to activate the ward, fails, and resets. Over and over.
4. **Resolution options:**
   - **Destroy the echo** — Combat. Ends the loop. The ward goes fully dead. Safe, but something's lost.
   - **Complete the loop** — Arcana DC 16 or Binding Song performance. The player finishes what Liss was trying to do. The ward flickers to life briefly — 30 seconds of full power — then dies permanently. But Liss's echo dissolves peacefully.
   - **Anchor the echo** — Arcana DC 18. Bind Liss's pattern to the central stone as a permanent, minimal ward. Not enough to protect a settlement, but enough to mark this as sacred ground. The Frayed One vanishes. The stone glows.

### Reward
- XP from Thread-Puppet combat
- Lore: the ward-circle's behavior demonstrates that wards can persist (barely) without a Wardweaver — important context for the Release path in Act IV
- If anchored: a future fast-travel/rest point in Ashenmoor
- Emotional: confronting what happens when a Wardweaver fails (and the system that let it happen)

## 3. The Cairn Offerings

**Location:** The Singing Cairn summit
**Trigger:** After arriving at the Cairn, before descending to repair anchor stones
**Tags:** lore, mystery, social
**Template basis:** Request From Beyond

### Setup
Among the centuries of Threadwalker offerings on the Cairn, one is recent — a woven token left within the past week. It's a Mending Path offering style, but the thread used is unusual: silvery, slightly luminescent, not a material any living Threadwalker uses.

### Investigation
- Investigation DC 12: The token is fresh. The thread is warm to the touch.
- Arcana DC 14: The thread isn't dyed or treated — it's **raw Threadline material**, physically woven into a token. No mortal craft can do this.
- If Farweft is present: She recognizes the Mending Path pattern. It's Hest's style. She's shaken.
- Survival DC 15: Tracks lead away from the Cairn toward the west — toward the sealed thin place where Hest vanished.

### Outcome
Plants the seed for Hest's survival. The player now knows someone is alive in or near the Fray who can physically interact with Threadlines. Farweft's personal quest advances — she becomes driven to follow the tracks (but the main quest pulls the party to Loomhearth first).

## 4. Guild Dues

**Location:** Loomhearth — Guild Quarter
**Trigger:** Exploring Loomhearth after arrival
**Tags:** social, faction, moral
**Template basis:** Faction Request + Trade Dispute

### Setup
A frontier village representative named **Petton Hayward** is at the Guild Hall, arguing with a clerk. His village (Bracken Fell, three days north) can't afford this season's ward maintenance tithe. The Guild says no tithe, no Wardweaver dispatch for their Hollowing ceremony. Petton is desperate.

### Stages
1. **The Situation** — Petton's village had a bad harvest. They can pay half the tithe. The Guild wants full payment.
2. **Investigation:**
   - Talk to Petton (Insight DC 10: genuine, not lying about the hardship)
   - Talk to the Guild clerk (Insight DC 13: the clerk is sympathetic but following policy)
   - Talk to Elm Bindfast (she'll share — quietly — that Bracken Fell is in the Thinning zone, and skipping the ceremony there is more dangerous than the Guild admits)
   - Talk to Knotmother Sable (Persuasion DC 17 to get a meeting; she argues that exceptions undermine the system)
3. **Resolution options:**
   - **Pay the difference** — Player covers the shortfall. Petton's village gets a Wardweaver. Guild is satisfied. Player poorer.
   - **Negotiate** — Persuasion DC 15 with the clerk, DC 17 with Sable. Half-tithe accepted, but Bracken Fell goes to the bottom of the dispatch list.
   - **Go around the Guild** — Ask the Threadwalkers to check on Bracken Fell during patrol. Longstitch agrees (he's concerned about frontier wards), but this creates faction tension.
   - **Expose the system** — Use Elm's data to publicly argue that the tithe system is failing frontier villages. High-risk: Persuasion DC 18 public speech. Success shifts opinion; failure makes the player a Guild enemy.
   - **Do nothing** — Bracken Fell's Hollowing ceremony fails. Ward weakens. Fray breach two months later.

### Reward
- Faction reputation shifts (Guild up/down, Threadwalker up/down depending on approach)
- Elm Bindfast becomes a contact (if player engaged with the data angle)
- Lore: reveals the Guild's structural failure — they're maintaining the appearance of competence while the frontier crumbles

## 5. The Pattern Thief

**Location:** Loomhearth — the Stacks (Rememberers' Quarter)
**Trigger:** Exploring the Archive of Threads
**Tags:** mystery, faction, social
**Template basis:** Rivalry + Mystery hybrid

### Setup
Verse (Rememberer leader) asks the player for help: someone is stealing texts from the restricted section. Not valuable ones — minor Threadwalker patrol logs from decades ago. Who would steal boring patrol data?

### Investigation
1. **The stolen texts** — All from the same 20-year period, all from the same frontier region (the area around Hollowreach, before it fell)
2. **Who had access** — Three people: Verse herself, an elderly Rememberer copyist named **Sedge**, and a visiting Wardweaver Guild auditor
3. **Clues:**
   - The thefts align with the auditor's visit schedule (Investigation DC 13)
   - The auditor is **Elm Bindfast** (if the player met her in Guild Dues, this lands differently)
   - Elm isn't stealing to destroy — she's copying the patrol logs to cross-reference with her Thinning acceleration data. The logs prove the Thinning started decades earlier than the Guild admits.
4. **Confrontation** — Elm admits it. She needed the data and couldn't get it through official channels. She'll return the originals.

### Resolution options
- **Cover for Elm** — She keeps her copies. Verse gets the originals back. Elm's secret research continues.
- **Report to Verse** — Verse is furious at the breach but fascinated by Elm's findings. The two begin a covert data-sharing arrangement.
- **Report to Sable** — Elm is disciplined. Her research is confiscated. The data is buried. Player gains Guild standing, loses Elm as an ally.
- **Publish the findings** — Force Elm's data into the open. Political explosion. All factions react. Accelerates Act II's political tension.

### Reward
- Advances understanding of the Thinning's timeline
- Opens or closes the Elm Bindfast alliance
- Seeds the political dynamics that drive Act IV's path choices

## 6. Wren's Request

**Location:** Hollowreach (during the main quest passage through)
**Trigger:** After negotiating passage through Hollowreach
**Tags:** social, craft, faction
**Template basis:** Crafting Commission + Faction Request

### Setup
Wren Looseweft asks the player for a favor. She's been experimenting with Thread-Iron — her invention that physically stabilizes Fray zones — but she needs materials she can't get inside the Frayed zone: **uncontaminated iron ore** and **fresh wardthread** (thread used in active ward-circles).

### Stages
1. **The Ask** — Wren explains Thread-Iron: ordinary iron, smelted with wardthread woven into the crystal structure. It physically interacts with the weave. She's running out of wardthread because nobody trades with the Hollow Court.
2. **Sourcing:**
   - Iron ore: Available from any mine or traded in Loomhearth (easy)
   - Wardthread: Must come from an active ward-circle. This means either buying from the Guild (they won't sell to anyone connected to the Hollow Court) or taking thread from a ward (which weakens it)
3. **The Dilemma** — Getting wardthread ethically requires convincing a Wardweaver to donate/sell knowing it goes to the Hollow Court. Getting it unethically means stealing from a ward-circle. Elm Bindfast or Longstitch might help — but it's a risk for them.

### Resolution options
- **Legitimate supply** — Convince a Wardweaver to trade. Persuasion DC 16. Wren gets materials. Slow trust-building between Court and Guild.
- **Stolen wardthread** — Stealth/Deception to take from a ward. Wren gets materials, but the source ward weakens. If discovered, serious consequences.
- **Substitute material** — Nature/Arcana DC 17 to find a Fray-adjacent alternative. Less effective but avoids the ethical problem. Thread-Iron quality is lower.
- **Broker a deal** — Persuasion DC 18 to arrange an ongoing supply chain between the Guild (wardthread) and the Court (Thread-Iron prototypes). The hardest outcome but the most consequential — first formal trade between factions.

### Reward
- Thread-Iron prototype item (functions as a minor Fray-protection talisman)
- Wren's personal quest ("The Forge in the Fray") advances
- Faction reputation: Hollow Court up, Guild variable depending on approach
- Seeds the Release path — Thread-Iron is essential for the Ward Engine concept in Act IV
