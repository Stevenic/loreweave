# Act III Side Quests — The Deep Weave (Levels 9-12)

*The journey into the wilderness. These quests reveal the world's deeper truths and test what the player has learned.*

---

## 1. The Frequency Map

**Location:** Threadfall Expanse — Wynn's Camp
**Trigger:** Meeting Wynn Hollowscript
**Tags:** exploration, lore, companion
**Template basis:** None (unique)

### Setup
Wynn needs data. She's mapped Threadline vibration patterns for twenty years, but her framework is incomplete — she needs readings from **three distant Threadline nodes** to calibrate her translation matrix. She's too valuable to risk and too fragile for the journey. She asks the player.

### Stages
1. **Wynn's briefing** — She gives the player a device: a tuning fork made of Loom Stone fragment. Hold it near a Threadline node, let it resonate, bring it back. She needs readings from three nodes, each in a different biome.
2. **Node 1: The River Confluence** — Where two rivers merge. The Threadlines here run through water. Accessing the node requires diving (Athletics DC 14) or diverting flow (group skill challenge). The tuning fork vibrates differently underwater — the reading is the longest.
3. **Node 2: The Lightning Crown** — A rocky peak where Threadlines pass through high altitude. Climbing the peak (Athletics DC 15, 3 checks) plus exposure to high-altitude static discharges. The Threadlines here are thin and fast.
4. **Node 3: The Root Vein** — Inside the Knotwood, where a Threadline runs through an ancient root system underground. Requires navigating the Knotwood to find the root chamber (Survival DC 14 in the Knotwood) and persuading the forest to reveal the access point (Nature DC 13 or singing).

### Resolution
- All 3 readings: Wynn completes her translation framework. She can now partially decode Weaver's Marks and Threadline messages. This is **required** for the Weaver's Mark scene in the Knotwood.
- 2 readings: Partial framework. Wynn can decode fragments but with gaps. The Weaver's Mark translation is incomplete — the message is tantalizing but ambiguous.
- 1 reading: Wynn's framework barely advances. She's frustrated. The Weaver's Mark remains unreadable.

### Reward
- XP from traversal challenges
- Wynn's linguistic framework (narrative unlockable — enables future Weaver's Mark translations)
- If Wynn joins as companion: this quest is her trust threshold

## 2. The Walker's Silence

**Location:** Between the Expanse and the Knotwood
**Trigger:** Farweft companion quest, triggered by finding a sealed thin place
**Tags:** companion, exploration, combat, emotional
**Template basis:** None (unique companion quest)

### Setup
Farweft has been tracking Hest's trail since the Cairn Offerings discovery in Act II. Between the Expanse and the Knotwood, she finds what she's looking for: a **sealed thin place** — a pocket of Frayed reality, closed off from the world, with something living inside.

### Stages
1. **The Seal** — The thin place is sealed by old Threadwalker wardwork. Farweft recognizes the technique — it's Mending Path, her former order. Someone sealed Hest inside deliberately.
2. **Opening the Seal** — Arcana DC 16 or group skill challenge (4 successes, 2 failures). The seal fights back — it was designed to be permanent.
3. **Inside** — A pocket of Frayed reality the size of a large room. Threadlines visible everywhere. The air shimmers. And **Hest** is there — alive, changed, but himself. He's been inside for two years.
4. **Hest's story** — His patrol found the flaw — a place where the Threadlines were being actively pulled toward the Weavers' Cradle. When he reported it, someone in the Mending Path leadership sealed the thin place with him inside, rather than let the information spread. Someone in the old guard wanted the status quo preserved.
5. **The revelation** — Hest survived because he learned to **weave raw Fray material** into temporary forms. The sealed pocket became his workshop. He's discovered that Fray isn't the opposite of weave — it's unstructured weave. It can be shaped.

### Resolution
- **Rescue Hest** — He's free. Farweft has her partner back. She's also furious at the Mending Path. Her loyalty shifts fully to the player's cause.
- **What Hest knows** — His Fray-weaving ability + Wren's Thread-Iron = a combination nobody has imagined. Raw Fray, structured by mortal craft, stabilized by Thread-Iron. This is the seed for Act IV's Release path technology.
- **The betrayer** — Someone in the Mending Path leadership made this call. Farweft wants answers. This can be pursued in Loomhearth or deferred to Act IV.

### Reward
- Hest joins as an NPC ally (not a full companion, but available for specific quests)
- Farweft's personal quest completes — she reconciles her two identities (Mending Path heritage + New Stitch independence)
- Fray-weaving introduced as a narrative concept — critical for Act IV
- Emotional payoff: two years of grief resolved

## 3. Glasseye's Map

**Location:** Hollowreach — the Deep Edge
**Trigger:** Returning to Hollowreach with Act III knowledge, or if Glasseye is encountered in the Knotwood border
**Tags:** exploration, mystery, lore
**Template basis:** Ruin Exploration variant

### Setup
Glasseye, the Hollow Court's furthest-ranging salvager, has something to show the player. He's been mapping the Deep Fray — the uncontrolled zone beyond Hollowreach's Thread-Iron boundary. He's found **architecture**.

### Stages
1. **The Briefing** — Glasseye shows his maps: crude but detailed, drawn from memory of places no sane person visits. Deep in the Fray, where reality breaks down, he's found structures. Not ruins — structures that were never built. They formed from the Fray itself.
2. **The Expedition** — Glasseye guides the player into the Deep Edge. Constitution saves for Fray exposure (DC 13 per hour). 3-4 hours of increasingly distorted terrain.
3. **The Architecture** — They find it: a building that doesn't exist. Walls of solidified Fray energy. Doorways that lead to rooms that shouldn't fit inside the footprint. Windows that look out on a different sky. The structure is **a Loom Stone, inside out** — the same weave-patterns, but reversed, as if something tried to build a Loom Stone from the wrong direction.
4. **The Interpretation** — Arcana DC 16 or Wynn's framework: this is what happens when Weaver consciousness fragments completely in an area. The Fray doesn't dissolve into chaos — it crystallizes into imperfect structures. The Weavers, even fragmented, can't stop building.

### Resolution
- **Documentation** — Glasseye and the player map the structure. This data is valuable to all factions:
  - Rememberers: evidence the Weavers persist even in total fragmentation
  - Wardweavers: the structure could potentially be harnessed (proto-Ward Engine concept)
  - Threadwalkers: proof that the Fray has order, not chaos — changes their entire doctrine
- **The risk** — Extended Fray exposure. Player likely gains 1-2 levels of Fray exposure. Glasseye is unaffected (already fully adapted).

### Reward
- Lore critical to all three Act IV paths
- Glasseye as a trust-bonded NPC (essential for the Restoration path where he serves as guide)
- Fray exposure (mechanical cost) — demonstrates that knowledge in Threadhallow always has a price
- Seeds the understanding that the Fray isn't destruction — it's the Weavers' unconscious output

## 4. The Forest's Question

**Location:** The Knotwood — the Listening Glade
**Trigger:** Resting in the Knotwood after finding the Weaver's Mark
**Tags:** mystery, lore, social (with a forest)
**Template basis:** None (unique)

### Setup
The player sleeps in the Listening Glade. They dream — but the dream is structured. The Knotwood is asking a question.

### The Dream
The dream is a series of sensations (the DM engine presents these as narrative, not dialogue):

1. **A tree being planted** — hands in earth, the feeling of roots taking hold. The tree grows impossibly fast. Branches reach for other trees. They intertwine. A forest forms from a single act of planting.
2. **A fire** — not destructive, but controlled. A clearing burned to let new growth. The forest lets part of itself die so the rest can live. Pain accepted, not fought.
3. **A question** — the sensation of a fork in a root. Two paths. One goes deep (stability, permanence, slow change). One goes wide (connection, vulnerability, fast growth). The tree can't do both. Which way does the root grow?

### Resolution
- **Deep** — The player values stability and preservation. The Knotwood responds: paths become more reliable, the forest becomes protective. Mechanically: Knotwood navigation DCs drop to 8 for the rest of the visit. Narratively: the forest considers the player a kindred spirit. This aligns with the **Restoration** path.
- **Wide** — The player values connection and risk. The Knotwood responds: the forest opens new areas — paths to places it doesn't normally show visitors. Mechanically: access to a hidden grove with rare resources. Narratively: the forest considers the player bold. This aligns with the **Harmony** path.
- **Refuse the binary** — The player rejects the question's framing. The Knotwood is... surprised? A third sensation: a root that splits, goes both deep AND wide, but thinner in both directions. The forest considers this interesting. No mechanical benefit, but the Weaver's Mark glows brighter during the translation scene. This aligns with the **Release** path (mortals finding their own answer).

### Reward
- No XP — this is a narrative/alignment moment
- Foreshadows Act IV path choices
- Establishes the Knotwood as a conscious entity the player has a relationship with
- The forest remembers the player's answer — it affects Act V if the player returns

## 5. The Last Rememberer

**Location:** Between the Knotwood and the Weavers' Cradle
**Trigger:** Approaching the Cradle for the first time
**Tags:** social, lore, emotional
**Template basis:** The Stranger + Request From Beyond

### Setup
On the ridge overlooking the Weavers' Cradle, the player encounters an old woman — impossibly old — sitting on a stone, looking down at the valley. Her name is **Aleth**. She's a Rememberer. She's been here for sixty years.

### The Conversation
Aleth is the last person who tried to reach the Cradle and stayed. She came as a young scholar. The Cradle's weave density keeps her alive far beyond a natural lifespan, but she's paying for it — her memories are fragmenting. She can't remember her own surname. She remembers the Cradle.

She shares what she knows:
- The First Loom Stone is warm because "someone is still holding it from the other side"
- The Keystones pulse in the order the Weavers wove themselves in — the sequence is a record of who went first and who went last
- The last Weaver to dissolve left a mark that's different from the others — not instructions, but a name. She can't remember the name. She wrote it down sixty years ago. She can't remember where she put the note.

### Resolution
- **Find the note** — Investigation DC 14 in Aleth's campsite (a cave on the ridge). It reads: "Threadhollow." The last Weaver's name. The same name as Liss Threadhollow — the murdered Reedmere Wardweaver. The Ashwick family. The naming convention isn't coincidence. Some mortal families carry echoes of the Weavers who dissolved in their region.
- **Talk to Aleth about leaving** — She can't. She's been in the Cradle's weave too long. Leaving would kill her. She's accepted this. She asks only that someone remember her name: **Aleth Greenhand**. Greenhand. Sorrel's surname. Another echo.
- **Promise to remember** — The player adds Aleth's name to the Offering Ground at the Cradle. The stone hums when they do.

### Reward
- Deep lore: the Weavers' dissolution wasn't total erasure — echoes of them persist in mortal bloodlines
- Emotional: Aleth is what the Weavers' fading looks like at human scale
- Connection: Sorrel Greenhand is connected to a Weaver, whether she knows it or not. This becomes important if she's a companion during Act V.
- The name "Threadhollow" links to the world's name (Threadhallow) — not a coincidence. The world is named after the last Weaver.
