# Act IV-V Side Quests — The Three Paths & The Binding (Levels 13-20)

*Late-game quests that deepen the chosen path, test alliances, and resolve companion arcs. These quests are path-dependent — some only appear based on the player's Act IV choice.*

---

## Act IV Side Quests (Levels 13-16)

### 1. The Seventh Verse

**Location:** Various — continental travel
**Trigger:** Act IV begins (any path)
**Tags:** lore, exploration, social
**Available on:** All paths

### Setup
The Binding Songs have five known verses. The Mechanics Companion's skill challenge uses them. But Aleth mentioned a sixth, and the Weaver's Mark in the Knotwood hinted at a seventh — the verse the Weavers themselves sang, in their own language.

### Stages
1. **The Clue** — Cross-referencing Wynn's linguistic data with the Archive's Binding Song collection reveals gaps — places where the Song's structure expects a verse that doesn't exist in any mortal record.
2. **The Search** — Three fragments of the seventh verse are scattered:
   - **Fragment 1:** Woven into the Knotwood's Weaver's Mark (re-visit with Wynn's completed framework; Arcana DC 17)
   - **Fragment 2:** Encoded in the pulse sequence of the Seven Keystones at the Cradle (requires 4 hours of observation + Investigation DC 16)
   - **Fragment 3:** In Aldith Ashwick's woven memorial tablet in Thornwick (the player tried to access this in Act I for the Fifth Verse — now they understand what they were really looking at)
3. **Assembly** — Wynn or the player pieces the fragments together. The seventh verse isn't a song — it's a **greeting**. The Weavers' way of saying "I see you, I know you, we are in this together."

### Resolution
- **Complete seventh verse** — Changes the nature of the Binding Song from ritual to conversation. Essential for the Harmony path's finale. Helpful for all paths — any Act V ceremony that includes the seventh verse is more effective.
- **Partial** — The greeting is incomplete. The Weavers can be addressed but can't fully respond.

### Reward
- The most important piece of lore in the game — the Weavers left instructions for communication, not just maintenance
- Aldith Ashwick connection confirmed: she knew the seventh verse because she was a Weaver echo
- Sorrel (if companion) can learn to sing it — her Greenhand bloodline resonates

### 2. The Ward Engine Prototype

**Location:** Hollowreach + one Keystone site
**Trigger:** Choosing the Release path OR exploring the concept regardless of path
**Tags:** craft, faction, exploration
**Available on:** Release path (primary), others (secondary)

### Setup
Wren's Thread-Iron + Hest's Fray-weaving + Elm Bindfast's ward data = the theoretical basis for a Ward Engine. But theory isn't proof. Wren proposes building a prototype at the nearest Keystone.

### Stages
1. **Design** — Wren, Hest, and Elm collaborate. The player mediates (these three have never worked together). Group skill challenge: 4 successes / 2 failures using Persuasion, Arcana, tool proficiency, Insight.
2. **Materials** — Thread-Iron (Wren provides), raw Fray material (Hest provides), wardthread (Elm diverts from Guild stores — risky), and a Loom Stone fragment (must be chipped from a Keystone — sacrilege to Threadwalkers).
3. **Assembly** — At the nearest Keystone. The prototype is crude: a Thread-Iron frame around a Loom Stone fragment, with wardthread circuits and Fray-material insulation. Building it is a skill challenge: 6 successes / 3 failures, DCs 14-17.
4. **Activation** — It works. Barely. The Ward Engine produces a stable ward-field the size of a small room. Wren cries. Hest stares. Elm starts calculating how to scale it.

### Resolution
- **Prototype succeeds** — Proof of concept for the Release path. The Ward Engine can be scaled to Keystone-sized installations. The Release path becomes mechanically viable.
- **Prototype fails** — Something's missing. The engine flickers and dies. Need more data from the Weavers directly — which pushes the player toward Harmony or Restoration instead.
- **Faction reactions** — Threadwalkers: horrified at the Keystone damage. Wardweavers: terrified the Court built something that works. Rememberers: fascinated. Common Folk: cautiously hopeful.

### Reward
- Ward Engine prototype (quest item, symbolic)
- Cross-faction alliance (Wren + Hest + Elm) — the first collaboration between Court, Guild, and independent researcher
- Release path advancement
- Seeds the Act V coordination challenge

### 3. The Mending Path Betrayal

**Location:** Loomhearth + Threadwalker territory
**Trigger:** Hest's rescue in Act III revealed someone in the Mending Path sealed him deliberately
**Tags:** social, mystery, faction
**Available on:** All paths (Farweft companion quest resolution)

### Setup
Farweft wants answers. Who sealed Hest? Why? The trail leads to the Mending Path's inner council.

### Stages
1. **The Investigation** — The seal was old Mending Path technique. Only a senior Walker could have done it. Three candidates:
   - **Pathmaster Longstitch** — But he transferred to the regional office before Hest disappeared. Alibi holds.
   - **Trail-Keeper Dusk** — Retired Mending Path elder, lives outside Loomhearth. Declined to be interviewed.
   - **The Cairn-Keeper** — Anonymous figure who maintains the Singing Cairn. Nobody's seen them in months.
2. **Trail-Keeper Dusk** — An old woman. Evasive. Insight DC 15: she knows something. Persuasion DC 16 (or Farweft's direct appeal): she reveals the Cairn-Keeper ordered it. The Cairn-Keeper is **the oldest living Threadwalker** — they've been maintaining the Cairn for forty years. They sealed Hest because his discovery threatened everything the Walkers believe about the Fray being the enemy.
3. **The Cairn-Keeper** — Found at the Singing Cairn, tending the offerings. Ancient, frail, unrepentant. They sealed Hest because "the truth about the Fray would destroy the Orders. We exist to fight it. If the Fray is just the Weavers' unconscious output, what are we fighting?"

### Resolution
- **Compassion** — The Cairn-Keeper is a person who sacrificed someone else to preserve meaning. Farweft confronts them but doesn't destroy them. The Cairn-Keeper retires. The Mending Path is shaken but survives.
- **Justice** — Farweft demands the Orders judge the Cairn-Keeper. Public trial. The truth about Hest comes out. The Mending Path splits — some support the Keeper's reasoning, some are horrified.
- **Silence** — Farweft and the player decide the Orders can't handle this right now. The Cairn-Keeper stays. The betrayal is known but not prosecuted. Pragmatic but corrosive.

### Reward
- Farweft's arc fully resolved — she's either reconciled with her heritage or broken from it
- Threadwalker Order politics reshuffled
- The philosophical question matters: if your purpose is built on a misunderstanding, is the purpose still valid?

### 4. The Continental Summit

**Location:** Loomhearth (or wherever the player has built the strongest base)
**Trigger:** Mid-Act IV, after the player has committed to a path
**Tags:** social, faction, politics
**Available on:** All paths

### Setup
Grand Knotmother Aelith arrives from the capital with a delegation. She's been corresponding with Thinwalker in secret for five years. She knows the truth is coming out. She's here to manage the fallout — and to decide, on behalf of the continental Guild, which path to support.

### Stages
1. **The Arrival** — Aelith brings authority, resources, and political weight. She also brings the capital's perspective: the Thinning is a continental crisis, not a regional one. Every faction has a continental counterpart watching.
2. **The Summit** — The player organizes a gathering of all faction leaders:
   - Knotmother Sable (regional Guild)
   - Pathmaster Longstitch (Threadwalkers)
   - Verse (Rememberers)
   - Wren Looseweft (Hollow Court)
   - Bryn Oakheart (Common Folk, arrived from Thornwick)
   - Grand Knotmother Aelith (continental Guild)
   - Thinwalker (Frayborn)
3. **Negotiations** — The player presents their chosen path and argues for continental support. Each faction leader responds based on their values, the player's reputation, and the evidence presented. This is the biggest social encounter in the game.
4. **The Vote** — Not literal — more like consensus or fracture. The player needs at least 4 of 7 faction leaders to commit.

### Resolution
- **Consensus** — All factions align behind the chosen path. Act V proceeds with full support. Easier finale.
- **Majority** — 4-5 support, 2-3 oppose. Act V proceeds but with complications — opposing factions actively or passively resist.
- **Split** — 3-4 support, 3-4 oppose. The player must choose between forcing the issue (making enemies) or compromising the path.
- **Rook Ashwick's moment** — Bryn's nephew, representing Common Folk, delivers the speech that breaks a deadlock (if one exists). The farmer's kid says what the faction leaders won't: "We don't care which path you pick. We care that you pick one. Do something."

### Reward
- Political alignment for Act V — determines difficulty and available resources
- Every faction relationship tested simultaneously
- Rook Ashwick established as a voice that matters

## Act V Side Quests (Levels 17-20)

### 5. The Keystone March

**Location:** Seven Keystone sites across the continent
**Trigger:** Act V begins
**Tags:** exploration, combat, logistics
**Available on:** All paths

### Setup
All three paths require action at multiple Keystones. The player can only be at one site personally — allies handle the rest. This quest is about preparation: visiting each site, assessing conditions, assigning the right ally to each one.

### Stages
1. **Reconnaissance** — Visit 2-3 Keystones personally. Each has unique terrain, local threats, and a population that may help or hinder.
2. **Ally Assignment** — Match allies to Keystones based on their strengths:
   - Farweft: best in wilderness Keystones
   - Wren: best where Thread-Iron is needed
   - Wynn: best where Weaver communication is required
   - Glasseye: best in Fray-adjacent Keystones
   - Longstitch: best where Threadwalker expertise helps
   - Elm: best where Guild resources matter
3. **Preparation** — Each Keystone needs something before the finale:
   - Cleared of Fray creatures (combat at each site, handled by assigned ally)
   - Ward circle repaired or Ward Engine installed (path-dependent)
   - Communication array established (Harmony path) or reverse-Binding prepared (Restoration) or Engine powered (Release)

### Resolution
- **All Keystones prepared** — Act V finale proceeds at full power. Every ally reports ready.
- **Some Keystones underprepared** — The finale is harder. Gaps in the network create vulnerabilities the Fray exploits.
- **Ally failure** — If an ally is mismatched to their Keystone, they report problems. The player must triage: go help personally (losing preparation time at other sites) or trust them to improvise.

### Reward
- Setup for the Act V finale — this quest IS the preparation
- Tests every alliance the player has built
- The continental scale of the crisis becomes personal — the player has walked these sites, assigned these people, built this network

### 6. Sorrel's Song

**Location:** Thornwick → the Weavers' Cradle
**Trigger:** Act V, Sorrel is a companion, the seventh verse has been found
**Tags:** companion, lore, emotional
**Available on:** All paths (Sorrel companion quest finale)

### Setup
Sorrel has been hearing something in the Threadlines since Act I — a vibration that only she perceives. With the seventh verse discovered, she understands: she's hearing the Weavers trying to respond. Her Greenhand bloodline carries a Weaver echo. She can compose a new verse — the eighth. A mortal response to the Weaver greeting.

### Stages
1. **Composition** — Sorrel works on the verse. It's not easy. She's a trained Wardweaver, not a linguist. She needs the player's support: encouragement (Persuasion), structure (Arcana), and honesty (Insight — she asks "is this right?" and the player must genuinely assess).
2. **Testing** — She sings the verse at the Thornwick ward-circle. The ward flares brighter than it has in years. The standing stones hum. Bryn, watching from the Green, weeps. Something answered.
3. **The Realization** — The eighth verse isn't just a response. It's a proof of concept. Mortals can compose Binding Song verses. The Songs aren't a closed canon — they're a living conversation. This changes everything for every path.

### Resolution
- **Verse composed** — Sorrel has created the first new Binding Song verse in 1,200 years. Essential for Harmony finale. Powerful amplifier for Restoration and Release finales.
- **Sorrel transformed** — She's no longer just a Wardweaver. She's the first **Speaker** — the archetype that the Harmony path wants to create an order of.
- **Thornwick's pride** — The village that was one ceremony away from disaster in Act I has produced the most important magical innovation in twelve centuries.

### Reward
- Sorrel's personal quest complete
- New Binding Song verse (mechanical: +2 successes in any Binding Song skill challenge)
- The emotional arc from Act I lands: the scared apprentice who couldn't face her dead mentor has become someone who can talk to the Weavers
- Seeds epilogue content: if Harmony wins, Sorrel founds the Speaker order
