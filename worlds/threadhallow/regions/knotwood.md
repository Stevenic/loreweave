# The Knotwood — The Living Forest

*An ancient forest where the trees have forgotten how to be separate.*

---

## Overview

The **Knotwood** is the oldest forest in the world. Not old like Greenmantle Forest (centuries). Old like the weave itself (millennia). The trees here grew when the Weavers were still coherent, and the weave saturated them so thoroughly that they became part of the Tapestry's structure. Roots and branches are indistinguishable. Trees grow into each other, through each other, around each other. The canopy is so dense that ground level exists in permanent twilight.

The forest is alive in a way that goes beyond biology. It **responds** to the presence of conscious beings. Paths open when you walk with purpose and close behind you. Clearings appear where you need to rest. The canopy shifts to reveal or conceal the sky. It's not hostile — it's *attentive*.

**Biome:** Deep forest — ancient trees, massive root systems, dense canopy, thick undergrowth, minimal clearings. Temperature is cooler than surrounding terrain. Moisture is high. Sound doesn't carry far.

## Geography

### The Forest

| Location | Description | Threat Level |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| **The Border** | Where the Threadfall Expanse meets the Knotwood. The transition is a wall — open grassland ends and solid canopy begins within ten paces. | None (striking) |
| **The Root Roads** | Elevated root systems wide enough to walk on. The Knotwood's "paths" — they're more reliable than the ground, which is tangled undergrowth. | Low (footing) |
| **The Woven Groves** | Clusters where trees have grown into each other so completely they form living architecture — arches, halls, spiral staircases. These feel intentional but are purely organic. | None (awe) |
| **The Weaver's Mark** | A living tree the size of a cathedral, with Weaver's Marks grown into the bark over a thousand years. The inscription is visible but enormous — you walk the perimeter to read it. | None |
| **The Listening Glade** | A circular clearing that appears when you need it. The canopy opens to show the sky. The ground is soft moss. The forest watches. If you speak here, the trees rustle in response — not with wind, but with something like attention. | None (unsettling) |
| **The Heart-Knot** | The deepest point, where the oldest trees converge. The roots here descend into bedrock. The weave is so thick the air shimmers. Wynn says the Threadlines here aren't aerial — they run through the wood itself. | Low (disorientation) |

### Navigation

The Knotwood cannot be mapped conventionally. Paths change. Distances shift. The forest moves — not quickly, not visibly, but a clearing you camped in yesterday isn't there today.

**Navigation rules:**
- Survival checks to maintain direction (DC 14, disadvantage without a guide)
- Nature checks to read the forest's intent (DC 12 — the forest helps those who pay attention)
- The forest is hostile to people who damage it (cut trees, start fires) — paths close, undergrowth thickens, insects swarm
- The forest is neutral-to-friendly toward those who walk respectfully — paths open, fruit appears on branches, water is always nearby

## Atmosphere

The Knotwood feels like **being inside a living thing that's aware of you and hasn't decided what you are yet**. It's not threatening — it's the opposite of threatening. It's *interested*. Every surface is alive. Every sound has a source you can't quite see. The air is thick, warm, humid, and carries the smell of growing things at impossible density.

**What you see:** Green on green on green. Layers of canopy. Root systems taller than houses. Mushrooms the size of tables. Bioluminescent moss on north-facing bark. Shafts of light that move like searchlights as the canopy shifts. Insects that glow. Flowers that open when you look at them.

**What you hear:** Creaking wood — constant, slow, like a ship. Dripping water. Birdsong that harmonizes with itself (the weave synchronizes even the animals). Your own breathing, amplified by the close canopy. In the Woven Groves, a deep hum — the trees vibrating at the frequency of the Threadlines running through them.

**What you smell:** Earth, moss, sap, decay, growth — all at once. The Knotwood smells like a compost heap and a flower garden simultaneously. Near the Weaver's Mark, a warm-stone scent identical to Loom Stones.

**What you feel:** Watched. Not by eyes — by attention. The air is thick enough to feel on exposed skin. The ground is soft and gives slightly, like breathing. Temperature is constant: warm, humid, regardless of exterior season.

## The Weaver's Mark

The most complete Weaver inscription anyone has ever found. Grown into the bark of a tree so large it takes five minutes to walk its circumference.

**The inscription** is in the Weaver language — indecipherable without Wynn's linguistic framework. With the framework (assembled from this site + the Singing Cairn marks + Threadfall Expanse data), it reads:

> *"We are the wound and the needle. We are the flaw and the mending. If you can read this, sing to the thread beneath your feet. We are listening."*

This is the Act III key revelation setup. The inscription confirms:
- The Weavers were self-aware about the flaw in the Tapestry
- They chose to become the repair mechanism
- They left instructions for mortals to communicate with them
- They are still present — distributed, fading, but listening

## The Forest's Behavior

The Knotwood is a character, not a setting. The DM engine should treat it as an NPC with these behaviors:

| Player Action | Forest Response |
|--------------|-----------------|
| Walking respectfully | Paths open, clearings appear at rest time, fruit on branches |
| Asking for direction (speaking aloud) | Subtle shifts — a shaft of light indicates the way, birdsong intensifies in one direction |
| Cutting or damaging | Paths close, undergrowth thickens, insects become aggressive, navigation DCs increase by +4 |
| Singing (especially Binding Song fragments) | The trees vibrate in response. Threadlines become visible. The Weaver's Mark glows. |
| Extended presence (3+ days) | The forest becomes familiar — navigation DCs drop to 10, clearings appear predictably |

## Seasonal Mood

| Season | The Knotwood Feels Like... |
|--------|----------------------|
| **Spring** | Explosive growth. Everything blooms simultaneously. The canopy thickens. The forest is at its most responsive. |
| **Summer** | Dense, humid, alive. The bioluminescence is brightest. Night in the Knotwood looks like a festival. |
| **Autumn** | Nothing changes. The Knotwood doesn't follow seasons inside. Leaves don't fall. But the Threadlines running through the wood pulse faster as the Hollowing approaches. |
| **Winter** | Nothing changes. The interior temperature remains warm. Snow may accumulate on the canopy but never reaches the ground. |

## Weave State

**Dominant:** Hyper-stable (like the Expanse, but expressed through biology rather than visible Threadlines)
**Fray creatures:** None. The forest's weave density is absolute. Even Looselings cannot form here.
**The significance:** The Knotwood is what the whole world would look like if the Weavers were still fully coherent. It's a living fossil — a preserved fragment of the world as it was meant to be.
