# The Singing Cairn — Threadline Convergence

*Three Threadlines meet at the top of the world. They used to harmonize. Now they scream.*

---

## Overview

The **Singing Cairn** is a hilltop monument where three major Threadlines converge — a natural waypoint for Threadwalkers and one of the oldest maintained sites outside of settlements. The cairn itself is a stack of worked stone, twenty feet tall, carved with Weaver's Marks that predate mortal writing. Offerings of thread and woven tokens hang from iron pegs driven into the stone centuries ago.

The name comes from the sound: when the Threadlines are in sync, the convergence produces a low harmonic tone — audible within a mile, felt in the chest. Threadwalkers navigate by it.

**Now it's wrong.** The three Threadlines are vibrating at different frequencies, producing a dissonance that manifests as auditory hallucinations, headaches, and a persistent feeling of wrongness. The cairn doesn't sing anymore. It keens.

**Biome:** hills — elevated, exposed, windswept. Grass, exposed rock, heather. Visibility is excellent on clear days; you can see Ashenmoor to the west, the forests to the south, and the road to Loomhearth to the north.

## Atmosphere

The Singing Cairn feels like **standing at the center of a tuning fork that's been struck wrong**. The air vibrates. Your teeth ache. Conversation is possible but requires raised voices — not because of volume, but because the dissonance makes it hard to parse words.

**What you hear:** The keening — a dissonant chord that shifts and wavers. Underneath, if you listen carefully (Perception DC 16), you can hear the three individual Threadlines: one steady, one warbling, one stuttering. Each repaired anchor stone brings its Threadline back into harmony.

**What you see:** The Threadlines are faintly visible here — shimmering lines in the air, like heat haze with direction. On clear days, you can trace them to the horizon. The Weaver's Marks on the cairn glow brighter when a Threadline is in tune.

**What you feel:** Pressure. Not physical — more like standing in a strong current. The weave is concentrated here, and the dissonance makes it turbulent. Spellcasters feel it as static. Non-magical characters feel it as unease.

---

## The Cairn Itself

Twenty feet of stacked, worked stone — each block carved with Weaver's Marks. The marks are the most complete Weaver inscription outside the Weavers' Cradle, but they're in a script nobody can read (until Wynn Hollowscript's linguistic framework in Act III).

**What the marks say** (translatable later): Instructions for maintaining the convergence point. A name — the Weaver who built this junction. And a warning: "If the song splits, follow each voice to its root. Silence is not the answer — silence is the symptom."

**The offerings:** Centuries of Threadwalker offerings hang from iron pegs: woven tokens, thread braids, small carved figures. Some are recent (within the year). Some are ancient enough to have fossilized. A Threadwalker character or NPC recognizes specific offering styles from different eras and orders.

## Weave State

**Dominant:** Thin (the convergence should be stable, but the dissonance is degrading it)
**After repair:** Stable. The cairn becomes a ward-like zone — Fray creatures avoid it, and the harmonic tone returns. Resting here provides a minor boon (Inspiration or equivalent).
**Fray creatures:** Rare near the cairn itself (the concentrated weave repels them even when dissonant), but common along the damaged Threadlines — Looselings cluster at the anchor stones.
