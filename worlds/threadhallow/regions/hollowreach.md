# Hollowreach — The Court in the Fray

*An abandoned town reclaimed by the people reality forgot.*

---

## Overview

**Hollowreach** was a prosperous market town of ~800 people until three years ago, when a Frayed zone expanded and swallowed it. The Wardweaver Guild declared it lost. The residents evacuated — most of them.

The Fray-touched stayed. They could breathe in the Fray. They could see in the distortion. They rebuilt. Now Hollowreach is the largest **Hollow Court** settlement: a town inside a Frayed zone, population ~200, all Fray-touched to varying degrees.

From outside, Hollowreach looks like a heat mirage — buildings shimmer, dimensions seem wrong, the light is perpetually golden-grey regardless of time of day. From inside, it's a town. Damaged, strange, but alive. The Fray-touched have adapted the architecture: walls are reinforced with Thread-Iron (Wren's invention), gardens grow plants that thrive on weave instability, and the old ward-circle has been repurposed as a Fray anchor that *stabilizes* the zone rather than repelling it.

**Biome:** Any surface biome (the Fray overlay is what defines it). The underlying terrain is hills and forest.

## Atmosphere

Hollowreach feels like **a home someone built in a place that shouldn't be livable, and made it work through sheer stubbornness**. It's strange — deeply, fundamentally strange — but it's also warm, lived-in, and defiant. The Fray-touched are not victims. They're pioneers.

**What you see:** Colors are slightly off — blues lean purple, reds lean orange. Shadows don't always point the right direction. The buildings look normal until you notice the edges don't quite meet, the angles are slightly non-Euclidean. Wren's Thread-Iron installations glow faintly, providing a latticework of stability.

**What you hear:** The background hum of the Fray — a persistent, low vibration below the threshold of conscious hearing. Conversations carry normally inside the anchor zone. In the Quiet Ward, you hear whispers that aren't there. Or are.

**What you smell:** Normal town smells (cooking, forge smoke, animals) overlaid with ozone. Near the Boundary, the metallic tang of raw Fray.

**What you feel:** Pressure changes. Walking from the Anchor Square into the Quiet Ward feels like descending in an elevator. Time perception shifts — an hour in the Quiet Ward feels like thirty minutes. Non-Fray-touched characters experience mild disorientation: Constitution save DC 12 per hour or gain level 1 Fray exposure.

## Key Dynamics

### The Court's Dilemma

Hollowreach exists in tension:
- They've proven the Fray-touched can live productively, but the outside world sees them as contaminated
- The Wardweaver Guild considers Hollowreach an abomination — a settlement *inside* the thing wards are meant to repel
- The Threadwalkers are fascinated but cautious — Hollowreach breaks their models
- The Rememberers want to study the Court's Fray-adaptation — the Court resents being treated as specimens

### What Wren Knows

Wren Looseweft has been inside the Fray for three years. She's discovered:
- The Fray-touched can **feel Threadlines being pulled** — the direction leads to the Weavers' Cradle
- Thread-Iron (her invention) physically stabilizes Fray zones — it's the first mortal-made material that interacts with the weave mechanically, not magically
- The Fray isn't chaos. Up close, inside it, there are patterns. She doesn't know what they mean, but they're there.

### Glasseye's Perspective

**Glasseye** (Act III NPC, but present in Hollowreach during Act II) operates from the Deep Edge. He's a salvager who goes further into the Fray than anyone else. He sees the patterns Wren mentions, but more clearly — he describes them as "architecture." He's the player's first hint that the Fray isn't destruction. It's structure without a builder.

## Seasonal Mood

| Season | Hollowreach Feels Like... |
|--------|----------------------|
| **Spring** | The Fray-gardens bloom with impossible colors. The zone contracts slightly as the weave strengthens seasonally. |
| **Summer** | Longest days mean more time in the golden-grey light. The Court hosts a "Stillweave Festival" — their own ceremony, not from the Wheel of Binding. |
| **Autumn** | The Fray expands. The Deep Edge pushes closer. Wren reinforces the Thread-Iron boundary. Tension rises. |
| **Winter** | The Fray dims. Colors mute further. The Quiet Ward becomes almost tolerable. The Court pulls inward. |

## Weave State

**Dominant:** Frayed (the entire town sits in a Frayed zone)
**Anchor Square:** Artificially stabilized — not truly Stable, but Fray effects are suppressed within Thread-Iron radius
**Quiet Ward:** Approaching Unraveled in patches
**Deep Edge:** Unraveled
**Fray creatures:** Controlled within the town (Thread-Iron repels most creatures). Looselings occasionally drift through. Thread-Eaters are actively hunted by Court patrols. Hollow Walkers prowl the Deep Edge.
