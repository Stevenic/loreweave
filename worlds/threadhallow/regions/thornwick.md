# Thornwick — Starting Region

*A forest village where the weave is thin and the Wardweaver just died.*

---

## The Village

**Thornwick** sits in a shallow valley at the edge of the **Greenmantle Forest**, where the trees are old enough to have names and the paths between settlements are walked rather than paved. It's a village of ~200 people — farmers, shepherds, woodcutters, a blacksmith, a brewer, and the usual frontier folk.

The village is built around a central **ward-circle** — a ring of standing stones (minor Loom Stones) connected by woven thread-patterns buried in the earth. The ward-circle is the village's immune system. Inside it, the weave is stable, Fray creatures can't manifest, and the worst the wilds can throw at you is bad weather and wolves.

The ward-circle has been maintained by the Ashwick family for six generations. The last Ashwick, **Elder Maren**, died three weeks before the game begins.

## The Atmosphere

Thornwick feels like **early autumn in a village that doesn't know it's in danger yet**. The harvest is in. The pub is warm. Children play on the Green. But there's an undercurrent:

- The ward-circle's glow is dimmer than the oldest residents remember
- The forest edge feels closer than it used to — not literally, but the shadows are deeper
- Maren's death has left a gap that everyone feels but no one talks about openly
- Animals from the Thinning Forest have been acting strange — a fox walked into the village at noon and sat in the ward-circle for an hour before leaving

The player should feel *welcome* in Thornwick. It's cozy, it's kind, and it's one missed ceremony away from disaster.

---

## The Ward-Circle

The ward-circle is Thornwick's most important feature. Mechanically, it's a ring of seven minor Loom Stones connected by woven thread-patterns. Narratively, it's the village's heartbeat.

**Current state:** Weakening. Maren maintained it daily — small adjustments, replaced threads, sang to it in the mornings. Without her, it's coasting on stored energy. The Binding ceremonies recharge it, but the next ceremony (the Hollowing) is the most demanding, and nobody knows the full Song.

**What the player sees:** A faint shimmer at the boundary, visible at dusk. A warmth when crossing the threshold. The Loom Stones hum at a frequency that's felt more than heard. When the ward is at full strength, flowers grow between the stones year-round. Right now, the flowers are wilting.

**What Threadwalkers see:** The weave is taut inside the circle and loose outside it. The contrast is getting sharper. The village is an island of stability in a slowly loosening sea.

## Seasonal Mood

| Season | Thornwick Feels Like... |
|--------|----------------------|
| **Spring** | Relief. The ward held through winter. New growth. Children run further into the forest than their parents like |
| **Summer** | Abundant. Market days, visiting traders, long evenings on the Green. The ward glows strongest |
| **Autumn** | Tension. Harvest is good but the forest presses in. Preparations for the Hollowing dominate conversation |
| **Winter** | Intimate. Everyone stays close. Stories by the fire. The ward-circle is the only thing between the village and the long dark |

The game begins in **early Fraywatch** (early autumn). Six weeks until the Hollowing.
