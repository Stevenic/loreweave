# The Threadfall Expanse — Where Reality Runs Thick

*A grassland where the weave is so dense you can see it. Time doesn't work the way you expect.*

---

## Overview

The **Threadfall Expanse** is a vast grassland between Loomhearth's territory and the deep wilderness. It's where the weave transitions from mortal-maintained infrastructure to raw, ancient Tapestry. The name comes from the visible Threadlines that hang in the air like permanent aurora, descending from high altitude to touch the ground in sweeping arcs.

This is not a Frayed zone — it's the opposite. The weave here is **dense**, almost over-concentrated. The grass grows in repeating spiral patterns. Time perception warps: a day of travel sometimes takes four hours, sometimes twelve, with no correlation to distance covered. The sky is permanently twilight-tinged, even at noon.

**Biome:** Plains — but otherworldly. Open grassland with no trees, minimal elevation change, enormous sky. The grass itself is unusual: taller than normal, silver-tipped, and it ripples in patterns that repeat if you watch long enough.

## Geography

### The Expanse

| Location | Description | Threat Level |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| **The Threadfall** | The visible Threadlines. Dozens of luminous threads hang in the air, some as thick as rope, some hair-fine. They sway in a wind that doesn't exist on the surface. Walking through them produces a chime. | None (beautiful, disorienting) |
| **Wynn's Camp** | A solitary research station. Canvas tent, stacked notebooks, thread-maps pinned to standing stones. Wynn Hollowscript has lived here for twenty years. | None |
| **The Spiral Fields** | Areas where the grass grows in perfect mathematical spirals. Some spirals are hundreds of yards across. Walking the spiral path takes less time than walking straight — the weave is bending distance. | Low (temporal disorientation) |
| **Time Pools** | Patches where temporal distortion concentrates. A campfire lit in a time pool takes six hours to burn in what feels like forty minutes. Useful for rest; dangerous if you don't notice you've lost a day. | Low-Moderate (time loss) |
| **The Singing Ground** | A stretch of the Expanse where the Threadlines converge close to the surface. The ground vibrates. If you press your ear to it, you hear something like a distant choir. This is where Wynn discovered the Threadlines carry a language. | None |

## Atmosphere

The Threadfall Expanse feels like **walking inside a cathedral made of light and grass**. There's a reverence to the place. The visible Threadlines produce a constant, gentle chiming when the air moves. The grass ripples in patterns that feel intentional. Everything here suggests *design* — not nature, not randomness, but something that was made, and is still working.

**What you see:** Silver-tipped grass to the horizon. Threadlines overhead, glowing soft blue-white, swaying. The sky is perpetually golden-purple, like the last ten minutes of sunset stretched across the day. At night, the Threadlines glow brighter — the Expanse is never truly dark.

**What you hear:** Chiming. Wind in the grass. The faint, subsonic vibration of the Singing Ground. Your own heartbeat seems louder here. Conversation is easy but feels oddly intimate, as if the space amplifies whispers.

**What you feel:** A gentle pressure, like being underwater at very shallow depth. Time slippage — you lose track of hours. The weave is thick enough that magic-users feel it constantly: a tingling across the skin, a sense of being *seen*.

**What you smell:** Clean grass, ozone, and something like warm stone even when there's no stone nearby. The air tastes slightly metallic.

## Wynn's Camp

The player's key encounter in the Expanse. Wynn Hollowscript has been alone here for twenty years, mapping Threadline vibration patterns. Her camp is:

- A weathered canvas tent, heavily patched
- Dozens of notebooks stacked in oilskin bundles
- A network of strings and small bells stretched between sticks — her Threadline detection apparatus
- A cleared circle of ground where she's scratched the beginnings of a translation matrix in the dirt
- Food supplied by occasional Threadwalker patrols (she forgets to eat otherwise)

**What Wynn has discovered:** The Threadline vibrations aren't random. They're patterned. They repeat. They modulate in response to events (she's documented correlations between vibration changes and distant ward failures). Her conclusion: **the Threadlines are carrying messages**. Not broadcasts — they're structured, with syntax. A language.

She can't read it. She's been working on a translation framework for years. She needs more data points — specifically, Weaver's Mark inscriptions from multiple sites to cross-reference with her vibration maps.

## Temporal Distortion

Time in the Expanse is unreliable. The DM engine should track this mechanically:

| Scenario | Perceived Time | Actual Time |
|----------|---------------|-------------|
| **Normal travel** | 8 hours | 4–12 hours (1d4+2 × 2 hours) |
| **Resting in a Time Pool** | 2 hours | 6–8 hours |
| **Crossing a Spiral Field on the path** | 30 minutes | 10 minutes |
| **Crossing a Spiral Field off the path** | 30 minutes | 3 hours |

Time distortion is not dangerous — you don't age faster or slower. It's perception only. But it means planning travel through the Expanse requires giving up control over scheduling.

## Seasonal Mood

| Season | The Expanse Feels Like... |
|--------|----------------------|
| **Spring** | The grass turns green-silver. The Threadlines pulse in slow rhythms. Wynn says they're "quieter" in spring — fewer messages. |
| **Summer** | Peak visibility. The Threadfall is at its most spectacular. Time pools are most common. |
| **Autumn** | The grass dies to gold. The Threadlines sharpen, vibrate faster. Wynn says they're "agitated." The Hollowing approaches. |
| **Winter** | The grass is dormant. The Threadlines dim but don't vanish. The Expanse feels like it's sleeping. Time distortion is minimal. |

## Weave State

**Dominant:** Hyper-stable — the weave here is denser than anywhere else except the Weavers' Cradle
**Fray creatures:** None. The weave concentration repels them utterly. This is one of the safest places in the world, if you can handle the time distortion.
**The paradox:** The Expanse is beautiful, safe, and wondrous — and it's also evidence that the weave is *concentrated*, which means it's *thinning* everywhere else. The Expanse is stable because the Threadlines are pulling weave energy away from the frontier.
