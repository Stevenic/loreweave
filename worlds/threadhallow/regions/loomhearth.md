# Loomhearth — Seat of Power

*Where the factions meet, argue, and pretend the world isn't ending.*

---

## Overview

**Loomhearth** is the largest town in the region — population ~2,000, built around the densest cluster of Loom Stones outside the Weavers' Cradle. It's a crossroads: the Wardweaver Guild's regional headquarters, the Threadwalker Order's primary waypost, and home to the Rememberers' Archive of Threads.

The town is prosperous. The Loom Stone cluster generates a ward so strong that the Thinning barely registers here — which is exactly the problem. Loomhearth's leaders can afford to deny what's happening in the frontier because their own ward makes the crisis invisible.

**Biome:** Forest and farmland. Loomhearth sits in a broad river valley with cleared farmland around it and forest on the surrounding hills.

## Atmosphere

Loomhearth feels like **a prosperous town that believes its own prosperity is proof that everything is fine**. The streets are clean. The inns are full. The Guild Hall gleams. The ward is so strong you can forget it's there — which means you can forget why you need it.

The tension is under the surface. Guild apprentices whisper about frontier reports being suppressed. Threadwalkers arrive haggard from patrol and are told to rest before their reports are "reviewed." Rememberers mutter about texts they're not allowed to show the public. The Common Folk notice the prices going up and the patrols getting longer and ask questions nobody answers.

**What you see:** A functioning, beautiful town. Stone and timber buildings in good repair. The Loom Stone cluster visible from everywhere — seven pillars of worked stone, 10-15 feet tall, glowing faintly day and night. Thread-patterns woven between them catch the light like spider silk.

**What you hear:** Normal town bustle. But near the Archive, hushed arguments. Near the Guild Hall, confident speeches that ring slightly hollow. Near the Walker's End, silence that means exhaustion.

**What you smell:** Baking bread, forge smoke, river water, horse. The Stone Circle smells faintly of ozone and warm stone — the ward's signature.

## Political Landscape

### The Three Powers

**Wardweaver Guild** (Knotmother Sable)
- Controls the ward infrastructure for the entire region
- Collects maintenance tithes from frontier settlements
- Official position: the Thinning is manageable with more resources and trained Wardweavers
- Actual position: Sable's private journal is full of data she won't publish because it would cause panic

**Threadwalker Order** (Pathmaster Longstitch)
- Patrols the frontier, monitors Threadlines, maps thin places
- Reports accelerating Thinning across the region
- Official position: the situation is dire and requires immediate expedition to the source
- Frustrated that the Guild controls the political process and can delay action

**Rememberers** (Verse)
- Maintain the Archive of Threads — the largest collection of pre-Silence and post-Silence texts
- Have found references to a previous Thinning event in fragmentary pre-Silence records
- Official position: knowledge is the path forward, and the Guild is suppressing it
- Actual position: Verse knows the Archive contains things that would shatter the Guild's authority

### The Reformist

**Elm Bindfast** — Junior Wardweaver, data collector, quiet radical. She's independently mapped the Thinning's acceleration and knows Sable's public numbers are underreported. She'll share her data with a player who earns her trust, but she won't go public without proof Sable can't dismiss.

### The Outsider View

**Thinwalker** — Frayborn faction leader, present in Loomhearth as an uncomfortable guest. The Guild tolerates the Frayborn's existence but keeps them at arm's length. Thinwalker knows things about the Fray that the institutional factions can't accept because their worldview requires the Fray to be the enemy.

## The Archive of Threads

The single most important building outside the main quest locations. A three-story stone structure packed with:

- Pre-Silence texts (fragmentary, in archaic scripts, partially translated)
- Post-Silence Wardweaver records (complete for the last ~400 years)
- Threadwalker patrol logs (last ~200 years)
- Binding Song collections (the most complete repository of known verses)
- Weaver's Mark rubbings (collected from across the continent)

**The restricted section** (Verse has the key) contains texts the Guild has classified: reports of ward failures, Thinning acceleration data, and a pre-Silence fragment that describes the Weavers "arguing about a flaw" — the key revelation of Act II.

## Seasonal Mood

| Season | Loomhearth Feels Like... |
|--------|----------------------|
| **Spring** | The Greening Knot ceremony. Optimism. Trade season opens. New apprentices arrive at the Guild. |
| **Summer** | Peak prosperity. The Loom Stone Fields host festivals. The ward glows brightest. Easy to pretend everything's fine. |
| **Autumn** | Political season. Budget negotiations for frontier wards. Threadwalker reports pile up. Tension builds toward the Hollowing. |
| **Winter** | Quiet. The ward holds easily. The town turns inward. The Archive's reading rooms are full. |

## Weave State

**Dominant:** Stable — the Loom Stone cluster maintains a powerful ward that covers the town and immediate farmlands
**Beyond the farmlands:** Thin — the ward's influence drops off sharply
**Fray creatures:** Essentially zero within the ward. Patrols encounter them within a day's walk.
**The irony:** Loomhearth is so well-protected that its leaders can't feel the crisis. The ward is a shield and a blindfold.
