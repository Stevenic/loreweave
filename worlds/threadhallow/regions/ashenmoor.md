# Ashenmoor — The Drowned Farmland

*A bog that remembers being a village. The Fray didn't invade — it seeped.*

---

## Overview

**Ashenmoor** is what happens when a ward fails slowly. Thirty years ago, this was **Reedmere** — a farming village of ~150 people on the banks of the River Loom. When bandits killed the Wardweaver, the ward-circle degraded over months. The river shifted. The ground softened. The village sank.

Now it's moorland — open, windswept, wet. The old buildings poke through the peat like bones. The ward-circle's standing stones are still visible at low tide, half-submerged, covered in moss. The weave here is **thin to frayed**, patchy and unpredictable.

**Biome:** hills (moorland variant) — NOT swamp. Ashenmoor is open heath and peat bog, not dense wetland. High visibility, low shelter, constant wind.

## Atmosphere

Ashenmoor feels like **grief that's had thirty years to become landscape**. The wind never stops. The sky is enormous — no canopy, no walls, just open grey. When the fog rolls in, which is often, the moor becomes a featureless void where distance loses meaning and the sunken buildings appear without warning.

**What you hear:** Wind through heather. Water trickling. Birds that fall silent when you approach the old ward-circle. On still nights, the standing stones hum — not the warm hum of a live ward, but something thin and intermittent, like a radio losing signal.

**What you smell:** Peat, wet earth, heather. Near the sunken village, something faintly metallic — the smell of degraded Threadwork.

**What you feel:** Cold. Damp. The ground gives slightly underfoot. Near the ward-circle, a prickling sensation — the weave is trying to hold but can't.

---

## The Wardweaver's Cottage

The primary quest location. Half-submerged but structurally sound (stone construction). The door is below the current waterline; entry requires wading.

**Interior:** A single room, knee-deep in cold, dark water. Shelves on the upper walls still hold jars, books (ruined), and thread spools (degraded). A locked chest on a stone shelf above the waterline contains the **Reedmere Journal** — the Wardweaver's observations about the Thinning, written years before anyone else noticed.

**The Journal** reveals: The Wardweaver (named **Liss Threadhollow**) documented accelerating ward decay rates, unusual Threadline behavior, and three instances of Fray creatures appearing inside supposedly stable wards. She sent reports to the Guild. They were filed and forgotten. She was killed before she could escalate.

## Seasonal Mood

| Season | Ashenmoor Feels Like... |
|--------|----------------------|
| **Spring** | Brief green. The heather blooms purple. Meltwater makes the moor impassable in places. |
| **Summer** | The most bearable season. Dry enough to walk without sinking. Long evenings, haunting light. |
| **Autumn** | Fog season. The moor vanishes. Sound carries impossibly far, then not at all. |
| **Winter** | Frozen peat, standing ice. The sunken buildings become ice sculptures. Beautiful and hostile. |

---

## Weave State

**Dominant:** Thin (80% of the moor), Frayed (near the old ward-circle)
**Stable zones:** None. The ward is dead.
**Fray creatures:** Looselings in the fog (common), Thread-Puppets in the ruins (occasional — they animate scarecrows and old tools), a single Frayed One rumored to haunt the Wardweaver's cottage (this is Liss Threadhollow's echo in the weave — not truly her, but enough to be heartbreaking).
