# The Singing Cairn — Anchor Stone Mini-Dungeons

*Three mini-dungeons, each housing a failing anchor stone that sustains one of the Singing Cairn's three Threadlines. Repairing all three restores the Cairn's harmonic convergence and is the primary dungeon content of Act II.*

*Each dungeon is 4-6 rooms, designed for a party of 2-3 (player + 1-2 companions) at levels 5-7. Estimated play time: 45-90 minutes each.*

---

## Design Philosophy

The three anchors are in different biomes and have different *types* of failure — one is physical displacement, one is elemental contamination, and one is social/territorial. This teaches the player that the weave's problems aren't one-dimensional: sometimes you dig, sometimes you drain, sometimes you talk.

Each dungeon has a **combat path** and a **skill path**. Neither is "correct." The combat path is straightforward. The skill path is faster but requires higher DCs and specific knowledge.

Repairing each anchor produces an immediate effect: the corresponding Threadline snaps back into tune, and the Cairn's dissonance lessens. After all three, the Cairn *sings* — the first time in months.

## 1. The Forest Anchor — Greenmantle Deep

**Biome:** Deep forest (dense old-growth south of the Cairn)
**Problem:** Massive root systems have physically shifted the anchor stone off its foundation. The Threadline is stretched like a guitar string tuned too tight — it vibrates painfully instead of humming.
**Mood:** Green, close, suffocating. The trees are so dense the sky disappears. Something large has been nesting here.

### Room 1: The Overgrown Path

*The Threadline enters the forest as a visible shimmer — but here it's pulled taut, angled downward, and the trees along its path lean inward as if drawn by magnetism.*

- **Navigation:** Survival DC 13 to follow the Threadline path (it cuts through trackless forest). Failure doesn't prevent progress but adds 2 hours and a random encounter roll.
- **Observation:** Perception DC 14 reveals deep claw marks on trees at 8-foot height. Something large and heavy has been using this path. Nature DC 12: bear marks, but wider than normal — something's wrong with the local wildlife.
- **Foreshadowing:** The trees along the Threadline are growing faster and stranger than surrounding forest. Trunks twist toward the line. Branches weave together overhead, forming a tunnel.

### Room 2: The Root Chamber

*A natural hollow where the forest floor has collapsed into a bowl-shaped depression. The roots of four massive oaks form a cage around a central space. The Threadline descends into this hollow, pulled down by the root mass.*

- **Environmental:** The roots are alive and slowly contracting — the depression is deeper than it was a year ago. Athletics DC 12 to descend safely (15 feet down slick root-walls).
- **Hazard:** Root tendrils grab at legs and equipment. Not hostile — reflexive, like a sleeping hand closing. Strength DC 11 to free yourself. Happens once per character on entry.
- **Discovery:** At the bottom, a massive root cluster has wrapped around the anchor stone's base and is physically pulling it sideways. The stone is tilted 30 degrees from vertical. The Threadline's tension is visible here — it's stretched thin and vibrating audibly (a high keening).

### Room 3: The Bear's Den

*A side chamber off the Root Chamber. A Fray-touched bear has made its den here — drawn by the concentrated weave energy leaking from the stressed Threadline.*

- **The Bear:** One **Fray-touched brown bear** (use bear stats with Fray-touched template: +2 AC from crystalline patches on hide, resistance to force damage, luminous eyes). It's not aggressive unless its den is approached — it's basking in the Threadline energy, which feels good to it.
- **Combat path:** Fight the bear. Straightforward CR 2 encounter.
- **Skill path:** Animal Handling DC 15 (advantage if character has druid/ranger background) to calm the bear and lead it away. Nature DC 14 reveals it's addicted to the Threadline bleed — moving it away will cause distress for a few days, then it'll normalize. If Farweft is present, she knows a Threadwalker technique for redirecting Fray-addled animals (+2 to the check).
- **Loot (den):** Scattered bones, a Threadwalker's pack (old, from someone who never returned — contains a sealed letter to the Mending Path waypost at Loomhearth and a ward-stone fragment worth 15 gp to the right buyer).

### Room 4: The Anchor Foundation

*The anchor stone itself — a six-foot obelisk of pale granite, carved with Weaver's Marks, tilted at an angle by the root mass wrapped around its base. The Threadline connects at its apex, pulled painfully taut.*

- **Assessment:** Arcana DC 13 reveals the stone isn't damaged — just displaced. The roots need to be cleared and the stone re-seated on its original foundation (a carved socket in the bedrock, visible under the root tangle).
- **The Roots:** Two approaches:
  - **Cut them** (Athletics DC 14, 30 minutes of labor, or combat-speed if using slashing weapons — treat as 3 root clusters with AC 12, 15 HP each, immune to piercing/psychic/poison). Effective but the trees above groan and the canopy shudders. The forest doesn't like this.
  - **Coax them apart** (Nature DC 16, requires speaking to the roots as one would to a living thing — druid/ranger affinity applies). Slower (1 hour) but the roots withdraw willingly. The forest settles. If the Knotwood is reached later in Act III, the player recognizes this behavior — the deep forest *listens*.
- **Re-seating:** Once roots are clear, Athletics DC 13 (with help from companions: advantage) to lever the stone back into its socket. Arcana DC 14 to verify alignment — the Weaver's Marks must face specific cardinal directions.

### Completion

The stone drops into its socket with a resonant *thunk* felt in the ribcage. The Threadline goes slack for one heartbeat — then tightens to its proper tension. The keening stops. A low, clean hum replaces it. The nearest trees straighten perceptibly over the next few minutes.

Back at the Cairn, one third of the dissonance resolves. The keening becomes a two-note discord instead of three.

## 2. The Moor Anchor — Ashenmoor's Edge

**Biome:** Hills/moorland (west of the Cairn, at Ashenmoor's eastern fringe)
**Problem:** Rising water from Ashenmoor's drowned landscape has half-submerged the anchor stone. Peat-water saturated with Fray energy is conducting contamination up the Threadline — the vibration isn't wrong because the stone moved, but because the *signal* is dirty.
**Mood:** Bleak, wet, cold. Wind never stops. The moor stretches flat and brown in every direction. You can see for miles and there's nothing to see.

### Room 1: The Bog Approach

*The Threadline runs west from the Cairn across open moorland, descending gradually toward the waterline where hills become Ashenmoor. The shimmer in the air is tinged brown — the color of peat water.*

- **Navigation:** Survival DC 14 to find safe footing across the moorland approach. The ground is deceptive — solid heather over shin-deep peat bogs. Failure: one character sinks to the knee. Athletics DC 12 to extract. No damage, but boots are ruined and speed is halved until dried.
- **Observation:** Investigation DC 13 reveals old Threadwalker route markers — iron stakes driven into the ground, most tilted or sunk. Someone maintained this path once.
- **Environmental:** The wind is constant and cold. The Threadline's contamination manifests as a low thrumming that makes your back teeth ache. Constitution save DC 11 or disadvantage on Perception checks while in this area (the thrumming is distracting).

### Room 2: The Waterline

*The moor slopes into standing water — the edge of Ashenmoor's flooded territory. The anchor stone is visible 200 feet out: a dark shape rising from knee-deep water, leaning at an angle. Brown, Fray-tainted water laps against it. The Threadline enters the water here and the shimmer becomes murky, diffuse.*

- **Assessment:** Nature DC 12: The water is peat-stained and still. It's not deep (2-3 feet at most) but the bottom is treacherous — soft peat that can swallow a foot without warning.
- **Hazard:** 4 **Looselings** drift in the water around the stone — tangles of pale thread that look like drifting pond weed until they move. They're drawn to the Fray energy bleeding from the stone. Not aggressive but territorial — they'll cluster around anyone who enters the water.
- **Combat path:** Wade in and fight. The Looselings attack anyone who approaches within 15 feet of the stone. Ankle-deep water = difficult terrain. CR 1/4 each, 4 of them.
- **Skill path:** Arcana DC 14 to generate a ward-pulse that drives the Looselings back for 10 minutes. Long enough to work. Or: create a distraction (Performance DC 13 or a thrown light source) to draw them away from the stone.

### Room 3: The Submerged Foundation

*Wading to the anchor stone reveals the problem: the stone's base is submerged in two feet of Fray-tainted water. The water is warm (wrong — the moor water is cold) and has a faint luminescence. The stone's Weaver's Marks are coated in a brown-green biofilm that pulses faintly. The Threadline connects at the stone's apex, but the contamination is visible — the shimmer above the stone is muddy, wavering.*

- **Assessment:** Arcana DC 15: The stone is fine. The foundation socket is intact. The problem is the water — peat-water has been absorbing Fray bleed from a minor breach somewhere upstream, and the saturated water is conducting that contamination into the stone and up the Threadline. Solution: drain the water around the stone and seal the surface so it can't re-contaminate.
- **Drain option 1:** Athletics DC 14 + 2 hours of labor — dig a drainage channel through the peat to lower the water level around the stone. Physically exhausting. Tools help (advantage with shovel or similar).
- **Drain option 2:** If a character has any water-manipulation ability (Threadcraft, druid magic, creative use of Prestidigitation/Shape Water): Arcana/Nature DC 13 to push the water back and hold it for 30 minutes while the cleansing work is done.
- **Cleansing:** Once the base is exposed, Arcana DC 15 to scrub the biofilm from the Weaver's Marks and perform a basic binding to seal the stone's surface against re-contamination. If Sorrel is present, she knows a folk-ward technique for waterproofing a binding — reduces the DC to 13.

### Room 4: The Upstream Source (Optional)

*Following the warm-water trail upstream (Survival DC 14) leads to a small peat pool 400 yards away where the Fray breach sits — a hairline crack in reality, invisible to the naked eye but detectable by the warmth and the faint luminescence of the water flowing from it.*

- **The Breach:** Tiny — barely a foot across. But it's been bleeding Fray energy into the water table for months.
- **Seal it:** Arcana DC 16 (or Arcana DC 13 if the player learned anything from the Scarecrow quest in Act I about minor breaches). The seal is temporary — a proper Wardweaver could make it permanent. Report it to the Guild at Loomhearth for a permanent fix.
- **Leave it:** The anchor stone repair holds, but the contamination will slowly resume. The next Threadwalker patrol will need to re-cleanse the stone. (Consequences noted but not immediate.)

### Completion

The biofilm dissolves. The Weaver's Marks glow clean blue for the first time in months. The warm water around the base cools to match the moor. The Threadline's shimmer clears — brown tinge gone, replaced by the pure heat-haze of a healthy line.

Back at the Cairn, the discord drops to a single note. Two Threadlines hum in harmony. One remains off-key.

## 3. The River Anchor — Loom Ford

**Biome:** Forest/riverside (north of the Cairn, along the road toward Loomhearth)
**Problem:** River erosion has undercut the bank where the anchor stone sits. The stone is structurally sound but teetering on a collapsing shelf of earth, inches from falling into the river. A bandit group has also camped nearby, using the stone as a territorial landmark — they don't know what it is, but they know Threadwalkers visit it, and Threadwalkers carry coin.
**Mood:** Green and moving. The river is loud. The bandits are loud. This is the most "normal" of the three dungeons — the problem is half-geological, half-social.

### Room 1: The Ford Approach

*The road to Loomhearth crosses the River Loom at a shallow ford. The anchor stone sits on a bluff above the crossing — originally a commanding position overlooking the ford, now an eroding shelf of earth above a ten-foot drop to the water.*

- **Navigation:** No check needed — the road leads here. But Perception DC 13 reveals signs of habitation before you see the camp: boot prints, a latrine trench poorly concealed, fire-smoke smell.
- **Assessment:** Investigation DC 12 from the road: 4-6 people camped near the stone. Armed. Not military — ragged, varied equipment.
- **Choice point:** The player can approach openly, scout first, or bypass the camp entirely (the anchor stone can be reached from the river bank below, but that means climbing the eroding bluff — see Room 4).

### Room 2: The Bandit Camp

*Six bandits camped in a ring of lean-to shelters around a fire pit, 50 feet from the anchor stone. Their leader — a scarred woman named **Tulla Red** — sits on a stolen Threadwalker's pack. The stone is behind them, on the bluff's edge.*

**The Bandits (6):**
- **Tulla Red** (bandit captain stats, CR 2) — Practical, not cruel. She's an ex-Guild guard who deserted after her wage was cut. She doesn't want a fight, but she won't give up the camp willingly.
- **5 bandits** (CR 1/8 each) — A mix of deserters, debtors, and one kid who ran away from an apprenticeship. Morale is low. They follow Tulla because she keeps them alive.

**Combat path:** Standard CR 2 + 5×CR 1/8 encounter. The bandits aren't suicidal — if Tulla goes down or 3 of 5 are dropped, the rest surrender.

**Social path:**
- Persuasion DC 14: Explain the anchor stone's importance. Tulla doesn't care about Threadlines, but she cares about the road. If the stone falls, the Threadline fails, the region becomes less stable, and fewer traders use the ford — fewer targets for her. She'll move camp (grudgingly) if the player guarantees the road stays safe.
- Intimidation DC 13: Easier if the player has visible Threadwalker or Guild association. Tulla knows not to fight people who matter.
- Deception DC 15: Claim to be a Guild inspector or Threadwalker patrol. Tulla is sharp — she'll spot inconsistencies (Insight +3).
- **Bribe:** 25 gp and she moves. No check needed. She's practical.
- **Farweft's approach:** If Farweft is present, she recognizes the stolen pack as belonging to a Mending Path walker. She's furious. This escalates — Tulla becomes defensive. But if the player mediates (Persuasion DC 16), Farweft settles for recovering the pack and the bandits relocate.

### Room 3: The Eroding Bluff

*The anchor stone sits at the bluff's edge. The ground beneath it has been undercut by the river — a ten-foot overhang of earth and root over moving water. Cracks run through the soil around the stone's base. A strong rain could send it into the river.*

- **Assessment:** Investigation DC 12: The erosion has been accelerating. Nature DC 13: The river didn't used to run this close — it shifted course when the Reedmere ward collapsed upstream (the same event that created Ashenmoor). The Thinning has indirect, cascading infrastructure effects.
- **Stabilization option 1:** Athletics DC 14 + 3 hours of labor: shore up the bank with timber and stone. A construction project. If the bandits were persuaded (not fought), two of them help — reduce to 2 hours and grant advantage.
- **Stabilization option 2:** If a character has earth-shaping magic or engineering tools: Arcana/Nature DC 15 to reinforce the bank permanently by binding the soil with a minor ward-pattern. Faster (30 minutes) but requires Threadcraft knowledge.
- **Emergency option:** If the bluff collapses during work (10% chance per hour of labor, or if combat occurs on the bluff): Dexterity save DC 14 to avoid going into the river with the stone. If the stone falls, Athletics DC 16 to recover it from the riverbed and re-seat it on stable ground. More difficult but not impossible.

### Room 4: The Stone's Repair

*Once the bank is stabilized (or the stone recovered), the anchor stone needs re-mounting. It's not displaced like the Forest Anchor — it's on its original foundation, but the foundation itself has shifted.*

- **Repair:** Arcana DC 13 to realign the Weaver's Marks with the Threadline. The stone has settled 4 inches lower than its original position — a wedge of flat stone under the base corrects the angle.
- **Bonus discovery:** Investigation DC 15 on the stone's base (only visible if the stone was moved or tilted during the process): A Weaver's Mark on the bottom surface — one that was never meant to be seen. It reads (with Wynn's linguistic framework, later): "The river remembers. Ask it where the threads began." A seed for Act III's journey to the Weavers' Cradle.

### Completion

The stone settles. The final Threadline snaps into tune — the sound is immediate and physical, like a string instrument finding its note. From here, you can *hear* the Cairn. Not the keening — the *song*. Three notes in harmony, clear and clean, carrying across the hills.

At the Cairn summit, the dissonance is gone. The stone hums. The offerings flutter in a breeze that wasn't there before. Farweft stands very still, eyes closed, and says quietly: "I'd forgotten what it sounds like when it works."

If the player has repaired all three: the Cairn becomes a fast-travel waypoint and rest location. Resting here grants Inspiration (or equivalent mechanical benefit). And the revelation crystallizes: someone — or something — was deliberately pulling the Threadlines apart. The dissonance wasn't natural decay. It was interference.

## Encounter Summary (for Loremaster Handoff)

| Dungeon | Combat Encounters | Skill Challenge Moments | Estimated XP Budget |
|---------|------------------|------------------------|-------------------|
| **Forest Anchor** | 1 (Fray-touched bear, CR 2) or 0 (skill bypass) | Root clearing, stone re-seating, navigation | 450-600 XP |
| **Moor Anchor** | 1 (4 Looselings, CR 1/4 each) or 0 (skill bypass) | Drainage, cleansing, optional breach seal | 200-400 XP |
| **River Anchor** | 1 (bandit captain + 5 bandits) or 0 (social bypass) | Bank stabilization, stone repair | 450-700 XP |

**Design intent:** The Forest Anchor is about patience and understanding nature. The Moor Anchor is about persistence and environmental problem-solving. The River Anchor is about people and negotiation. Together they demonstrate the range of challenges the weave's failure creates — it's not just monsters. It's infrastructure, ecology, and society.
