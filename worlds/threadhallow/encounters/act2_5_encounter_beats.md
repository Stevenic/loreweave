# Encounter Narrative Beats — Acts II through V

*Narrative beats, terrain, mood, and tactical context for each major encounter. Loremaster builds CR budgets, stat blocks, and XP rewards on top of these.*

---

## Act II: The Threadwalker's Path (Levels 5-8)

### Encounter 1: The Drowned Ward

**Location:** Ashenmoor — Reedmere ruins
**Terrain:** Knee-deep standing water over uneven stone foundations. Half-collapsed walls provide partial cover. The ward-circle stones create a ring of slightly shallower water.
**Lighting:** Overcast daylight, fog reducing visibility to 60 feet.
**Mood:** Folk horror. The scarecrows aren't just animated — they're assembled from a dead village's debris: fence posts, thatch, a child's doll. The Frayed One is tragic, not threatening, until provoked.

**Enemies:**
- 4 Thread-Puppets (assembled from village debris, shambling through shallows)
- 1 Frayed One (weave-echo of the murdered Wardweaver, Liss Threadhollow)

**Narrative beat:** The Thread-Puppets are the warm-up. The real encounter is the Frayed One — she's not attacking, she's trying to activate the ward. Over and over. The combat should feel like interrupting something sad.

**Environmental factors:**
- Difficult terrain (standing water)
- Partial cover from ruined walls
- The ward-circle stones pulse faintly — standing near them grants advantage on Arcana checks
- The Frayed One resets her loop if not engaged within 3 rounds

**Non-combat resolution:** Complete the ward activation Liss is attempting (Arcana DC 16 or Binding Song performance). This pacifies the Frayed One and briefly activates the dead ward.

---

### Encounter 2: Singing Cairn — Forest Anchor

**Location:** Dense old-growth forest south of the Cairn
**Terrain:** Massive root systems, dense undergrowth, narrow paths between ancient trees. The anchor stone is at the base of a tree the size of a building, surrounded by overgrown roots that have physically shifted it.
**Lighting:** Permanent twilight under canopy. Bioluminescent moss provides dim light.
**Mood:** Green cathedral invaded by the wrong kind of growth. The roots are choking the anchor stone like vines on a ruin.

**Enemies:**
- 2 Looselings (tangled in the root system, grasping at anything that moves)
- 1 awakened tree or treant-type guardian (optional — the forest defending what it thinks is a threat to itself; the player must convince it to let them repair the stone)

**Narrative beat:** The combat is secondary. The anchor stone is being crushed by root overgrowth. The player must clear roots (Athletics/Nature), navigate the root system (Acrobatics/Survival), and re-seat the stone (Arcana DC 14). The Looselings are obstacles, not the point.

**Environmental factors:**
- 3D terrain (root systems at multiple elevations)
- Difficult terrain everywhere
- The anchor stone hums when approached — Arcana proficiency grants insight into the repair

---

### Encounter 3: Singing Cairn — Moor Anchor

**Location:** Western edge of Ashenmoor
**Terrain:** Open peat bog. The anchor stone is half-submerged, surrounded by standing water. No cover. Flat visibility to the horizon except for fog patches.
**Lighting:** Daylight, but fog limits visibility to 120 feet. Fog patches reduce to 30 feet.
**Mood:** Exposed and isolated. The moor stretches forever. You're the tallest thing for miles. The fog moves in waves.

**Enemies:**
- 3-4 Looselings (emerging from the peat, grasping blindly)
- 1 Thread-Eater (drawn to the anchor stone's residual magic — this is the first Thread-Eater encounter for most players)

**Narrative beat:** The Thread-Eater is the escalation. Looselings are weather; Thread-Eaters are predators. This encounter should establish Thread-Eaters as a different level of threat — they're smart, they target magic, and they're terrifying near anything warded.

**Environmental factors:**
- Treacherous footing (Athletics DC 12 to move at full speed, or half speed)
- Standing water conceals depth (Investigation DC 13 to avoid sinkholes)
- The anchor stone is leaking Fray energy into the water — the Thread-Eater is feeding on it
- The fog patches move — enemies can appear and vanish between rounds

---

### Encounter 4: Singing Cairn — River Anchor

**Location:** River Loom ford, north of the Cairn
**Terrain:** Riverbank, the anchor stone on an eroding bluff. A bandit camp is set up on the far bank with a view of the ford. The stone hangs at the edge of a ten-foot drop to the river.
**Lighting:** Open daylight.
**Mood:** A human problem, not a monster one. These bandits don't know what they're camped next to. They just know it's a good ambush spot.

**Enemies:**
- 4-6 bandits + 1 bandit captain
- Optional: 1 Looseling drifting along the riverbank (the Fray leaking from the damaged anchor attracted it — bandits are ignoring it, which tells you something about how desensitized the frontier has become)

**Narrative beat:** This is the Act II encounter that can be entirely social. The bandits can be fought, bribed, intimidated, or negotiated with. The captain is an ex-Guild guard who was dismissed when ward-maintenance funding was cut. She's bitter, not evil. If the player explains what the anchor stone is, she'll let them repair it — she remembers enough to care.

**Environmental factors:**
- River crossing (Athletics DC 13 in water, DC 10 at the ford)
- Elevation advantage for bandits on far bank
- The bluff is unstable — the stone needs stabilizing before it falls into the river (Athletics/Engineering)
- If combat: the river is a tactical feature (push effects, difficult terrain)

### Encounter 5: Hollowreach Boundary Crossing

**Location:** The edge of Hollowreach's Frayed zone
**Terrain:** The Boundary — reality transitions from normal to Frayed over ~100 feet. Buildings shimmer. Colors shift. The Thread-Iron boundary posts glow faintly.
**Lighting:** The Frayed zone has its own lighting — perpetual golden-grey regardless of time of day.
**Mood:** Threshold. The party is crossing into a place that shouldn't be livable. It's not hostile — it's deeply, fundamentally strange.

**Enemies:**
- No enemies (this is an environmental encounter)
- Constitution saves for Fray exposure (DC 12) for each non-Fray-touched character
- Perception checks (DC 14) to avoid disorientation penalties

**Narrative beat:** This isn't a fight. It's an adjustment period. The 10-minute transition as senses recalibrate should feel like descending in a submarine — pressure changes, light changes, sound changes. The player's first experience inside a Frayed zone should be memorable as an experience, not as a combat.

**Environmental factors:**
- Fray exposure saves (DC 12 per 10 minutes for first hour)
- Disorientation (disadvantage on Perception for 10 minutes unless Perception DC 14 is passed)
- If Wren or a Hollow Court NPC guides the party: saves are at advantage

## Act III: The Deep Weave (Levels 9-12)

### Encounter 6: Threadfall Expanse — Time Pool Ambush

**Location:** Threadfall Expanse, a Time Pool at the edge of a Spiral Field
**Terrain:** Open grassland with silver-tipped grass. Threadlines visible overhead. A Time Pool creates a shimmering depression in the ground — time moves differently inside.
**Lighting:** The Expanse's perpetual golden-purple twilight.
**Mood:** Surreal. Combat in the Expanse should feel wrong because time works differently. Initiative order gets disrupted by temporal distortion.

**Enemies:**
- 2 Hollow Walkers (they've followed the party from outside the Expanse — this is the first time the player encounters CR 4 Fray creatures in the open)

**Narrative beat:** Hollow Walkers are the escalation signal. Their time-stuttering movement is at home in the Expanse — they use the Time Pools as tactical terrain, phasing in and out of normal time flow. This should be the encounter that makes the player realize the Thinning has sent the Fray's apex predators out of their territory.

**Environmental factors:**
- Time Pools: creatures that start their turn inside one can use a bonus action to "skip" — they vanish and reappear at the start of their next turn (effectively gaining a round of displacement)
- The Threadlines overhead chime during combat — audible damage to the weave
- The Hollow Walkers' time-stutter ability is amplified here

---

### Encounter 7: The Knotwood — The Living Maze

**Location:** Knotwood interior, the Root Roads
**Terrain:** A root system the size of a building, with paths, dead ends, and chambers formed by interlocking roots. The "maze" moves — paths close behind you, new ones open ahead.
**Lighting:** Dim (bioluminescent moss, filtering through root lattice).
**Mood:** The Knotwood isn't attacking you. It's testing you. The forest wants to know if you're worthy of the Weaver's Mark.

**Enemies:**
- No traditional enemies
- The forest itself: Wisdom saves (DC 14) to resist disorientation, Nature checks (DC 13) to read the forest's intent, Survival checks (DC 15) to maintain direction
- If the player damages the forest (cutting roots, using fire): the maze becomes hostile. Roots grapple (Athletics DC 16 to escape), paths seal, insects swarm.

**Narrative beat:** Skill challenge, not combat. 5 successes / 3 failures. Success: the forest opens a direct path to the Weaver's Mark. Failure: the player reaches the Mark but exhausted (one level of exhaustion) and with the forest's wariness — the Listening Glade won't offer the dream (Act III Side Quest #4).

**Environmental factors:**
- Moving terrain (paths shift between rounds)
- Communication with the forest possible (speaking aloud, singing, touching roots respectfully)
- Singing a Binding Song fragment: automatic success on one check

---

### Encounter 8: Weavers' Cradle Descent

**Location:** The Descent — spiral path from rim to valley floor
**Terrain:** Narrow stone pathway carved into the mountain, wide enough for single file. Weaver's Marks on the walls glow. The Threadlines converge overhead, deafening to anyone with magical sensitivity.
**Lighting:** The First Loom Stone's amber glow illuminates the valley below.
**Mood:** Pilgrimage. The descent should feel sacred and dangerous simultaneously. The weave density is so high it's physical — breathing requires effort. The Weavers' presence is felt as vast, confused love.

**Enemies:**
- No enemies (the Cradle repels Fray creatures)
- Environmental: Constitution saves (DC 13) against weave density (failure: stunned for 1 round as sensory overload hits)
- Wisdom saves (DC 14) against the Weavers' fragmentary consciousness pressing against the player's mind (failure: a vision fragment — disorienting but informative)

**Narrative beat:** This is the approach to the most important location in the game. Every step should build. The Threadlines get louder. The Weaver's Marks get brighter. The First Loom Stone grows from a distant amber point to a pillar of light. By the time the player touches it, they should be overwhelmed by the place itself before the vision even begins.

## Act IV: The Three Paths (Levels 13-16)

### Encounter 9: Keystone Assault (Any Path)

**Location:** Any of the Seven Keystone sites
**Terrain:** Varies by Keystone (mountain summit, coastal cliff, forest clearing, river confluence, etc.). Each Keystone is a Loom Stone surrounded by defensive terrain.
**Lighting:** The Keystone's glow provides 30 feet of bright light.
**Mood:** Desperate defense. The player is trying to do something complex (reverse-Binding, Ward Engine installation, or communication array setup) while Fray creatures assault the site.

**Enemies (scaled per Keystone):**
- Wave 1: 4-6 Looselings + 2 Thread-Puppets
- Wave 2: 2 Thread-Eaters + 1 Frayed One
- Wave 3: 1 Hollow Walker + 2 Thread-Eaters
- (Escalation if extended): 1 Weft Spider (CR 8 — first appearance, needs Loremaster stat block)

**Narrative beat:** Tower defense meets skill challenge. The player must protect the Keystone work while waves of Fray creatures assault it. Allies handle some waves. The player handles the rest. Each wave is stronger because the Keystone work is sending shockwaves through the weave, attracting bigger predators.

**Environmental factors:**
- Keystone glow provides light but also attracts Fray creatures
- The work-in-progress (Binding ceremony / Engine / array) takes 6 rounds to complete
- If work is interrupted: progress resets by 1 round
- Companion and ally NPCs handle secondary flanks

---

### Encounter 10: The Summit Debate (Social Encounter)

**Location:** Loomhearth (or player's chosen base)
**Terrain:** Indoor — the Guild Hall, Archive, or Loom Hall
**Lighting:** Torchlit, formal
**Mood:** Political. Seven faction leaders in a room. Everyone has an agenda. The world is ending and they're arguing about who pays for the solution.

**Enemies:** None (social encounter)

**Participants:** Sable, Longstitch, Verse, Wren, Bryn, Aelith, Thinwalker + the player

**Narrative beat:** This is the game's political climax. Each faction leader makes their case. The player must build consensus using: evidence gathered across Acts I-III, personal relationships, faction reputation, and pure persuasion. Loremaster should build this as a multi-round social skill challenge with faction-specific DCs based on player reputation.

**Skill challenge framework:**
- 7 rounds (one per faction leader)
- Each round: Persuasion, Insight, Intimidation, or Deception vs. faction-specific DC
- Natural 20 = faction fully commits
- Background evidence (Elm's data, Reedmere journal, Weaver's Mark translation, etc.) lowers DCs
- 4+ successes = consensus; 3 = split; 2 or fewer = fracture

## Act V: The Binding (Levels 17-20)

### Encounter 11: Restoration Finale — The Loom Chamber

**Location:** Cavern beneath the First Loom Stone
**Terrain:** Cathedral-scale circular cavern. The ceiling is the First Loom Stone's underside. Threadlines woven into the walls pulse with light. The floor is smooth warm stone.
**Lighting:** Ambient glow from Threadlines — bright, shifting, no shadows.
**Mood:** Awe and terror. The Weavers' consciousness manifests as vast, luminous thread-beings. They are beautiful and confused and potentially hostile.

**Enemies (if diplomacy fails):**
- 1-3 Weaver Fragments (CR 15+ — need stat blocks from Loremaster)
- Environmental: reality distortions as the Weavers try to "re-weave" mortals into the Tapestry

**Narrative beat:** The optimal resolution is NOT combat. The player uses everything — Binding Songs, the seventh verse, companion relationships, faction alliances, and raw persuasion — to introduce mortal civilization to its creators. Combat is the failure state: the Weavers, confused and frightened, try to absorb the player into the Tapestry.

---

### Encounter 12: Release Finale — The Severing

**Location:** Central Keystone (First Loom Stone), with simultaneous action at 6 other Keystones
**Terrain:** The Weavers' Cradle valley. Seven Ward Engines surrounding the First Loom Stone. Fray creatures assaulting from the ridges. Allies defending each Engine.
**Lighting:** Ward Engine glow (blue-white) vs. Fray energy (golden-grey) — a visual battle.
**Mood:** Coordination under pressure. The player isn't fighting alone — they're commanding a continental operation. The real encounter is the last Weaver fragment at the First Loom Stone: it doesn't want to die.

**Enemies:**
- Waves of Fray creatures at each Keystone (allies handle these, with failure reports if overwhelmed)
- At the First Loom Stone: 1 Weaver Fragment (CR 17+ — the last coherent piece of Weaver consciousness)
- Environmental: if any Ward Engine fails, a Fray shockwave hits the Cradle (area damage + Constitution saves)

**Narrative beat:** The climax is a conversation, not a fight. The last Weaver fragment loves its creation. It doesn't want to leave. The player must convince it that mortals are ready. This is the hardest Persuasion check in the game — but every piece of evidence, every alliance, every choice the player made can lower the DC.

---

### Encounter 13: Harmony Finale — The First Song

**Location:** Weavers' Cradle, the Hollowing night
**Terrain:** The valley floor, Keystones arranged in a ring. Speakers positioned at each Keystone. The First Loom Stone at center. The sky is the Fray — pressing down, visible, churning.
**Lighting:** Weaver glow vs. Fray dark. The Threadlines are screaming.
**Mood:** Everything at once. The world's most dangerous night, the world's most important ceremony, and the first real conversation between creators and creation. It's terrifying and beautiful.

**Enemies:**
- Waves of Fray creatures assaulting the Cradle perimeter (allies and Speakers defend)
- 1 Unspun (CR 5) that breaches the perimeter (the player must handle it without breaking the Song)
- Environmental: the Fray itself — Constitution saves for exposure, Wisdom saves against the Weavers' consciousness overwhelming the Speakers

**Narrative beat:** The Song is a sustained skill challenge while the world ends around you. Successes push the Fray back. Failures let it in. The Loom-Born manifest physically to add their voices. Every ally contributes. And in the middle of it all, the Weavers respond. Not in words. In the weave itself stabilizing. The Threadlines stop screaming. The sky clears. The first sunrise after the Hollowing rises over a world that's still here, and still connected to its makers.

**Skill challenge:** 8 successes / 4 failures (the largest in the game). Performance, Arcana, Religion, Persuasion, Constitution. The seventh and eighth verses each grant an automatic success.
