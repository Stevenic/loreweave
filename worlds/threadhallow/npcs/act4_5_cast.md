# Act IV–V Cast — The Three Paths & The Binding

*The player has the truth. These are the people who help them decide what to do with it — and the people who stand in the way.*

---

## New NPCs by Path

Acts IV and V introduce fewer *new* characters and more *transformed* ones. The companions, faction leaders, and allies from Acts I–III become the cast of the endgame. The new faces below fill specific roles that the existing cast can't.

---

## Grand Knotmother Aelith — Head of All Wardweaver Guilds

**Age:** 68. **Appearance:** White hair worn in an elaborate coiled arrangement held together with golden thread-pins — each pin a miniature ward-pattern. Small-framed but commands a room by presence alone. Her robes are layered in the Stitchford fashion: dark outer layer embroidered with the full guild sigil, lighter inner layers visible at wrist and collar. Carries a staff of polished blackthorn wrapped in living ward-thread that pulses faintly with maintained Threadcraft.

**Personality:** If Knotmother Sable is the local obstacle, Grand Knotmother Aelith is the continental one. She oversees all Wardweaver Guild chapters from Stitchford and has held the position for twenty-two years. She is not Sable's superior — the guild is decentralized — but her opinion carries the weight of tradition.

Aelith is not an ideologue. She's a pragmatist who has spent two decades keeping a fractious institution functional through compromise and careful inaction. She doesn't deny the Thinning — she manages it. She allocates resources, mediates between chapters, and makes sure the guilds keep working even when individual Knotmothers disagree.

The player's arrival with the Weavers' truth forces her to make a decision she's avoided for years: support radical action or preserve institutional stability. She hates being forced. She'll respect the player for forcing her anyway.

**Voice:** Formal, deliberate, uses the royal "we" in official contexts and drops it when being honest. "We appreciate the gravity of your discovery. Personally — and I do not say this lightly — I think you may have just ended the world I've spent twenty years holding together. The question is what we build next."

**Role in story:** The continental-level gatekeeper. The player needs guild cooperation for any path — ward expertise for Release, ceremonial coordination for Restoration, institutional legitimacy for Harmony. Aelith is the key to all three. Securing her support is the Act IV political challenge.

**Arc:** Aelith makes her choice based on the player's evidence and the state of the guild. If Sable was already won over in Act II, Aelith follows more readily. If Sable is hostile, Aelith is harder to convince. She ultimately supports whichever path she believes will preserve the most lives — not the most elegant solution, but the most survivable one.

**Secret:** Aelith has been in quiet correspondence with Thinwalker for five years. Not about Frayborn philosophy — about ward-pattern anomalies. They've been sharing data. The guild's greatest traditionalist and its greatest heretic have been collaborating in secret because the data demanded it. Neither has told their respective orders.

## Rook Ashwick — Thornwick's Voice at the Table

**Age:** 24. **Appearance:** Sandy-haired, lean, sunburned. Has Bryn Oakheart's jawline and his father's nervous energy. Wears travel-worn farmer's clothes with a single thread-knot pin at the collar — a Thornwick ward-token Bryn made him carry.

**Personality:** Rook is Bryn's nephew. When Thornwick sent a representative to the larger world in Act III (to spread word of what the player discovered), Bryn sent Rook — not because he's exceptional, but because he's *reliable*, and because she couldn't leave the village.

He is out of his depth and knows it. He's a farmer's kid standing in rooms with Knotmothers and Pathmasters. But he carries Thornwick's perspective — the common folk's perspective — with a stubbornness that comes from having watched his village nearly die. He doesn't let the factions forget that the people at the bottom of the hierarchy are the ones who actually perform the Binding Songs.

**Voice:** Earnest, slightly overwhelmed, cuts through political noise with blunt honesty. "I don't understand half of what you're all arguing about. But I know that back home, my aunt sings the ceremony alone because there's no one left who knows the words. Whatever you decide here — does it help her? Because if it doesn't, I don't see the point."

**Role in story:** The audience surrogate at the Act IV political table. When faction leaders argue theory, Rook grounds the conversation in Thornwick-level stakes. He's the player's reminder of why any of this matters.

**Arc:** Rook grows from overwhelmed messenger to confident advocate. By Act V, he coordinates the Common Folk contingent for whatever path the player chooses — the voice that rallies ordinary people to sing, to work, to hold the line.

**Secret:** Rook has the same ability as Bryn — he can hear the ward-circle humming. He discovered it during the Hollowing ceremony in Act I. He hasn't told Bryn because he's afraid of what it means. He hasn't told anyone because he's not sure anyone would believe a farmer.

## Aldith Ashwick — The Voice in the Stone (Acts IV–V)

**Age:** Dead for sixty years. **Appearance:** When encountered through Loom Stone visions or Weaver-mediated communication, Aldith appears as she was at the height of her power: a tall, raw-boned woman in her fifties with sharp dark eyes and hands rough from thread-work. She wears no guild sigil. She never joined.

**Personality:** Aldith was Maren Ashwick's grandmother and Thornwick's greatest Wardweaver — a woman who, without guild training, without Rememberer archives, without Threadwalker techniques, independently developed Threadcraft so powerful that the ward she wove over Thornwick has held for sixty years after her death.

She's not a ghost. She's a *memory* — woven into the Thornwick ward-circle by her own last Binding Song, the same way the Weavers wove themselves into the Tapestry. She did it on purpose. She understood, instinctively and without the vocabulary, what the Weavers did. And she chose to do the same thing for her village.

She's available in Act IV–V as a source of insight for any player who returns to Thornwick and sings the Binding Song at the ward-circle while carrying knowledge of the Weavers' truth. She doesn't have all the answers — she's a village Wardweaver, not a cosmic being — but she has the perspective of someone who made the same choice the Weavers made, at human scale.

**Voice:** Dry, warm, no-nonsense. Appalachian-grandmother energy. Speaks in fragments because maintaining coherence costs her. "Mmm. So they did the same thing I did, just — bigger. Much bigger. And it wore out. That tracks. Everything wears out, love. The trick is knowing when to let it."

**Role in story:** Optional but powerful. She provides context for the endgame choice: she is the Weavers' decision writ small. She wove herself into Thornwick's ward. It held for sixty years. Now it's fading. The same pattern, the same choice, the same outcome — on a village scale instead of a continental one. She is evidence that there's no permanent solution, only choices about how long you're willing to hold.

**Arc:** None — she's a memory, not a living character. But her existence enriches all three endgame paths: Restoration (can you bring back what was given willingly?), Release (she shows what "letting go" looks like — her ward is fading and she's at peace with it), Harmony (she proves mortal-weave dialogue is possible, since she *is* a mortal woven into the weave).

**Secret:** Aldith's Fifth Verse — the one buried with her — is not a Binding Song verse. It's an *address*. A greeting in the Weaver language, composed by a woman who heard the Weavers in the ward-circle sixty years ago and said hello. The Weavers never forgot it. It's the reason Thornwick's ward held so long — the Weavers maintained it as a courtesy, a kindness returned. The implications are staggering.

## NPC Transformations in Acts IV–V

Most of the Act IV–V "cast" is made up of returning characters whose roles shift dramatically:

| NPC | Acts I–III Role | Act IV–V Role |
|-----|----------------|---------------|
| **Sorrel Greenhand** | Lost apprentice → competent Wardweaver | The strongest mortal Threadcraft practitioner. Essential for all three paths. |
| **Farweft** | Guide and walker | Bridge between Threadwalker orders. Coordinator of field operations. |
| **Wren Looseweft** | Hollow Court leader | Thread-Iron pioneer. Ward Engine chief smith (Release) or Fray-interface crafter (Restoration/Harmony). |
| **Wynn Hollowscript** | Isolated linguist | The first Speaker. Architect of mortal-Weaver communication. |
| **Knotmother Sable** | Political obstacle | Ally (if convinced) or obstacle (if not). Controls guild resources. |
| **Pathmaster Longstitch** | Threadwalker authority | Facilitator. Convinces the orders to cooperate regardless of path. |
| **Verse** | Archive leader | Harmony architect. Designs the Speaker training program. Composes the true Binding Song framework. |
| **Elm Bindfast** | Quiet reformist | Chief engineer (Release) or first guild Speaker (Harmony) or ceremony technician (Restoration). |
| **Glasseye** | Salvager, observer | Weaver-perceiver. The eyes of any path's final ceremony. |
| **Thinwalker** | Frayborn founder | Philosophical anchor. Offers the "Fray is potential, not enemy" perspective at the endgame table. |
| **Bryn Oakheart** | Village headwoman | The Common Folk leader. Organizes the thousand voices needed for any path's finale. |
| **Old Hawthorn** | Eccentric peddler | His thirty years of trade route data becomes the most comprehensive map of the Thinning's advance. The Rememberers finally get his testimony. |
| **Colm Bindstone** | Grumpy lay-priest | The voice of tradition at the endgame. Reminds everyone that the ceremonies have held for centuries — maybe listen to the people who've been doing the work. |
| **Tam Woolsey** | Scared shepherd | The everyman. Shows up at the finale because Bryn asked, scared out of his mind, sings anyway. His presence is the emotional proof that the Common Folk matter. |
| **Hest** | Lost walker, returned | Bridge between Hollow Court and Threadwalkers. His Fray-weaving ability combined with Wren's Thread-Iron changes what's mechanically possible. |
| **Rook Ashwick** | (new in Act IV) | Common Folk coordinator. Rallies the ordinary people. |
| **Aldith Ashwick** | (memory, Acts IV–V) | The Weavers' decision at human scale. Proof of concept for mortal-weave integration. |
| **Grand Knotmother Aelith** | (new in Act IV) | Continental-level gatekeeper. Guild cooperation depends on her. |
