# Act I Cast — Thornwick NPCs

*The people the player meets first. They set the tone for the entire game.*

---

## Bryn Oakheart — Village Headwoman

**Age:** 45. **Appearance:** Broad-shouldered, sun-lined face, calloused hands. Wears her hair in a practical knot secured with a woven thread-pin (her mother's).

**Personality:** Bryn runs Thornwick the way a good farmer runs a field — with attention, effort, and no illusions. She doesn't inspire. She *manages*. When the bridge floods, Bryn is already organizing sandbagging crews. When a family feud threatens the harvest, Bryn mediates with the patience of someone who's heard it all before.

She's exhausted. Maren's death hit the village hard, and Bryn is holding it together through sheer stubbornness while privately terrified about the Hollowing.

**Voice:** Direct, slightly clipped. Doesn't waste words. Uses farming metaphors. "We plant with what we have, not what we wish for."

**Role in story:** The player's first quest-giver. She asks for help not because she trusts the player, but because she's out of options. Her respect is earned through action, not words.

**Secret:** Bryn can hear the ward-circle humming. She's never told anyone. She doesn't have the Sight — she has a grandmother's lullaby that accidentally taught her to listen. She doesn't know this is significant.

---

## Sorrel Greenhand — The Lost Apprentice

**Age:** 28. **Appearance:** Lean, anxious, dirt under her nails. Lives in a makeshift camp in the Thinning Forest. Her clothes are forest-stained and mended with Threadcraft — tiny, precise repairs that reveal her skill.

**Personality:** Sorrel is talented, scared, and angry at herself. She left Thornwick two years ago after an argument with Maren — the old woman wanted Sorrel to commit to the Wardweaver path, and Sorrel felt trapped. She said things she regrets. Maren died before they reconciled.

Now Sorrel lives alone at the edge of the Thinning, unable to go home (guilt) and unable to leave the area (she can feel the weave loosening and it frightens her). She performs small maintenance on the Threadlines near her camp — not enough to fix anything, but enough to notice how bad things are getting.

**Voice:** Defensive, sharp, then vulnerable when her guard drops. She talks to herself. She talks to the trees. She's been alone too long.

**Role in story:** She knows three of the five Binding Song verses. Convincing her to return to Thornwick is the first major social challenge of the game. She's not unreasonable — she wants to help — but she needs the player to acknowledge that the situation is complicated, not just say "come back."

**Arc:** If the player brings her home, Sorrel becomes Thornwick's new Wardweaver and a potential companion for Acts II–V. She grows from guilt-ridden recluse to confident practitioner. If the player doesn't invest in the relationship, she provides the verses but stays in the forest, and Thornwick's long-term recovery is slower.

**Secret:** Sorrel has been hearing *something* in the Threadlines. Not words — patterns. She thinks she's imagining it. She isn't. She's one of the rare mortals who can perceive the Weavers' distributed consciousness.

## Colm Bindstone — Lay-Priest of Morrith Hollowdark

**Age:** 72. **Appearance:** Thin, stooped, sharp eyes under heavy brows. Wears a thread-cord around his left wrist with seven knots (one for each person he's buried this year). Carries a walking stick carved with Morrith's symbol — a thread with a frayed end.

**Personality:** Colm is the oldest person in Thornwick and acts like it. He speaks in proverbs, folklore, and pointed silences. He's not a Threadcraft practitioner, but he knows more about the weave than most Stitchwrights — he just knows it as *tradition*, not theory.

He's grumpy, opinionated, and protective of the old ways. He resents the Wardweaver Guilds for treating village traditions as superstition. He distrusts outsiders. But he's also genuinely wise, and when the player earns his respect, he becomes an invaluable source of deep lore.

**Voice:** Short sentences. Proverbs. "A thread untended is a thread lost." Refers to the player as "the wanderer" until they prove themselves, then by name.

**Role in story:** Gatekeeper to the Fifth Verse quest. The missing verse was buried with Maren's grandmother, and Colm presides over burial rites. Disturbing the dead violates everything he believes in. Convincing him requires either a compelling argument, discovery of a loophole in tradition, or an alternative solution.

**Secret:** Colm has performed the village Binding Song solo every ceremony for the past three years — Maren was too sick to lead but no one knew. He doesn't know the full song. He's been *improvising* the parts he doesn't know, and the ward has held. This means either Colm is unconsciously channeling real Threadcraft, or the weave is more forgiving than doctrine suggests. Both possibilities frighten him.

## Old Hawthorn — The Traveling Peddler

**Age:** Indeterminate (looks 60, moves like 40). **Appearance:** Weather-beaten, perpetually cheerful, wears a coat with too many pockets. His cart is pulled by a mule named Stitch (yes, really).

**Personality:** Hawthorn is a contradiction: he's a simple traveling peddler who happens to know things he shouldn't. Trade routes that were just established. Rumors from towns three weeks' travel away. The names of people he's never met. He explains this as "good ears and long roads." Nobody fully believes him.

**Voice:** Folksy, rambling, drops significant information into casual conversation. "Heard a funny thing in Millhaven — their Wardweaver's been buying extra thread. Double the usual order. Now why would she need twice the thread, unless the stitches aren't holding like they used to?"

**Role in story:** Information source and mystery. He sells the player a map that's half-accurate and half-interesting. His goods are useful but eclectic. He appears in multiple acts, always having arrived just before the player.

**Secret:** Hawthorn is Fray-touched. Mildly — he has the temporal perception variant. He always knows the exact time, he can sense Fray activity over moderate distances, and he heals slightly faster than normal. He's never told anyone. He doesn't identify with the Hollow Court. He considers himself a peddler who had a bad experience in a thin place thirty years ago and got some perks out of it.

His real value: Hawthorn has been unknowingly mapping the Thinning's advance for decades through his trade routes. His memory of which villages were healthy and which were struggling — and when the change happened — is the most comprehensive longitudinal record of the Thinning in existence. The Rememberers would kill for his testimony.

---

## Tam Woolsey — The Shepherd

**Age:** 19. **Appearance:** Stocky, sun-freckled, perpetually worried. Smells of sheep.

**Personality:** Tam is the youngest adult in the core cast. He's kind, earnest, and terrified of the changes happening in the forest. His sheep are his responsibility, and when three of them come back *changed* from the Thinning Forest, he doesn't know what to do.

He represents the Common Folk perspective: not interested in cosmology or factional politics, just wants things to be normal. His fear is genuine and grounding. When the player is neck-deep in Weaver revelations, Tam's worry about his sheep reminds them what the stakes actually look like at ground level.

**Voice:** Nervous, apologetic, genuine. "I'm sorry to bother you with this, it's just — they came back wrong, and I don't know who else to ask."

**Role in story:** Side quest (Lost Flock) and emotional anchor. Returns in later acts if the player builds the relationship, providing a Common Folk perspective on the unfolding events.

---

## Aldric Tapper — The Pub Owner

**Age:** 50. **Appearance:** Round, red-cheeked, always wiping a mug. The Knotted Ewe is his life's work and he's proud of it.

**Personality:** Aldric is the social hub of Thornwick. He hears everything, remembers most of it, and shares what he thinks people need to hear (which isn't always the truth, and isn't always a lie). He's not malicious — he's a gossip who understands that information is currency in a small village.

He subtly undermines Bryn's authority not out of ambition but out of a genuine belief that she's too rigid. He thinks the village should be more open to trade, to visitors, to new ideas. He and Bryn are friends who drive each other crazy.

**Voice:** Warm, conspiratorial, always offering a drink. "Sit, sit. You look like you've had a day. What are you having? First one's on the house."

**Role in story:** Rumor mill. Side quest seeds. Social space for NPC interactions. The pub is where the player hears about opportunities, problems, and gossip that leads to content.
