# Act III Cast — The Deep Weave

*The player has left civilization behind. These are the people at the edges — scholars, survivors, and the almost-lost.*

---

## Wynn Hollowscript — The Linguist in the Grass

**Age:** 49. **Appearance:** Weathered past her years. Sun-darkened skin, deep crow's feet, hands permanently stained with ink and grass-dye. Hair that was once black is now streaked gray and white, roughly cut with a knife. Wears layered traveling clothes so patched they're practically quilted. Her tent in the Threadfall Expanse is surrounded by hundreds of pages of hand-drawn notation, pegged to the ground with stones, fluttering in the wind like prayer flags.

**Personality:** Wynn is what happens when an exceptional mind is left alone too long. She was a brilliant Thread-Linguist — the Rememberers' best — who became convinced twenty years ago that the Threadline vibration patterns carried structured information. The Archive thought she was chasing ghosts. She walked into the Threadfall Expanse to prove them wrong, and she never came back.

She's not mad. But she's not entirely here. She talks to herself, answers her own questions, and occasionally addresses the Threadlines as though they're listening (they are, she just can't prove it yet). She's intensely protective of her work — twenty years of notation in a shorthand only she can read. She's also desperately lonely, though she'd rather eat her own boots than admit it.

She's paranoid, brilliant, prickly, and the most important person in the world. She just doesn't know the last part yet.

**Voice:** Rapid, fragmented, self-interrupting. Swings between lecturing and muttering. "No, no, you're looking at the wrong frequency — here, see this line? This oscillation repeats every fourteen days. Fourteen. Not thirteen, not fifteen. The solstice cycle divides into — are you writing this down? Never mind, I'll write it. Where's my — I had a pen. I definitely had a pen."

**Role in story:** The Act III revelation depends on Wynn. Her linguistic framework is the Rosetta Stone for the Weaver language. Without her, the vision at the First Loom Stone is overwhelming and fragmentary. With her, it's devastating and clear. Recruiting her is the Act III social challenge.

**Arc:** Wynn goes from isolated genius to the first Speaker — the first mortal who consciously communicates with the Weavers. Her twenty years of "wasted" work becomes the foundation for a new era of mortal-Weaver partnership. The moment when the Weavers respond to her first question ("Finally.") is the emotional climax of her character arc.

**Secret:** Wynn left the Archive not just because they doubted her — she left because she was *afraid*. Three months before her departure, she performed an experiment: she played a Binding Song fragment backward near a Threadline. The Threadline *moved*. Not vibrated — physically repositioned itself toward her. She ran. It took her a year to go back. She's been cautiously approaching the Threadlines ever since, but she's never repeated that experiment. She will, when the player is there. It goes better the second time.

## Glasseye — Salvager, Pattern-Seer

**Age:** Somewhere between 30 and 50 (Fray-touch makes it impossible to tell). **Appearance:** Average height, nondescript build. You'd walk past him in a crowd. Then you'd notice his eyes — a flat, silvered surface that reflects light like mercury. No visible iris or pupil. He sees perfectly, but what he sees isn't what you see. He wears dark wraps around his eyes in bright sunlight, not for protection but because pattern sight in direct light gives him migraines.

His clothes are practical salvager's gear — tough fabric, many pockets, nothing decorative. His hands are scarred from Fray-zone work. He moves carefully, like someone who's learned that the ground isn't always where it looks.

**Personality:** Glasseye is quiet, not shy. He's learned that describing what he sees to people who can't see it either frightens them or makes them think he's insane. So he usually doesn't. He'll tell you a room is safe or dangerous, but he won't explain that he can see the weave's structure running through the walls like a nervous system, and three of the threads are frayed, and the pattern suggests something pressed against the weave from the other side about six hours ago.

When he does open up — usually with other Fray-touched or with someone who's earned his trust — he speaks about the world with a precision that's almost poetic. He doesn't see beauty in sunsets. He sees beauty in the architecture of reality. To him, a well-woven ward is a cathedral.

He chose "Glasseye" because his eyes make people uncomfortable and he'd rather name the discomfort than pretend it doesn't exist.

**Voice:** Brief, precise, occasionally revelatory. "The Threadline here splits four ways. Three are carrying weight — ward maintenance from the settlement east of here. The fourth is singing. I don't know what to tell you. It's singing."

**Role in story:** Glasseye confirms the player's Act III revelation from direct perception. He's *seen* the Weavers in the Threadlines — flickers of coherent pattern that he mistook for dead echoes. Learning they're alive reorganizes everything he knows. He becomes an essential guide for Act IV–V regardless of path.

**Arc:** Glasseye goes from observer to participant. For years he's watched the weave passively — describing, not intervening. The revelation that the Weavers are alive and fading gives him purpose. On the Restoration path, he guides the Keystone ceremonies (he can see the Weavers re-cohering). On the Release path, he monitors the Weavers' dissolution to ensure it's peaceful. On the Harmony path, he serves as a translator between the Weavers' visual patterns and Wynn's auditory framework — the missing link.

**Secret:** Glasseye has seen something in the deepest Frayed zones that he's never told anyone: structure. Not random Fray chaos — *architecture*. Patterns in the Fray that don't come from the Tapestry. He doesn't know what they are. He suspects they're what the Tapestry was woven *from* — the raw blueprint the Weavers worked with. If he's right, the Fray isn't entropy. It's the original design, waiting.

## Hest — The Walker Who Came Back

**Age:** 36. **Appearance:** Gaunt, hollow-eyed, Fray-touched. His skin has the slightly translucent quality of deep Fray exposure. His left hand trembles — not from weakness, but from temporal desynchronization (it moves a half-second behind the rest of him). His Threadwalker coat is in tatters, repaired with materials that shouldn't exist — Fray-crystallized fibers woven with techniques no Threadwalker ever taught.

He looks like a man who survived something that should have killed him. He did.

**Personality:** Hest was Farweft's patrol partner. Two years ago, a Fray manifestation pulled him into a thin place that sealed behind him. He's been surviving inside a Frayed pocket — a bubble of partially unraveled reality — with nothing but his training, his will, and an increasingly intimate understanding of the Fray.

He's different now. He speaks slowly, chooses words with care, and sometimes pauses mid-sentence as though listening to something no one else can hear. He's not broken — he's *calibrated*. Two years inside the Fray taught him to think in patterns rather than words. He can feel the Threadlines the way a sailor feels the tide.

He doesn't resent Farweft for moving on. He doesn't resent the order for declaring him dead. He understood the probability assessment. He survived anyway.

**Voice:** Slow, careful, patient. Occasionally says something that reframes the entire conversation. "The Fray isn't hostile. I lived in it for two years. It was... waiting. I think it's always been waiting. I just don't know what for."

**Role in story:** Hest is the resolution of Farweft's companion quest. Finding and extracting him is a major event in Act III or early Act IV. His survival — and his Fray-touch — forces Farweft to reconsider everything she believed about the old ways vs. new ways. He provides firsthand testimony about what the Fray looks like from *inside*.

**Arc:** Hest joins the Hollow Court after extraction. He doesn't rejoin the Threadwalkers — he's changed too much. But he becomes a bridge between the Court and the orders, the first person who belongs to both worlds. His experience is invaluable for any path: he's been inside the thing everyone's trying to understand.

**Secret:** Hest can weave Fray material. Not Threadcraft, not Wardweaving — something new. Inside the pocket, he learned to take the unstructured potential of the Fray and give it temporary form. He can make tools, shelters, even crude wards from raw Fray. He doesn't know the implications. Wren will, when she sees it. The combination of his technique and her Thread-Iron could change everything.
