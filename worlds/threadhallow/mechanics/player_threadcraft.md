# Player Threadcraft Progression

*Addendum to the Threadhallow Mechanics Companion §2*
*Custom character option for D&D 5e — how a player learns and uses Threadcraft*
*Designed as a feat chain, not a class or multiclass, to be accessible to any character build*

---

## 1. Design Philosophy

Threadcraft is the world's magic — but it's not *the player's* magic. D&D already has a full spellcasting system. Threadcraft should:

1. **Complement, not replace** existing class features
2. **Be available to any class** — a Fighter can learn basic wards, a Wizard can study Threadline theory
3. **Carry real risk** — Fray exposure is the cost. Every use frays the caster.
4. **Progress through the story**, not just through leveling
5. **Feel different from standard spellcasting** — it's weaving, not incanting

The solution: a **feat chain** with narrative prerequisites. Each feat unlocks a tier of Threadcraft. Feats use the 2024 Free Rules feat structure (General Feats, any class, ability score prerequisites).

## 2. Threadcraft Feat Chain

### Tier 1: Threadcraft Initiate (General Feat)

**Prerequisite:** Level 4+, Intelligence or Wisdom 13+, must have witnessed Threadcraft being performed (narrative gate — happens naturally in Act I)

**Benefit:**
- You learn 2 cantrips from any one Threadcraft tradition's spell list (see Mechanics Companion §2).
- You learn 1 first-level spell from that tradition. You can cast it once without a spell slot, regaining the ability after a long rest. If you have spell slots, you can also cast it using those.
- Your spellcasting ability for Threadcraft is **Constitution** (per Mechanics Companion §2 — Threadcraft channels the body's pattern, not intellect or faith).
- **Fray Risk:** When you cast a Threadcraft spell of 1st level or higher, you must make a DC 10 Constitution saving throw. On failure, you gain 1 level of Fray Exposure (see Mechanics Companion §3). This DC increases by 1 for each Threadcraft spell you've cast since your last long rest.

**Tradition Choices:**
| Tradition | Flavor | Key Cantrips | Key 1st-Level Spell |
|-----------|--------|-------------|---------------------|
| Wardweaving | Protective barriers | *Mending*, *Resistance* | *Shield of Faith* |
| Threadwalking | Navigation, sensing | *Guidance*, *Druidcraft* | *Detect Magic* |
| Binding | Song-based, communal | *Minor Illusion*, *Thaumaturgy* | *Bless* |
| Remembering | Knowledge, history | *Message*, *Prestidigitation* | *Identify* |
| Fraytouch | Dangerous, entropic | *Chill Touch*, *Toll the Dead* | *Inflict Wounds* |
| Threadline Reading | Perception, truth | *Light*, *Guidance* | *Comprehend Languages* |

---

### Tier 2: Threadcraft Adept (General Feat)

**Prerequisite:** Level 8+, Constitution 14+, Threadcraft Initiate feat, must have trained with a practitioner of your tradition (narrative gate — available in Act II through faction NPCs)

**Benefit:**
- You learn 1 additional cantrip from your tradition.
- You learn 2 spells of 2nd level or lower from your tradition. You can cast each once without a spell slot, regaining the ability after a long rest.
- Your Fray Risk DC resets to 10 after a long rest (instead of carrying forward). You've learned to manage the strain.
- **Tradition Feature:** You gain your tradition's signature ability:

| Tradition | Signature Ability |
|-----------|------------------|
| Wardweaving | **Ward Sense.** You can sense ward strength within 60 ft. (exact number on a DC 12 Arcana check). You have advantage on saves against effects that would lower ward strength. |
| Threadwalking | **Thread Step.** Once per long rest, you can teleport up to 30 ft. to a point you can see along a visible Threadline. This does not provoke opportunity attacks. |
| Binding | **Harmonic Voice.** When you cast a Threadcraft spell with a verbal component, allies within 30 ft. gain temporary HP equal to your proficiency bonus. Once per short rest. |
| Remembering | **Pattern Memory.** You can cast *Identify* at will without components on objects related to the weave (Loom Stones, ward circles, Threadcraft items). You have advantage on History and Arcana checks related to the Weavers. |
| Fraytouch | **Entropy Channel.** When you deal necrotic or force damage with a Threadcraft spell, you can add your Constitution modifier to the damage. Once per turn. |
| Threadline Reading | **Weave Sight.** You can see Threadlines, weave state, and Fray creatures' true nature within 60 ft. This functions as *Detect Magic* + *See Invisibility* but only for weave-related phenomena. Lasts 10 minutes, once per short rest. |

---

### Tier 3: Threadcraft Master (General Feat)

**Prerequisite:** Level 13+, Constitution 16+, Threadcraft Adept feat, must have completed a significant Threadcraft achievement (narrative gate — repairing a Loom Stone, completing the Singing Cairn anchors, or similar Act III milestone)

**Benefit:**
- You learn 2 spells of 4th level or lower from your tradition. You can cast each once without a spell slot, regaining the ability after a long rest.
- Your Fray Risk DC base drops to 8 (mastery reduces baseline risk).
- **Fray Resistance.** You have advantage on Constitution saving throws against Fray Exposure from environmental sources (not from your own casting).
- **Dual Tradition.** You may learn 1 cantrip and 1 spell (2nd level or lower) from a second Threadcraft tradition.

---

### Tier 3.5: Speaker (Story Feat — Harmony Path Only)

**Prerequisite:** Threadcraft Master feat, Harmony path chosen in Act IV, trained by Wynn Hollowscript

**Benefit:**
- You can cast the **Binding Song** as a ritual (10 minutes). This is a unique Threadcraft spell:
  - **Level:** 5th (Threadcraft)
  - **Components:** V, S (singing + hand gestures mimicking weaving motions)
  - **Duration:** Concentration, up to 1 hour
  - **Effect:** You open a channel of communication between mortal consciousness and the Weavers' distributed consciousness in the Tapestry. Weavers within range (1 mile per proficiency bonus) can perceive mortal intent and respond through environmental changes (Threadlines brighten, weave stabilizes, visions appear).
  - **Ward Effect:** While concentrating, the Binding Song functions as a ward. Ward strength = 10 × proficiency bonus within a 100-ft. radius.
- No Fray Risk for the Binding Song. The Song *is* the weave responding to itself through a mortal voice. It is not fraying — it is mending.

## 3. Fray Exposure and Player Threadcraft

### The Risk Curve

The escalating Fray Risk DC is the core balancing mechanic. A player who casts 1 Threadcraft spell per day faces DC 10 — manageable. A player who casts 5 in one day faces DC 14 on the last one — dangerous.

| Casts Since Last Long Rest | Fray Risk DC (Initiate/Adept) | Fray Risk DC (Master) |
|---------------------------|-------------------------------|----------------------|
| 1st | 10 | 8 |
| 2nd | 11 | 9 |
| 3rd | 12 | 10 |
| 4th | 13 | 11 |
| 5th | 14 | 12 |
| 6th+ | 15+ | 13+ |

**Fray Exposure Consequences (Mechanics Companion §3 summary):**
| Level | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| 1 | Minor sensory distortion (cosmetic) |
| 2 | Disadvantage on Perception checks |
| 3 | Vulnerability to force damage |
| 4 | Speed halved |
| 5 | Disadvantage on all ability checks |
| 6 | HP maximum halved |
| 7 | Death (pattern dissolves) |

**Recovery:** 1 level removed per long rest in a warded area (ward strength ≥ 50). Greater Restoration removes 1 level anywhere. The Binding Song ritual removes all levels (Master feat, 10-minute cast).

### Strategic Tension

Threadcraft is *powerful but costly.* A Tier 2 Wardweaver can cast Shield of Faith without a spell slot — but risks Fray Exposure. A Tier 3 Master with dual tradition has access to multiple traditions — but more casting means higher DCs. The system rewards:

- **Judicious use:** Cast Threadcraft when it matters, not as routine.
- **Warded rest:** Sleeping in settlements (stable weave) vs. camping in frayed zones.
- **Companion synergy:** Sorrel can cast Threadcraft cantrips safely (she's a Wardweaver NPC). Let her handle minor tasks.

## 4. Interaction with Existing Class Features

### Spellcasters

Threadcraft spells are added to your known/prepared spells but use Constitution as the casting ability, not your class ability. This means:
- A Wizard with INT 20 and CON 14 casts Wizard spells at +10 but Threadcraft spells at +5
- Spell slots are shared — you *can* use a spell slot to cast a Threadcraft spell, but it still triggers Fray Risk
- Threadcraft cantrips use your proficiency bonus but CON modifier for attack/DC

### Martials

For non-spellcasters, Threadcraft feats are particularly valuable:
- 2 cantrips + 1 spell (Tier 1) gives a Fighter basic utility magic
- The tradition features (Tier 2) provide class-fantasy-adjacent abilities (Ward Sense for a defensive Fighter, Thread Step for a mobile Rogue)
- Fray Risk is slightly higher for martials (typically lower CON save proficiency), creating natural risk/reward tension

### Multiclassers

No special rules. Threadcraft feats stack with any class combination. The only interaction is spell slot sharing (Threadcraft spells can be cast with class spell slots).

## 6. Engine Implementation

### Data Model

```
PlayerThreadcraft {
    tradition: ThreadcraftTradition;      // primary tradition
    secondTradition?: ThreadcraftTradition; // only at Master tier
    tier: 1 | 2 | 3;                      // Initiate / Adept / Master
    isSpeaker: boolean;                   // Harmony path only
    knownCantrips: string[];              // spell IDs
    knownSpells: string[];                // spell IDs
    freecastsRemaining: Map<string, number>; // spell ID → uses remaining today
    frayRiskCastsToday: number;           // for escalating DC
    signatureAbilityUsed: boolean;        // for short/long rest tracking
}
```

### Pure Functions (for Beacon)

```
getFrayRiskDC(tier: number, castsToday: number): number
rollFrayRisk(dc: number, conMod: number, profBonus: number, proficient: boolean): { passed: boolean; roll: number }
castThreadcraftSpell(state: PlayerThreadcraft, spellId: string, useSpellSlot: boolean): PlayerThreadcraft
    // Increments frayRiskCastsToday, decrements freecastsRemaining if applicable
getAvailableSpells(state: PlayerThreadcraft): SpellInfo[]
canLearnFeat(playerLevel: number, conScore: number, currentTier: number, narrativeGate: boolean): boolean
```

## 7. RAW Deviations

| Deviation | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|
| Constitution as spellcasting ability | Per Mechanics Companion §2, Threadcraft channels the body's connection to the Tapestry. This is a world-specific design — not a generic D&D mechanic. CON as casting ability is rare but not unprecedented (some homebrew psionic systems use it). |
| Escalating Fray Risk DC per cast | No RAW equivalent. This is the core balancing mechanic — it makes Threadcraft powerful but self-limiting. Modeled after the exhaustion system's cumulative severity. |
| Feat chain with narrative prerequisites | 2024 Free Rules feats have level prerequisites but not story prerequisites. The narrative gates ensure Threadcraft progression tracks with the story (you can't learn Tier 2 without meeting a teacher). Engine enforces this via flags. |
| Speaker feat (Harmony path only) | Path-locked content. This is a story reward, not a general character option. Only one path grants it. |
| True Seeing as 4th-level spell (Threadline Reading) | True Seeing is a 6th-level spell in RAW. The Threadline Reading version is limited to 10-minute duration (vs. 1 hour) and only reveals weave-related phenomena. This reduction in scope justifies the lower level. |
| Fraytouch tradition +2 DC | No RAW equivalent for tradition-specific spell risk. Narrative justification: Fraytouch channels the Fray itself, which is inherently more dangerous than other traditions that work *with* the weave rather than *against* it. |
