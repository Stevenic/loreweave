# High-CR Fray Creatures — Stat Blocks

*Addendum to the Threadhallow Mechanics Companion §8*
*Source: Bard's narrative concepts (fray_creatures.md §Future Creatures), codified per 2024 Free Rules*

All creatures share the **Fray-Born** trait from §8 of the Mechanics Companion:

> **Fray-Born.** This creature is native to or was created by the Fray. It is immune to the Frightened condition. It has resistance to force damage. When reduced to 0 HP, it unravels into loose threads that dissolve in 1d4 rounds (no death saves). *Detect Magic* reveals it as a weave anomaly.

---

## 1. Weft Spider (CR 8)

*Huge aberration, neutral evil*

A massive Fray predator that builds "webs" from stolen Threadlines — rerouting the local weave to create hunting grounds. Its presence warps a 120-ft. radius, creating a pocket of redirected reality where movement, distance, and even spell targeting follow the spider's Threadline web instead of normal geometry.

**Armor Class** 16 (natural armor)
**Hit Points** 136 (16d12 + 32)
**Speed** 40 ft., climb 40 ft.

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 18 (+4) | 16 (+3) | 14 (+2) | 12 (+1) | 15 (+2) | 8 (-1) |

**Saving Throws** DEX +6, WIS +5
**Skills** Perception +5, Stealth +6
**Damage Resistances** force
**Damage Immunities** psychic
**Condition Immunities** Frightened, Restrained
**Senses** Tremorsense 60 ft., blindsight 30 ft., passive Perception 15
**Languages** understands Deep Speech but can't speak
**Challenge** 8 (3,900 XP) **Proficiency Bonus** +3

### Traits

**Fray-Born.** (See above.)

**Threadline Web (Recharge 5–6).** As a bonus action, the Weft Spider can reshape its Threadline web in a 120-foot radius centered on itself. Until the start of its next turn, the area becomes difficult terrain for all creatures except the spider, and creatures that start their turn in the area must succeed on a DC 14 Wisdom saving throw or use their movement to travel in a direction of the spider's choice (toward or away from it).

**Web Sense.** While in contact with a surface, the Weft Spider knows the exact location of any creature in contact with the same surface within 120 feet.

**Spider Climb.** The spider can climb difficult surfaces, including upside down on ceilings, without needing to make an ability check.

### Actions

**Multiattack.** The Weft Spider makes two Threadline Fang attacks.

**Threadline Fang.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +7 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. *Hit:* 13 (2d8 + 4) piercing damage plus 9 (2d8) force damage. On a hit, the target must succeed on a DC 14 Constitution saving throw or have its speed reduced by 10 feet until the end of its next turn (threads binding its limbs).

**Weave Snare (Recharge 5–6).** The spider targets one creature it can see within 60 feet. The target must succeed on a DC 14 Dexterity saving throw or be restrained by Threadline webbing. A restrained creature can use its action to make a DC 14 Strength (Athletics) check, freeing itself on a success. While restrained, the creature takes 7 (2d6) force damage at the start of each of its turns as the threads tighten and drain pattern.

**Redirect Spell.** When a creature within 120 feet casts a spell that targets a point or creature, the spider can use its reaction to make a DC 14 Wisdom saving throw for the caster. On a failure, the spell's target is redirected to a different valid target of the spider's choice within range. This ability does not work on spells that target only the caster.

### Reactions

**Thread Anchor.** When the Weft Spider would be moved against its will, it can anchor itself to the Threadline web, negating the movement. It can also use this reaction when it takes damage from a melee attack to pull the attacker 10 feet closer (DC 14 Strength save negates).

### Lair Actions

When fought in its Threadline web (a region it has occupied for at least 24 hours), the Weft Spider can take lair actions on initiative count 20 (losing ties):

- **Threadline Pulse.** Each creature in the web must succeed on a DC 14 Dexterity saving throw or be pulled 15 feet toward the spider.
- **Reality Fold.** The spider teleports to any point within its web within 60 feet.
- **Web Collapse.** A 20-foot-radius area within the web becomes a void — creatures in the area fall 20 feet into a pocket of raw Fray (2d10 force damage, DC 14 DEX save for half), then reappear prone at the area's edge.

## 2. The Loom-Ghost (CR 10)

*Medium undead (aberration), chaotic neutral*

An echo of a Weaver, trapped in the Fray for 1,200 years. Not truly alive — a memory given form by the residual pattern the Weaver left behind. Remembers fragments of the Binding Song. Can be communicated with but never fully trusted — its responses are corrupted by centuries of isolation and Fray degradation.

**Armor Class** 17 (natural deflection — threads of ancient pattern woven around it)
**Hit Points** 150 (20d8 + 60)
**Speed** 0 ft., fly 40 ft. (hover)

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 6 (-2) | 16 (+3) | 16 (+3) | 20 (+5) | 18 (+4) | 17 (+3) |

**Saving Throws** INT +9, WIS +8, CHA +7
**Skills** Arcana +9, History +9, Insight +8, Perception +8
**Damage Resistances** acid, fire, lightning, thunder; bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing from nonmagical attacks
**Damage Immunities** cold, necrotic, poison, force
**Condition Immunities** Charmed, Exhaustion, Frightened, Grappled, Paralyzed, Petrified, Poisoned, Prone, Restrained
**Senses** Truesight 120 ft., passive Perception 18
**Languages** All (speaks in fragmented, archaic form); telepathy 120 ft.
**Challenge** 10 (5,900 XP) **Proficiency Bonus** +4

### Traits

**Fray-Born.** (See above.)

**Incorporeal Movement.** The Loom-Ghost can move through other creatures and objects as if they were difficult terrain. It takes 5 (1d10) force damage if it ends its turn inside an object.

**Weaver's Memory.** The Loom-Ghost retains fragments of Weaver knowledge. A creature that spends 1 minute in conversation can make a DC 17 Intelligence (Arcana) or Charisma (Persuasion) check. On a success, the ghost shares one true piece of information about the Weavers, the Tapestry, or the Binding Song. On a failure, it shares something that *sounds* true but is corrupted by Fray interference. The DM determines which. The ghost can only be consulted 3 times before it becomes agitated and hostile.

**Pattern Aura.** Living creatures within 30 feet of the Loom-Ghost feel a profound sense of grief and dissonance. Creatures starting their turn in the aura must succeed on a DC 16 Wisdom saving throw or have disadvantage on concentration checks until the start of their next turn.

**Legendary Resistance (2/Day).** If the Loom-Ghost fails a saving throw, it can choose to succeed instead.

### Actions

**Multiattack.** The Loom-Ghost makes two Pattern Rend attacks.

**Pattern Rend.** *Melee Spell Attack:* +9 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 18 (3d8 + 5) psychic damage. The target must succeed on a DC 17 Intelligence saving throw or have one random spell slot of 3rd level or lower expended (no effect if the target has no spell slots).

**Binding Fragment (Recharge 5–6).** The Loom-Ghost sings a corrupted fragment of the Binding Song. Each creature within 30 feet must make a DC 17 Wisdom saving throw. On a failure, the creature is Incapacitated and its speed becomes 0 for 1 minute as it experiences fragmented visions of the Weavers' sacrifice. A creature can repeat the save at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on itself on a success. On a success, the creature takes 14 (4d6) psychic damage and is immune to this effect for 24 hours.

**Unweave Ward.** *Ranged Spell Attack:* +9 to hit, range 60 ft., one magical barrier or warded area. *Hit:* The ward or barrier takes 22 (4d10) force damage. If this reduces a ward's strength to 0, the ward falls. This targets magical effects, not creatures.

### Legendary Actions

The Loom-Ghost can take 2 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. The Loom-Ghost regains spent legendary actions at the start of its turn.

- **Phase Shift (Costs 1 Action).** The Loom-Ghost teleports up to 30 feet to an unoccupied space it can see.
- **Memory Lash (Costs 1 Action).** One creature within 60 feet must succeed on a DC 17 Intelligence saving throw or take 10 (3d6) psychic damage.
- **Corrupted Song (Costs 2 Actions).** The Loom-Ghost uses Binding Fragment if available, or makes one Pattern Rend attack.

## 3. Thread-Sovereign (CR 12)

*Large aberration, lawful evil*

A Fray creature that has consumed enough pattern to develop something like intelligence. It remembers what it consumed — the habits, the hierarchies, the social structures. It doesn't just attack; it *commands*. Lesser Fray creatures obey it instinctively. It covets wards not to eat them but to *wear* them — using stolen protection to stabilize its own form and extend its consciousness.

**Armor Class** 17 (woven ward fragments)
**Hit Points** 189 (18d10 + 90)
**Speed** 30 ft., fly 30 ft. (hover)

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 20 (+5) | 14 (+2) | 20 (+5) | 16 (+3) | 14 (+2) | 18 (+4) |

**Saving Throws** CON +9, INT +7, CHA +8
**Skills** Deception +8, Insight +6, Intimidation +8, Perception +6
**Damage Resistances** force; bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing from nonmagical attacks
**Damage Immunities** psychic
**Condition Immunities** Charmed, Frightened, Poisoned
**Senses** Truesight 60 ft., passive Perception 16
**Languages** Common, Deep Speech, telepathy 120 ft.
**Challenge** 12 (8,400 XP) **Proficiency Bonus** +4

### Traits

**Fray-Born.** (See above.)

**Consumed Memories.** The Thread-Sovereign has absorbed the memories of those it consumed. It can mimic any voice it has heard and knows the surface thoughts of creatures it has consumed. It has advantage on Deception and Insight checks.

**Fray Commander.** All Fray creatures within 120 feet of the Thread-Sovereign act on its initiative and obey its telepathic commands (no action required). Fray creatures within this range have advantage on attack rolls while the Sovereign is conscious.

**Ward Mantle.** The Thread-Sovereign wears stolen ward fragments. It has resistance to radiant damage and advantage on saving throws against spells cast by Wardweavers or clerics. If the Sovereign takes 30 or more radiant damage in a single turn, the Ward Mantle cracks — it loses this trait until the end of its next turn.

**Legendary Resistance (3/Day).** If the Thread-Sovereign fails a saving throw, it can choose to succeed instead.

### Actions

**Multiattack.** The Thread-Sovereign makes three attacks: two Thread Lash and one Consume Pattern.

**Thread Lash.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +9 to hit, reach 15 ft., one target. *Hit:* 15 (3d6 + 5) slashing damage plus 7 (2d6) necrotic damage.

**Consume Pattern.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +9 to hit, reach 5 ft., one creature. *Hit:* 14 (2d8 + 5) necrotic damage, and the target must succeed on a DC 17 Constitution saving throw or gain one level of Fray Exposure (see Mechanics Companion §3). On a critical hit, the target gains two levels instead.

**Sovereignty Decree (Recharge 5–6).** The Thread-Sovereign issues a telepathic command that reverberates through the local weave. Each creature of its choice within 60 feet must make a DC 16 Wisdom saving throw. On a failure, the creature is Dominated (as *Dominate Person*) until the end of its next turn. Fray creatures in the area immediately take an extra action (as if under the effect of *Haste*).

### Reactions

**Unravel Spell.** When a creature within 60 feet casts a spell of 4th level or lower, the Thread-Sovereign can attempt to unravel it. The Sovereign makes an Intelligence check (DC 10 + the spell's level). On a success, the spell fails and the caster's spell slot is expended. On a failure, the spell resolves normally.

### Legendary Actions

The Thread-Sovereign can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. The Thread-Sovereign regains spent legendary actions at the start of its turn.

- **Command Fray (Costs 1 Action).** One Fray creature within 120 feet can move up to its speed or make one attack.
- **Thread Lash (Costs 1 Action).** The Thread-Sovereign makes one Thread Lash attack.
- **Consume Ward (Costs 2 Actions).** The Thread-Sovereign targets one magical effect or warded area within 30 feet. The effect takes 22 (4d10) force damage. If this destroys a ward, the Sovereign regains 22 hit points and its Ward Mantle reactivates if cracked.
- **Summon Fray (Costs 3 Actions).** The Thread-Sovereign summons 1d4 Looselings or 1 Thread-Puppet in unoccupied spaces within 30 feet. They act on the Sovereign's initiative.

## 4. The Unweaver (CR 15)

*Large aberration, neutral evil*

A being from the deepest Fray that actively and methodically unravels the Tapestry. Not chaotic — precise. It follows the Threadlines backward to the source, unpicking reality stitch by stitch. Where other Fray creatures consume or corrupt, the Unweaver *erases*. It is the main quest's recurring antagonist in Acts IV–V.

**Armor Class** 19 (woven void)
**Hit Points** 225 (18d10 + 126)
**Speed** 40 ft., fly 40 ft. (hover)

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 22 (+6) | 18 (+4) | 24 (+7) | 20 (+5) | 16 (+3) | 14 (+2) |

**Saving Throws** STR +11, DEX +9, CON +12, INT +10, WIS +8
**Skills** Arcana +10, Perception +8, Stealth +9
**Damage Resistances** bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing from nonmagical attacks
**Damage Immunities** force, necrotic, poison, psychic
**Condition Immunities** Charmed, Exhaustion, Frightened, Grappled, Paralyzed, Petrified, Poisoned
**Senses** Truesight 120 ft., passive Perception 18
**Languages** all, telepathy 120 ft.
**Challenge** 15 (13,000 XP) **Proficiency Bonus** +5

### Traits

**Fray-Born.** (See above.)

**Erasure Aura.** Nonmagical objects within 10 feet of the Unweaver slowly unravel. At the start of each of the Unweaver's turns, each creature within 10 feet takes 7 (2d6) force damage and must succeed on a DC 18 Constitution saving throw or have one nonmagical item they are carrying or wearing destroyed (DM's choice of a non-critical item — never weapons or armor first). Magical items are immune.

**Threadline Follower.** The Unweaver can see Threadlines as physical paths. It always knows the direction to the nearest Loom Stone, ward circle, or source of powerful weave magic within 1 mile. It cannot be surprised by creatures with active magical effects.

**Legendary Resistance (3/Day).** If the Unweaver fails a saving throw, it can choose to succeed instead.

**Magic Resistance.** The Unweaver has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects.

**Methodical Destruction.** The Unweaver's attacks ignore resistance to force damage. Creatures immune to force damage instead have resistance to the Unweaver's force damage.

### Actions

**Multiattack.** The Unweaver makes three Unraveling Touch attacks.

**Unraveling Touch.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +11 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. *Hit:* 17 (2d10 + 6) force damage plus 9 (2d8) necrotic damage. If the target is concentrating on a spell, the concentration DC for this damage is increased by 2.

**Erase Pattern (Recharge 5–6).** The Unweaver targets a 30-foot cube within 120 feet. Each creature in the area must make a DC 18 Constitution saving throw. On a failure, a creature takes 45 (10d8) force damage, and if it has spell slots, it loses its highest remaining spell slot. On a success, half damage and no spell slot loss. Nonmagical terrain features in the area (trees, rocks, structures) are destroyed. Ward circles in the area take 55 (10d10) force damage.

**Void Step.** As a bonus action, the Unweaver can teleport up to 60 feet to an unoccupied space it can see, leaving a 5-foot-radius area of unraveled terrain at its departure point. This area is difficult terrain and deals 7 (2d6) force damage to creatures entering it for the first time on a turn or starting their turn there. The area lasts for 1 minute.

### Reactions

**Unmaking.** When the Unweaver is hit by a spell attack or fails a saving throw against a spell, it can use its reaction to attempt to unravel the spell. It makes an Intelligence check (DC 10 + the spell's level). On a success, the spell is countered (as *Counterspell*) and the Unweaver regains 10 hit points per spell level countered.

### Legendary Actions

The Unweaver can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. The Unweaver regains spent legendary actions at the start of its turn.

- **Void Step (Costs 1 Action).** The Unweaver uses its Void Step ability.
- **Unraveling Touch (Costs 1 Action).** The Unweaver makes one Unraveling Touch attack.
- **Threadline Sever (Costs 2 Actions).** The Unweaver targets one creature within 60 feet that is under the effect of a spell or magical effect. The target must succeed on a DC 18 Charisma saving throw or have one ongoing magical effect (DM's choice) immediately end.
- **Cascade Unravel (Costs 3 Actions).** The Unweaver pulses destructive energy. Each creature within 30 feet must succeed on a DC 18 Dexterity saving throw or take 21 (6d6) force damage. Fray creatures in the area are healed for the same amount instead.

### Lair Actions

When fought at a location it has been actively unraveling for at least 1 hour, on initiative count 20 (losing ties):

- **Thread Collapse.** A 20-foot-radius area within 120 feet becomes unraveled terrain (difficult terrain, 2d6 force damage on entry/start of turn) until the end of the encounter.
- **Reality Echo.** An illusory duplicate of the Unweaver appears within 60 feet. It has AC 10, 1 HP, and disappears if hit. Until destroyed or until initiative count 20 of the next round, the duplicate can be targeted by attacks and spells (wasting them).
- **Pattern Drain.** Each creature in the lair with active spell effects must succeed on a DC 18 Charisma saving throw or have the duration of all their active spell effects reduced by 1 minute (minimum 1 round).

## 5. Void Loom (CR 17)

*Gargantuan aberration, chaotic evil*

Not a creature — a location-entity. A place where the Fray has begun weaving its *own* pattern, creating anti-reality that overwrites the Tapestry. The Void Loom is a 60-foot-radius zone of actively self-organizing Fray energy anchored to a physical nexus point (typically a corrupted Loom Stone). It is the endgame dungeon's core mechanic.

**Armor Class** 20 (woven anti-pattern)
**Hit Points** 280 (16d20 + 112)
**Speed** 0 ft. (immobile)

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 26 (+8) | 6 (-2) | 24 (+7) | 22 (+6) | 18 (+4) | 20 (+5) |

**Saving Throws** CON +13, INT +12, WIS +10, CHA +11
**Skills** Arcana +12, Perception +10
**Damage Resistances** bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing
**Damage Immunities** force, necrotic, poison, psychic; bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing from nonmagical attacks
**Condition Immunities** All conditions
**Senses** Truesight 120 ft., tremorsense 60 ft., passive Perception 20
**Languages** — (communicates through environmental changes)
**Challenge** 17 (18,000 XP) **Proficiency Bonus** +6

### Traits

**Fray-Born.** (See above.)

**Immobile.** The Void Loom cannot move or be moved. It occupies a 60-foot-radius spherical zone centered on its nexus point.

**Anti-Pattern Zone.** The Void Loom's 60-foot zone is an area of anti-reality. Within this zone:
- All divination magic automatically fails.
- Teleportation is unreliable — any creature teleporting within the zone must succeed on a DC 19 Intelligence saving throw or arrive at a random location within the zone instead.
- The zone counts as Unraveled weave state for all mechanical purposes.
- Nonmagical light sources are extinguished. Magical light is reduced to half range.

**Pattern Overwrite.** At the start of each of the Void Loom's turns, it overwrites a 10-foot-radius area of terrain within its zone, permanently converting it to anti-pattern terrain. Creatures standing in overwritten terrain take 14 (4d6) force damage at the start of each of their turns (no save). Only the Binding Song, *Wish*, or destroying the Void Loom can restore overwritten terrain.

**Legendary Resistance (3/Day).** If the Void Loom fails a saving throw, it can choose to succeed instead.

**Magic Resistance.** The Void Loom has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects.

**Nexus Vulnerability.** The Void Loom's nexus point (a corrupted Loom Stone at its center) has AC 25 and 80 HP, separate from the Void Loom's main HP. If the nexus is destroyed, the Void Loom's Anti-Pattern Zone shrinks by 20 feet per round until it collapses entirely. Attacks against the nexus must be made from within 10 feet of it (deep inside the zone). The Binding Song performed within 30 feet of the nexus deals 55 (10d10) radiant damage to the Void Loom per successful skill challenge round.

### Actions

**Multiattack.** The Void Loom uses Fray Tendril three times and Spawn Fray once.

**Fray Tendril.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +14 to hit, reach 60 ft., one target. *Hit:* 22 (4d6 + 8) force damage plus 9 (2d8) necrotic damage. On a hit, the target must succeed on a DC 19 Strength saving throw or be pulled 20 feet toward the nexus point.

**Spawn Fray.** The Void Loom creates 1d4 Looselings, or 1 Thread-Eater, or 1 Hollow Walker in unoccupied spaces within its zone. Creatures spawned this way act on initiative count 10 and obey the Void Loom's will.

**Reality Inversion (Recharge 6).** The Void Loom inverts reality within a 30-foot-radius sphere centered on a point within its zone. For 1 round, within this sphere: gravity reverses (creatures fall 30 feet upward, 3d6 bludgeoning), healing spells deal damage and damage spells heal the Void Loom, and all saving throws are made with disadvantage. Each affected creature makes a DC 19 Wisdom saving throw; on a success, the creature is unaffected by the gravity reversal (but other inversions still apply).

### Legendary Actions

The Void Loom can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. The Void Loom regains spent legendary actions at the start of its turn.

- **Fray Tendril (Costs 1 Action).** The Void Loom makes one Fray Tendril attack.
- **Pattern Pulse (Costs 2 Actions).** Each creature within the 60-foot zone must succeed on a DC 19 Constitution saving throw or gain one level of Fray Exposure.
- **Void Surge (Costs 3 Actions).** The anti-pattern terrain expands by 15 feet from its current edge. All creatures in the newly overwritten area take 28 (8d6) force damage (DC 19 DEX save for half) and are pushed to the nearest edge of the overwritten area.

### Lair Actions

On initiative count 20 (losing ties):

- **Tendril Cage.** Up to 3 creatures within the zone must succeed on a DC 19 Dexterity saving throw or be restrained by Fray tendrils until the end of their next turn.
- **Zone Shift.** The Void Loom shifts the geography within its zone — corridors rotate 90 degrees, walls swap places, doors seal and new ones open. Creatures in the zone must succeed on a DC 19 Wisdom saving throw or become disoriented (disadvantage on attack rolls until the end of their next turn).
- **Absorption.** The Void Loom absorbs all magical effects in a 20-foot-radius area within its zone. All ongoing spell effects in the area end. The Void Loom regains 10 HP per spell level absorbed.

### CR Verification per 2024 Free Rules DMG

Offensive and defensive CR calculations for each creature:

**Weft Spider (CR 8):**
- Defensive CR: 136 HP → CR 4; AC 16 → +1 step → CR 5. With lair actions (effective HP increase from mobility), adjusted to ~CR 6.
- Offensive CR: ~40 DPR (2 × Threadline Fang avg 22 each) → CR 8. With Redirect Spell disruption → CR 9.
- Average: (6 + 9) / 2 ≈ CR 8 ✓

**Loom-Ghost (CR 10):**
- Defensive CR: 150 HP → CR 5; effective HP with resistances/immunities ×2 → 300 → CR 10. Legendary Resistance → +1 step → CR 11.
- Offensive CR: ~36 DPR (2 × Pattern Rend avg 18) → CR 7. Binding Fragment (avg 14 to all, incapacitate) → effective CR 10.
- Average: (11 + 10) / 2 ≈ CR 10 ✓

**Thread-Sovereign (CR 12):**
- Defensive CR: 189 HP → CR 7; effective HP with resistances ×1.5 → ~284 → CR 10. Legendary Resistance ×3 → +2 → CR 12.
- Offensive CR: ~44 DPR (2 × Thread Lash 22 avg each) + Consume Pattern 14 → ~58 DPR → CR 10. Sovereignty Decree + Fray Commander → effective CR 12.
- Average: (12 + 12) / 2 = CR 12 ✓

**The Unweaver (CR 15):**
- Defensive CR: 225 HP → CR 8; effective HP with immunities/resistances ×2 → 450 → CR 15. Magic Resistance + Legendary Resistance ×3 → +2 → CR 17. Adjustment: -1 for mobility reliance → CR 16.
- Offensive CR: ~78 DPR (3 × Unraveling Touch avg 26 each) → CR 13. Erase Pattern (avg 45 AoE + spell slot burn) → effective CR 15.
- Average: (16 + 15) / 2 ≈ CR 15 ✓

**Void Loom (CR 17):**
- Defensive CR: 280 HP → CR 10; effective HP with immunities ×2 → 560 → CR 17. Condition immunity + Legendary Resistance ×3 + immobile (disadvantage: easier to hit) → net CR 17.
- Offensive CR: ~93 DPR (3 × Fray Tendril avg 31 each) + Pattern Overwrite (~14 auto) → ~107 DPR → CR 17. Reality Inversion → effective CR 18.
- Average: (17 + 18) / 2 ≈ CR 17 ✓

## RAW Deviations

| Deviation | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|
| Void Loom is immobile with 60-ft zone | Location-entity design; combat is about *entering* the zone, not chasing the creature. Parallels D&D's approach to legendary creatures with lair actions. |
| The Unweaver's Methodical Destruction ignores force resistance | Required for narrative — the Unweaver must be able to unmake Fray-Born creatures and ward effects that rely on force resistance. Without this, it can't fulfill its role as the Fray's apex predator. |
| Thread-Sovereign's Sovereignty Decree is mass Dominate | Limited to 1 round (vs. Dominate Person's 1 minute). Balances the mass targeting by reducing duration dramatically. |
| Loom-Ghost's Pattern Rend expends spell slots | Extends the resource-drain mechanic from Thread-Eater's Consume Ward. Reflects the Fray's pattern-consuming nature. Not a standard 2024 Free Rules effect but consistent with the Fray's established mechanics. |
| Weft Spider's Redirect Spell has no action cost | It uses the spider's reaction; it can only redirect one spell per round. |
