# Player Threadcraft — Narrative Flavor

*Companion document to `player_threadcraft.md` (mechanical system)*
*This file tells the LLM **how Threadcraft looks, sounds, and feels** when cast — per tradition, per tier, and per risk level.*
*No pre-written prose blocks. Sensory vocabulary for the narrative engine to render live.*

---

## 1. Purpose

Standard D&D spellcasting — incantations, gestures, arcane foci — is someone else's magic layered on top of the world. Threadcraft is the world's own language. When a player casts Threadcraft, the narration must feel fundamentally different from a Wizard casting Fireball.

**Core principle:** Threadcraft is physical. It's felt in the body before it's seen in the world. The caster is not commanding reality — they are *participating in it*.

---

## 2. Universal Threadcraft Sensory Vocabulary

These apply to ALL traditions. The tradition-specific sections below add flavor on top.

### What the Caster Feels

| Tier | Sensation |
|------|-----------|
| **Initiate** | A tug behind the sternum, like something pulling tight. Fingertips tingle. The air feels thicker, like wading through warm water. Mild vertigo — the ground is still but your balance says it isn't. |
| **Adept** | The tug becomes a hum — a vibration that runs from your chest to your hands. You can feel the Threadlines before you see them. Your breath syncs to something. Colors shift at the edges of your vision. |
| **Master** | The distinction between you and the weave blurs momentarily. You know where the threads are without looking. Your heartbeat and the weave's rhythm overlap. For a moment, you don't end where the world begins. |

### What Observers See

| Tier | Visual |
|------|--------|
| **Initiate** | A faint shimmer around the caster's hands — like heat haze, barely visible. Threads of light might flicker for a split second. Most observers wouldn't notice unless watching closely. |
| **Adept** | Visible threads of pale light extend from the caster's fingertips or palms, reaching into the air like spider silk. The threads connect to something unseen and pull taut before dissolving. Observers feel a static charge. |
| **Master** | The caster's outline wavers. Threads of light wrap around them like a second skeleton. For a heartbeat, you can see the weave through them — the Threadlines that hold the world together, visible through the caster's body as if they were made of glass. Then it's gone. |

### What Everyone Hears

Not incantations. Not words of power. Threadcraft sounds like:

- A low hum, below speech — felt in the jaw more than heard in the ears
- The sound of thread being pulled taut (a thin, singing tension)
- A faint resonance, like a tuning fork pressed to wood
- At higher tiers: harmony — multiple tones that shouldn't come from one person

### What Nearby Threadlines Do

If there are visible Threadlines (thin/frayed zones, or with Weave Sight active):
- **Initiate casting:** Threadlines near the caster vibrate faintly, like plucked strings
- **Adept casting:** Threadlines lean toward the caster, drawn by the manipulation
- **Master casting:** Threadlines brighten, some re-aligning briefly — the weave *responds*

## 3. Tradition-Specific Flavor

### Wardweaving — "The Shield That Remembers"

**Core sensation:** Heaviness. Solidity. Wardweaving feels like becoming part of a wall. The magic doesn't flow — it *sets*, like mortar.

**Casting gestures:** Pressing palms flat against the air. Pushing something invisible into place. The hands move as if smoothing plaster or pressing clay into a mold. Fingers spread wide.

**Visual signature:** A brief grid pattern appears around the target — hexagonal, golden, faintly luminous. It flashes once and fades, leaving behind an invisible structure. Dust motes settle in the pattern's wake.

**Sound:** A low, resonant thud — like a heavy door closing firmly. Then silence. The silence *is* the ward.

**How it feels by spell:**
| Spell | Narrative Flavor |
|-------|-----------------|
| *Mending* | The caster presses broken edges together. The fracture warms. The material remembers what it was and becomes it again. |
| *Shield of Faith* | A golden lattice snaps into place around the target. They feel heavier — grounded. The ward-circle of a settlement, miniaturized to one person. |
| *Warding Bond* | A thread of light connects the caster to the target. When one is struck, the other feels the echo. Two patterns, linked. |
| *Banishment* | The caster presses both hands forward and *pushes*. The ward doesn't attack — it excludes. The target is walled out of reality. |

**Fray exposure flavor (Wardweaving):** The solidity cracks. The ward-feeling inverts — instead of heaviness, sudden lightness. The caster's hands shake. The world feels thin underfoot, like standing on ice over deep water.

---

### Threadwalking — "The Path That Finds Itself"

**Core sensation:** Movement. Direction. Threadwalking feels like the world is turning to face you — aligning, showing you the way. A compass in your bones.

**Casting gestures:** One hand extended, palm down, fingers trailing — as if touching the surface of flowing water. Walking gestures, even while standing still. The feet shift, finding purchase on something that isn't the ground.

**Visual signature:** Faint paths of light appear on the ground around the caster, like luminous footprints leading in multiple directions. They flicker and resolve into the correct path, then fade. The caster's shadow moves independently for a moment.

**Sound:** A whisper that sounds like wind through leaves, even indoors. The creak of a trail underfoot. A distant birdcall that seems to mark direction.

**How it feels by spell:**
| Spell | Narrative Flavor |
|-------|-----------------|
| *Guidance* | The caster touches the target. For a moment, both of them see the same path — the correct action illuminated like a trail marker. |
| *Detect Magic* | The world turns transparent for a heartbeat. The caster sees the weave beneath the surface — Threadlines like veins in a leaf, magic like knots in the pattern. |
| *Misty Step* | The caster takes a step in one place and arrives in another. Between start and finish: a flash of the Threadline network, seen from inside. The taste of ozone. |
| *Dimension Door* | The caster walks through a door that wasn't there. On the other side: a single step through the weave itself — raw pattern, luminous and vast. Then you're somewhere else, and the door is gone. |

**Fray exposure flavor (Threadwalking):** The compass breaks. Every direction pulls equally. The caster feels lost even in familiar places. The sense of movement continues after they stop — vertigo that says the ground is moving and you're standing still.

### Binding — "The Song That Holds"

**Core sensation:** Community. Resonance. Binding magic feels like singing a note that others join. The caster's voice becomes part of something larger — a chord that existed before them and will exist after.

**Casting gestures:** Mouth open, throat engaged even for non-verbal components. Hands move like a conductor — shaping the air, not the weave. The caster sways. Binding is rhythmic; the body can't stay still.

**Visual signature:** Ripples in the air, like sound waves made visible. The ripples spread outward from the caster and touch everyone nearby. For a moment, all affected creatures seem to breathe together. Faint golden threads briefly connect them at the chest.

**Sound:** Song. Always song. The caster may not be singing but the magic sounds like it — a harmony that comes from nowhere, an undertone that makes the back of your neck prickle. The Binding Song is the world's first music. Every Binding spell is an echo of it.

**How it feels by spell:**
| Spell | Narrative Flavor |
|-------|-----------------|
| *Bless* | The caster hums a single note. Others feel it in their ribs. For the next few heartbeats, they feel like part of something larger — more confident, more certain. The note sustains at the edge of hearing. |
| *Heroism* | The caster sings — wordless, three ascending notes. Fear burns away. The target stands taller. They can hear the echo of a song that was first sung by the Weavers themselves. |
| *Calm Emotions* | The caster exhales a held note. The room quiets. Rage dissolves like tension leaving a held string. Everyone in range feels the collective exhale. |
| *Hypnotic Pattern* | The caster's voice splits into harmonics no throat should produce. The sound has geometry — visible, shimmering, beautiful. Looking at it is listening. Listening is looking. The boundary dissolves. |

**Fray exposure flavor (Binding):** The harmony breaks. The caster hears dissonance — their voice sounds wrong, out of tune with everything. Other people's breathing becomes grating. The sense of community inverts into isolation. They feel alone in a room full of people.

### Remembering — "The Thread That Was"

**Core sensation:** Time. Memory. Remembering magic feels like turning to look at something that already happened — the world's memory surfacing through the caster. Not the caster remembering, but the *weave* remembering through them.

**Casting gestures:** One hand pressed to the forehead or temple. Eyes closed, then opened wide — but focused on something nobody else can see. Fingers trace invisible lines in the air, as if reading text that isn't there. The posture is scholarly — leaning forward, concentrating.

**Visual signature:** The caster's eyes briefly flicker with pale light — not glowing, but reflective, as if lit from behind by something distant. Writing-like patterns shimmer in the air around their hands, appearing and fading too quickly to read. Old objects near the caster seem to brighten momentarily.

**Sound:** Pages turning. The scratch of a quill. A voice speaking in a language just below comprehension — you almost catch the words but they slide away. The creak of very old wood.

**How it feels by spell:**
| Spell | Narrative Flavor |
|-------|-----------------|
| *Identify* | The caster holds the object and closes their eyes. The object's history floods in — who made it, why, what it was meant to do. The caster sees it being crafted, briefly, through someone else's hands. |
| *Comprehend Languages* | The caster's ears ring once, sharply. Then language resolves — not translation, but understanding. The words carry their history: dialects, regional shifts, the way the speaker's grandmother said the same phrase. |
| *Speak with Dead* | The caster reaches into the weave's memory and finds the deceased's pattern — an imprint, not a soul. The dead speak as they last spoke; the words are faded, threadbare, like text that has been copied too many times. |
| *Arcane Eye* | The caster pushes a point of awareness out of their body. The world seen through the eye is overlaid with pattern — every surface shows its age, its history, its connections to other things. |

**Fray exposure flavor (Remembering):** Memories intrude uninvited. The caster sees things that happened in this place — not their memories, the weave's. Flickers of people who stood here centuries ago. The present becomes uncertain. "When am I?" The disorientation of experiencing time as the weave does — all of it at once.

### Fraytouch — "The Thread That Isn't"

**Core sensation:** Absence. Entropy. Fraytouch feels like reaching into the gap where the weave should be and pulling the nothing back with you. The magic doesn't come from the caster or the world — it comes from what's *between* them.

**Casting gestures:** Hands curl inward, fingers closing as if catching smoke. The gestures are involuntary — the hands move and the caster follows. Fraytouch casters often look surprised by their own magic. The body temperature drops. Breath fogs.

**Visual signature:** A momentary hole in the visual field — not darkness, but *absence*. Like a pixel missing from reality. The edges around the effect warp slightly, as if reality is rushing to fill the gap. Where the effect touches, color drains briefly.

**Sound:** Silence. Not quiet — *silence*. The sound of something being unmade. A negative sound, where you hear what was there a moment ago *stop*. Then a rush of ambient noise filling the space back in, slightly wrong.

**How it feels by spell:**
| Spell | Narrative Flavor |
|-------|-----------------|
| *Chill Touch* | A hand that is not a hand reaches from the space between the caster's fingers. It's cold. It's the absence of warmth, weaponized. Where it touches, the target feels their own pattern fray — briefly, terribly, they understand what the Fray is. |
| *Inflict Wounds* | The caster presses their palm against the target and *takes*. They pull the target's pattern apart at the contact point. The wound is not cut or crushed — it's unraveled. Flesh forgets how to be flesh. |
| *Bane* | The caster exhales and the air around the targets goes flat. Colors dim. Confidence drains. The targets feel the certainty of failure — not magically imposed, but revealed, as if the Fray peeled back the comforting lie that they could succeed. |
| *Blight* | The caster reaches into the target and accelerates entropy. The target ages — not in years, but in pattern integrity. Living things wilt. Objects crack. The damage looks like centuries compressed into seconds. |

**Fraytouch Fray exposure flavor:** The nothing follows you back. The caster feels hollow — not hungry, not tired, *empty*. Their own pattern feels thin. They catch themselves looking at their hands and seeing through them for a moment. The Fray doesn't punish — it invites. "Come apart. It's easier."

**WARNING — LLM narration note:** Fraytouch is the most dangerous tradition narratively. The narration should convey that this magic is *not evil* but is *fundamentally risky*. The Fray is not malicious — it's the absence of structure. Using it is like handling antimatter. The danger is in what you become, not in what you do.

### Threadline Reading — "The Thread That Sees"

**Core sensation:** Clarity. Perception. Threadline Reading feels like adjusting the focus on a lens — the world doesn't change, but you suddenly see what was always there. The caster's awareness expands sideways.

**Casting gestures:** Two fingers pressed to the temple, then extended outward — pointing, tracing, tracking. The eyes move independently from the head, following things others can't see. Threadline Readers often stand very still while casting, their body frozen as their perception flies.

**Visual signature:** The caster's eyes develop a faint luminous quality — pale gold or silver, like lamplight reflected from far away. Threadlines in the area become faintly visible to observers for a moment, responding to being observed. The caster's pupils dilate fully.

**Sound:** A high, clear tone — the sound a crystal glass makes when circled with a wet finger. The pitch rises as the caster's awareness expands. At higher tiers, observers hear the Threadlines themselves: a complex, shimmering chord, like wind through a vast stringed instrument.

**How it feels by spell:**
| Spell | Narrative Flavor |
|-------|-----------------|
| *Light* | The caster finds the Threadline nearest to the target and plucks it. The vibration becomes visible light — not created, but *revealed*. The weave was already luminous; the caster just showed it. |
| *Faerie Fire* | The caster traces Threadlines to their anchor points — objects, creatures, anything with pattern. The traced patterns glow. Nothing is truly invisible; everything is woven, and woven things can be followed. |
| *See Invisibility* | The caster's perception drops below the surface layer. They see the weave directly — and in the weave, everything has a pattern. Invisibility is a surface trick. Pattern is truth. |
| *True Seeing* | The caster sees the world as the Weavers saw it. Every illusion dissolves. Every glamour falls. Every hidden thing is simply... there. The experience is overwhelming — the caster often gasps or stumbles. Reality is more complex than mortals normally process. |

**Fray exposure flavor (Threadline Reading):** The clarity won't stop. The caster can't un-see the weave. Perception becomes overloaded — every surface shows its pattern structure, every sound reveals its harmonic composition, every person is a knot of threads rather than a face. The caster needs to close their eyes and *still sees*.

## 4. The Fray Risk Arc — Narrative Progression

As the Fray Risk DC climbs within a single day, the narration should escalate:

| Cast # | Fray Risk Flavor |
|--------|-----------------|
| **1st** | Clean. The casting feels natural. A hum, a shimmer, done. The caster feels briefly exhilarated. |
| **2nd** | Still clean, but there's an afterimage — a faint tingling that didn't fade as fast. The caster blinks and the world re-settles. |
| **3rd** | The tug is harder. The Threadlines respond more slowly, as if reluctant. The caster's vision blurs at the edges for a moment. Their hands shake once. |
| **4th** | Something pushes back. The casting feels like it's being drawn from the caster rather than channeled through them. A brief chill. The taste of metal. "That one cost something." |
| **5th** | The boundary between the caster and the weave thins. For a moment during the casting, they feel the Fray — not as danger, but as invitation. The tug is in both directions now. The hum doesn't stop immediately. |
| **6th+** | The caster is fraying themselves. The narration should include: hands translucent for a moment, heartbeat audible to others, the sense of being *less solid* than the world around them. The casting still works. The cost is visible. |

### If Fray Exposure Is Gained

The LLM should narrate the moment of failure:

| Level Gained | Narration |
|--------------|-----------|
| **Level 1** | "Something shifts. For a heartbeat, the colors are wrong — oversaturated, then faded. The world wobbles and steadies. You feel fine. You tell yourself you feel fine." |
| **Level 2** | "The world develops a grain, like looking through gauze. Sounds are slightly muffled. People's voices come from a fraction of a second in the past. You can function. But the weave is letting you know it noticed." |
| **Level 3** | "Your skin prickles with static that doesn't discharge. When you touch metal, it sparks. The air pressure feels wrong — too much, then too little. Force damage finds you easily now. The weave has opened a seam." |
| **Level 4** | "Your legs are heavy. Not tired — heavy, as if the ground is pulling at you specifically. Movement requires intention. Your shadow doesn't quite match your movements anymore. It trails." |
| **Level 5** | "Everything is hard. Not physically — ontologically. Decisions feel enormous. Actions feel consequential. The world presses in. This is what the Frayed Ones feel. You understand them now." |
| **Level 6** | "You can see through your hand if you hold it up to the light. Not metaphorically. The pattern that is your body is thinning. Your heartbeat sounds like it's coming from the next room. Half of everything you were is *somewhere else*." |
| **Level 7** | "You don't feel the moment it happens. You were here. You were a pattern. The pattern unravels. The last thing that was you dissolves into the Threadlines — a final, quiet vibration. Somewhere, a Threadline hums a note it didn't have before. It sounds like your name." |

## 5. The Speaker — Narrative Apex

The Binding Song (Harmony path, Speaker feat) is the narrative climax of Threadcraft progression. It should be narrated as something qualitatively different from all other Threadcraft:

**It doesn't feel like casting.**

The Speaker doesn't reach for the weave. The weave reaches for them. The Song begins before the Singer opens their mouth — the Threadlines lean in, brighten, and begin to resonate. The Singer adds their voice and the resonance becomes intentional. Communication. Not manipulation, not channeling — *conversation*.

**What the Singer hears:** The Weavers. Not words — concepts. Age-deep impressions of patience, concern, curiosity. They have been waiting. They have been listening. "Finally" is the first thing they say, and it comes as a warmth in the chest, not a sound.

**What observers see:** The Threadlines become fully visible within the Song's radius. They pulse in rhythm with the Singer's voice. The ward effect is visible — a dome of woven light, intricate and ancient, that encompasses the area. Inside the dome, the weave is perfect. There is no thinning, no fraying, no age. For the duration of the Song, this is the world as it was 1,200 years ago.

**What observers feel:** Safety. The bone-deep, primal safety of a warded settlement, intensified a hundredfold. The fear that has been a constant companion since Act I — the low-grade awareness that the world is coming apart — goes silent. Some observers cry. Some laugh. Some can't explain why they do either.

**No Fray Risk.** The Song is not fraying. It is the opposite of fraying. The Singer is not taking from the weave — they are *answering* it. This is Threadcraft as the Weavers intended it: collaboration, not extraction.

## 6. Prompt Builder Integration

When the player casts a Threadcraft spell, inject this context:

```
THREADCRAFT CASTING CONTEXT:
- Tradition: [tradition_name]
- Tier: [Initiate/Adept/Master/Speaker]
- Spell: [spell_name]
- Casts today (before this one): [number]
- Current Fray Exposure level: [0-6]
- Weave state at location: [stable/thin/frayed/unraveled]

NARRATION RULES:
- Threadcraft does NOT look like standard D&D spellcasting — no incantations, no arcane symbols, no spell components theater
- Threadcraft is PHYSICAL — felt in the body, seen as thread/weave manifestations
- The narration should reflect the tradition's flavor (see tradition sections)
- The Fray Risk escalation should be narratively visible (see cast # progression)
- If Fray Exposure is gained, narrate the moment using the level descriptions
- In thin/frayed zones, Threadcraft effects are VISUALLY amplified — the weave responds more dramatically in damaged areas
- In unraveled zones, casting feels like shouting into a void — the effect still works but the feedback is wrong, delayed, distorted
- NEVER describe Threadcraft as "magic" in narration — it is weaving, singing, reading, touching, warding
```
