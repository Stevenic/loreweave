# Threadcraft Item Crafting Rules

*Addendum to the Threadhallow Mechanics Companion*
*Based on 2024 Free Rules crafting framework (Equipment Chapter, "Crafting" section) with Threadhallow extensions*
*Wren Looseweft's Thread-Iron forging is the narrative anchor for this system*

---

## 1. Overview

Threadcraft crafting creates items that interact with the weave — wards, pattern-infused weapons, Thread-Iron tools, and Fray-resistant gear. It extends the 2024 Free Rules crafting system with two additions:

1. **Thread-Iron** as a material type (requires Fray-sourced ore + Threadcraft to forge)
2. **Pattern Infusion** as a crafting method (imbuing items with weave-resonance)

---

## 2. Materials

### Thread-Iron

Thread-Iron is metal forged from ore harvested in Frayed zones and tempered with Threadcraft. It's the physical intersection of matter and pattern — metal that can touch the weave.

**Harvesting:**
- Found in Frayed or Unraveled zones only
- Prospecting: Survival DC 14 in Frayed zones, DC 12 in Unraveled zones (ore is more common but the zone is more dangerous)
- Yield: 1d4 units of raw Fray ore per successful prospecting (4 hours of work)
- Fray Exposure: CON DC 13 save per hour of harvesting in Frayed zone, DC 15 in Unraveled

**Smelting:**
- Requires a forge + Threadcraft proficiency (any tradition, Tier 1 minimum)
- Smith's Tools check DC 15 to smelt raw ore into Thread-Iron ingots
- 2 units of raw ore → 1 Thread-Iron ingot
- Smelting time: 8 hours per ingot
- Fray Risk: DC 12 CON save per smelting session (the Fray energy in the ore resists being shaped)

### Threadline Silk

Harvested from Threadlines by Threadwalkers. Used for light armor, clothing, and enchantment binding.

- Harvesting: Arcana DC 14 while in a Thin or Stable weave zone
- Yield: 1d6 yards per successful harvest (2 hours)
- Cannot be harvested in Frayed/Unraveled zones (Threadlines are too unstable)

### Ward Crystals

Crystallized ward energy from active ward circles. Used for protective items.

- Harvesting: requires Wardweaving tradition (Tier 2+), and permission from the ward's community
- Arcana DC 13 to extract without damaging the ward
- Yield: 1d4 crystals per harvest. Each extraction reduces ward strength by 2.
- Recovery: the ward regenerates the lost strength at its normal rate

### Fray Essence

Raw Fray energy captured in a container. Used for offensive items and Fraytouch crafting.

- Harvesting: requires Fraytouch tradition (any tier), in a Frayed or Unraveled zone
- Arcana DC 15 to capture safely
- Yield: 1 vial per harvest
- Failure: 1 level Fray Exposure (no save — direct contact with raw Fray)
- Storage: Fray Essence degrades. Loses potency after 7 days unless stored in a Thread-Iron container.

## 3. Crafting Process

### Base Framework (per 2024 Free Rules)

1. **Tools required:** Smith's Tools, Weaver's Tools, or Tinker's Tools (depending on item)
2. **Proficiency required:** Must be proficient in the required tools AND have at least Threadcraft Initiate feat
3. **Time:** Varies by item complexity (see table)
4. **Cost:** Materials + 50% of the item's base value in incidental supplies
5. **Check:** Tool proficiency check at the item's crafting DC

### Threadcraft Extension

In addition to the base check, Threadcraft items require:

1. **Pattern Infusion Check:** Arcana check using Constitution modifier (per Threadcraft casting rules). DC varies by item.
2. **Fray Risk:** CON save at the standard Threadcraft Fray Risk DC (base 10, escalating per cast that day). Crafting counts as 1 Threadcraft cast per hour of work.
3. **Weave State Requirement:** Some items can only be crafted in specific weave states.

## 5. Failure Consequences

### Tool Check Failure

| Margin of Failure | Consequence |
|-------------------|-------------|
| 1–4 below DC | Materials partially consumed (50%). Item is flawed — functions but breaks after 1d6 uses. |
| 5–9 below DC | Materials fully consumed. No item produced. |
| 10+ below DC | Materials consumed + backfire. Fray energy released: DC 14 CON save or 1 level Fray Exposure. |

### Infusion Check Failure

| Margin of Failure | Consequence |
|-------------------|-------------|
| 1–4 below DC | Item is mundane — no Threadcraft properties, but it's still a functional non-magical item. |
| 5–9 below DC | Item is unstable — Threadcraft properties work but with a 25% chance of failure per use (roll d4: on a 1, the effect doesn't trigger). |
| 10+ below DC | Item is Fray-tainted. It functions, but each use requires a DC 12 CON save or 1 level Fray Exposure for the user. |

### Critical Success (Natural 20)

- Item gains a minor bonus property at DM's discretion (e.g., Thread-Iron weapon becomes +2 instead of +1, Ward Stone lasts 48 hours instead of 24)
- Thread-Iron items crafted on a natural 20 have a faint hum — they resonate with the nearest Loom Stone

## 6. Economy and Balance

### Material Scarcity

Thread-Iron and other Threadcraft materials are deliberately scarce:
- Thread-Iron ore requires Frayed/Unraveled zone access (dangerous)
- Ward Crystals require community permission (social cost)
- Threadline Silk can only be harvested in safe zones (time cost — must travel to stable/thin areas)
- Fray Essence degrades in 7 days without Thread-Iron storage (logistics cost)

A player focused on crafting will spend significant game time acquiring materials. This is intentional — Threadcraft items should feel earned, not mass-produced.

### Comparison to Standard Magic Items

| Threadcraft Item | Closest RAW Equivalent | Advantage | Disadvantage |
|-----------------|----------------------|-----------|--------------|
| Thread-Iron Weapon (+1) | +1 Weapon (uncommon) | Bonus force damage vs Fray creatures | Requires Threadcraft feat + crafting investment |
| Threadline Silk Armor | Studded Leather +1 | Fray Exposure advantage | Lower base AC, requires Threadcraft feat |
| Ward Anchor | Portable Hole (sort of) | Creates safe rest zone | Small radius, requires recharging |
| Fray Bomb | Bead of Force | Cheaper, craftable | Single use, lower damage, force only |

Threadcraft items are **situationally superior** (against Fray creatures, in Frayed zones) but **generally equal or slightly inferior** to standard magic items of the same rarity. They shine in Threadhallow's specific challenges.

## 7. Engine Implementation

### Data Model

```
ThreadcraftItem {
    id: string;
    name: string;
    tier: 1 | 2 | 3;
    materials: MaterialRequirement[];
    craftingTime: number;           // hours
    toolDC: number;
    infusionDC: number;
    requiredWeaveState: WeaveState[];
    requiredTradition?: ThreadcraftTradition;  // some items need specific tradition
    effect: ItemEffect;
    isConsumable: boolean;
    durability?: number;            // uses before breaking (flawed items only)
}

MaterialRequirement {
    material: 'thread_iron_ingot' | 'ward_crystal' | 'threadline_silk' | 'fray_essence' | 'gold';
    quantity: number;
}
```

### Pure Functions (for Beacon)

```
canCraft(player: PlayerThreadcraft, item: ThreadcraftItem, weaveState: WeaveState): boolean
attemptCraft(player: PlayerThreadcraft, item: ThreadcraftItem, toolRoll: number, infusionRoll: number): CraftResult
    // Returns: success | flawed | failed | backfire | critical_success
    // Updates Fray Risk counter (1 per hour of crafting time)
getAvailableRecipes(player: PlayerThreadcraft): ThreadcraftItem[]
```

## 8. RAW Deviations

| Deviation | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|
| Thread-Iron as material type | No RAW equivalent. Mithral and Adamantine are the closest analogues. Thread-Iron occupies a similar niche (special material with unique properties) but is world-specific and craftable rather than found. |
| Pattern Infusion as second check | The 2024 FR crafting system uses a single tool check. Adding a second (Infusion) check creates the Threadcraft risk/reward tension — you can be a great smith and still fail the magical component. |
| Ward Crystals reducing ward strength | Creates a meaningful cost for crafting — you're borrowing from community protection. This ties crafting into the ward maintenance economy. |
| Fray Essence degradation | Prevents stockpiling. Forces players to plan crafting sessions around material availability. Creates logistical gameplay. |
| Wren's companion-quest-locked items | Gating powerful utility items behind companion engagement. Rewards relationship investment. |
