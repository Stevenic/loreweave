## Act II: The Threadwalker's Path (Levels 5–8)

### Encounter 1: The Drowned Ward

**Location:** Ashenmoor — Reedmere ruins
**Party Level:** 5–6 (recommended: 5)

**Creatures:**
| Creature | CR | XP | Count | Source |
|----------|-----|------|-------|--------|
| Thread-Puppet | 1 | 200 | 4 | Mechanics Companion §8 |
| Frayed One | 2 | 450 | 1 | Mechanics Companion §8 |

**XP Budget:** 1,250 XP total (800 Thread-Puppets + 450 Frayed One)
**Difficulty:** Hard for 1 PC + 1 companion (effective party 1.5); Medium for 1 PC + 2 companions (effective party 2.0)

**Terrain Mechanics:**
- Difficult terrain: standing water (half movement unless Athletics DC 12)
- Partial cover (+2 AC) from ruined walls
- Ward-circle stones: creatures within 5 ft. have advantage on Arcana checks
- Visibility: 60 ft. (fog, per 2024 FR Lightly Obscured — disadvantage on Perception checks relying on sight beyond 60 ft.)

**Action Economy Analysis:**
- Round 1: 4 Thread-Puppets engage (Slam +3, 1d6+1). Frayed One begins ward activation loop (not hostile yet).
- Round 2: If PC engages Frayed One, she attacks (Fraying Touch, DC 13 CON save or 1 level Fray Exposure). Thread-Puppets continue.
- Round 3: If Frayed One is not engaged, she resets loop. She does not pursue.

**Non-Combat Resolution:**
- Arcana DC 16 to complete the ward activation Liss is attempting
- OR Performance DC 14 (Binding Song) — advantage if Sorrel assists
- Success pacifies the Frayed One (no XP, but ward activates for 24 hours — suppresses Fray in 500-ft. radius)

**Tactics:**
- Thread-Puppets shamble through shallows, prioritize nearest target, no tactical intelligence
- The Frayed One fights only in self-defense. She uses Fraying Touch (reach 5 ft.) and Thread Wail (30-ft. cone, DC 13 WIS save, Frightened 1 round) if surrounded
- If reduced to half HP, the Frayed One attempts to flee toward the ward-circle stones

### Encounter 2: Singing Cairn — Forest Anchor

**Location:** Dense old-growth forest south of the Cairn
**Party Level:** 5–6

**Creatures:**
| Creature | CR | XP | Count | Source |
|----------|-----|------|-------|--------|
| Looseling | 1/4 | 50 | 2 | Mechanics Companion §8 |
| Awakened Tree (optional) | 2 | 450 | 0–1 | 2024 FR Monster Manual |

**XP Budget (combat only):** 100 XP (Looselings only) or 550 XP (with Awakened Tree)
**Difficulty:** Easy without tree; Medium with tree

**This is primarily a skill challenge, not a combat encounter.**

**Skill Challenge: Repair the Forest Anchor**
- **Successes needed:** 4 / **Failures allowed:** 2
- **Applicable skills and DCs:**

| Check | DC | What It Represents |
|-------|-----|-------------------|
| Athletics | 14 | Clear root overgrowth physically |
| Nature | 13 | Identify which roots are choking the stone vs. supporting it |
| Acrobatics | 13 | Navigate the multi-level root system |
| Survival | 12 | Find the safest path to the stone |
| Arcana | 14 | Re-seat the anchor stone (required as final success) |

- Natural 20 = 2 successes (per Mechanics Companion §7)
- Failure consequence: stone shifts but doesn't seat. Partial repair. Ward at half strength.

**Awakened Tree Resolution:**
- Nature DC 14 or Persuasion DC 15 to convince the guardian the PC is helping
- If combat: the tree has AC 13, HP 59, Slam +6 (3d6+4). It fights defensively and retreats at half HP.
- Singing a Binding Song fragment: automatic success on the Nature/Persuasion check

### Encounter 3: Singing Cairn — Moor Anchor

**Location:** Western edge of Ashenmoor
**Party Level:** 5–7 (recommended: 6)

**Creatures:**
| Creature | CR | XP | Count | Source |
|----------|-----|------|-------|--------|
| Looseling | 1/4 | 50 | 3–4 | Mechanics Companion §8 |
| Thread-Eater | 2 | 450 | 1 | Mechanics Companion §8 |

**XP Budget:** 600–650 XP
**Difficulty:** Medium for effective party 1.5; Easy for effective party 2.0

**Terrain Mechanics:**
- Treacherous footing: Athletics DC 12 each round to move at full speed; failure = half speed
- Sinkhole concealment: Investigation DC 13 when entering new 5-ft. square, or fall 5 ft. into water (1d6 bludgeoning, Athletics DC 12 to climb out as action)
- Fog patches: move 20 ft. per round in random direction. Creatures inside are Heavily Obscured.
- Anchor stone leaks Fray: Thread-Eater has advantage on attacks within 15 ft. of the stone

**Action Economy Analysis:**
- Looselings emerge from peat (surprise round possible — Stealth +2 vs. passive Perception)
- Thread-Eater approaches from fog (Stealth +4). It prioritizes the anchor stone and any creature casting spells near it.
- Thread-Eater's Consume Ward targets the anchor stone first. If the PC is repairing the stone, the Thread-Eater attacks the PC to prevent repair.

**Thread-Eater Tactics (the escalation moment):**
- Uses Consume Ward on any active magic within 30 ft. (reaction, DC 14 INT save by caster)
- Prioritizes spellcasters and magical items
- Retreats into fog if reduced below 20 HP, re-emerges 1d4 rounds later
- This is many players' first Thread-Eater — narrate it as a qualitative threat escalation

### Encounter 5: Hollowreach Boundary Crossing

**Location:** Edge of Hollowreach Frayed zone
**Party Level:** 6–8

**No combat. Environmental encounter.**

**Fray Exposure Mechanics (per Mechanics Companion §3):**
| Time Inside | Save DC | Effect on Failure |
|-------------|---------|-------------------|
| Entry (first 10 min) | 12 | 1 level Fray Exposure |
| 10–20 min | 12 | 1 level Fray Exposure |
| 20–30 min | 12 | 1 level Fray Exposure |

- History DC 15: recognize architectural remnants of pre-Fraying Hollowreach
- Insight DC 12: read the body language of Hollow Court residents watching you arrive

## Act III: The Deep Weave (Levels 9–12)

### Encounter 6: Threadfall Expanse — Time Pool Ambush

**Location:** Threadfall Expanse, Time Pool at Spiral Field edge
**Party Level:** 9–10

**Creatures:**
| Creature | CR | XP | Count | Source |
|----------|-----|------|-------|--------|
| Hollow Walker | 4 | 1,100 | 2 | Mechanics Companion §8 |

**XP Budget:** 2,200 XP
**Difficulty:** Hard for effective party 1.5; Medium for effective party 2.0

**Time Pool Mechanics (custom environmental hazard):**
- 15-ft. radius shimmering depression
- Creatures starting their turn inside can use bonus action to "skip" — they vanish, reappearing at start of their next turn in any space within the pool. Effectively: 1 round of displacement + attacks of opportunity cannot target them.
- Hollow Walkers use Time Pools tactically. One engages while the other skips, alternating pressure.
- PC can also use Time Pools — same mechanic applies.

**Hollow Walker Tactics:**
- Time-stutter ability amplified in the Expanse: their natural phase-shift (bonus action, become invisible until end of turn) recharges on 4–6 instead of 5–6
- One Walker engages melee, one harasses at range with Thread Pull (30 ft., DC 14 STR save or pulled 15 ft.)
- If one is killed, the other attempts to flee via Time Pool skip

**Environmental:**
- Threadlines overhead: Arcana DC 13 to read them. Success: learn the Hollow Walkers' point of origin (a Frayed zone 3 days' march east)
- Weave state: Thin. No encounter table modifications, but perception at disadvantage beyond 60 ft.

### Encounter 7: The Knotwood — The Living Maze

**Location:** Knotwood interior
**Party Level:** 9–11

**Skill Challenge: Navigate the Living Maze**
- **Successes needed:** 5 / **Failures allowed:** 3
- **Applicable skills and DCs:**

| Check | DC | What It Represents |
|-------|-----|-------------------|
| Wisdom save | 14 | Resist disorientation from shifting paths |
| Nature | 13 | Read the forest's intent and navigate harmoniously |
| Survival | 15 | Maintain direction as paths change |
| Perception | 14 | Spot path changes before they close behind you |
| Insight | 13 | Understand what the forest wants from you |
| Performance | 12 | Sing a Binding Song fragment (calms the forest) |
| Athletics | 16 | Force through closing root passages (only if hostile) |

**Special Rules:**
- Singing a Binding Song fragment: automatic success on one check (usable once)
- Damaging the forest (fire, cutting roots, Force damage) shifts the maze to **hostile mode**:
  - All DCs increase by +2
  - Roots grapple: Athletics DC 16 to escape (per 2024 FR Grappled condition)
  - Insect swarm: 2d4 piercing damage per round (no save, environmental)
  - Path sealing: lose 1 success from tally
- Communication (speaking aloud, touching roots respectfully): grants advantage on next Nature or Insight check

**Outcomes:**
- **Success (5 successes):** Direct path to Weaver's Mark. Forest opens the Listening Glade for Act III Side Quest #4.
- **Failure (3 failures):** Reach the Mark but: 1 level of exhaustion, forest wariness (no Listening Glade access).
- **Critical failure (3 failures while hostile):** Ejected from the Knotwood. Must re-enter and try again (DCs remain elevated).

### Encounter 8: Weavers' Cradle Descent

**Location:** The Descent spiral path
**Party Level:** 10–12

**No combat. Environmental gauntlet.**

**Weave Density Saves (progressive):**
| Depth | CON Save DC | Effect on Failure |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| Outer rim (first 500 ft.) | 13 | Stunned until end of next turn (sensory overload) |
| Mid-descent (500–1000 ft.) | 14 | Stunned 1 round + 2d6 psychic damage |
| Inner approach (1000+ ft.) | 15 | Stunned 1 round + 3d6 psychic damage + 1 level exhaustion |

**Weaver Consciousness Saves:**
| Depth | WIS Save DC | Effect on Failure |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| Outer rim | 14 | Vision fragment (disorienting, disadvantage on next check, but grants 1 piece of Weaver lore) |
| Mid-descent | 15 | Longer vision (1 round lost to vision, 2d6 psychic, but grants significant Weaver lore) |
| Inner approach | 16 | Full vision (Incapacitated 1 round, 3d6 psychic, but grants critical Weaver lore essential for Act V) |

**Design Note:** Failing these saves is *informative*, not purely punitive. Each failure delivers a vision fragment that builds the player's understanding of the Weavers. A player who fails every save arrives at the First Loom Stone exhausted but deeply informed. A player who passes every save arrives fresh but with less narrative context. This is intentional — the Cradle rewards vulnerability.

**Wynn's Assistance:** If Wynn Hollowscript is a companion, all WIS save DCs are reduced by 2 (her linguistic framework provides a mental anchor).

## Act IV: The Three Paths (Levels 13–16)

### Encounter 9: Keystone Assault (Any Path)

**Location:** Any of the Seven Keystone sites
**Party Level:** 13–15

**Wave Defense Encounter (6 rounds of Keystone work)**

| Wave | Round | Creatures | CR Total | XP |
|------|-------|-----------|----------|-----|
| 1 | 1–2 | 4 Looselings (CR 1/4) + 2 Thread-Puppets (CR 1) | 1.5 | 600 |
| 2 | 3–4 | 2 Thread-Eaters (CR 2) + 1 Frayed One (CR 2) | 6 | 1,350 |
| 3 | 5–6 | 1 Hollow Walker (CR 4) + 2 Thread-Eaters (CR 2) | 8 | 2,000 |
| Escalation | 7+ | 1 Weft Spider (CR 8) | 8 | 3,900 |

**Total XP Budget (all waves):** 7,850 XP
**Difficulty:** Hard/Deadly (intended — this is Act IV's signature combat)

**Keystone Work Mechanics:**
- 6 rounds to complete (Binding ceremony / Ward Engine installation / communication array)
- Requires 1 creature to spend their action each round on the work (Arcana DC 14, or DC 12 with Threadcraft proficiency)
- Failed check: no progress that round
- Interruption (working creature takes damage): progress resets by 1 round

**Companion/Ally Roles:**
- Each companion handles a secondary flank (roll 1d6 per round: 1–2 = ally takes damage and needs healing, 3–5 = ally holds, 6 = ally eliminates their flank)
- Faction allies present based on path chosen: Threadwalkers (Restoration), Guild engineers (Release), Speakers (Harmony)

**Weft Spider Tactics (first appearance):**
- Arrives from above (climb 40 ft.) on round 7 if work takes longer than 6 rounds
- Uses Threadline Web immediately to create difficult terrain
- Redirect Spell targets any buff or healing the PC casts
- Prioritizes the Keystone (Weave Snare on the working creature)

## Act V: The Binding (Levels 17–20)

### Encounter 11: Restoration Finale — The Loom Chamber

**Location:** Cavern beneath the First Loom Stone
**Party Level:** 17–19

**Phase 1: The Approach (diplomatic)**
- No combat unless diplomacy fails
- The Weavers manifest as 1–3 luminous thread-beings (Weaver Fragments)
- Communication: Performance (Binding Song) DC 16, Persuasion DC 18, or Arcana DC 17 to establish contact
- Each successful check de-escalates. 3 successes = full communication. The Weavers recognize mortal intent.

**Phase 2: If Diplomacy Fails (combat)**

**New Creature: Weaver Fragment**

| Creature | CR | XP | Count | Notes |
|----------|-----|------|-------|-------|
| Weaver Fragment | 16 | 15,000 | 1–3 | See stat block below |

**Weaver Fragment (CR 16)**
*Large celestial (aberration), chaotic neutral*

- **AC** 19 (woven light) | **HP** 200 (16d10 + 112) | **Speed** 0 ft., fly 60 ft. (hover)
- **STR** 10 (+0) **DEX** 20 (+5) **CON** 24 (+7) **INT** 26 (+8) **WIS** 22 (+6) **CHA** 24 (+7)
- **Saves:** INT +14, WIS +12, CHA +13
- **Immunities:** force, psychic, radiant; all conditions except Incapacitated and Unconscious
- **Resistances:** all damage from nonmagical attacks
- **Senses:** Truesight 120 ft. | **Languages:** all, telepathy 300 ft.
- **CR** 16 (15,000 XP) | **Prof** +5

**Traits:**
- **Incorporeal Movement.** As Loom-Ghost.
- **Legendary Resistance (3/Day).**
- **Magic Resistance.**
- **Tapestry Weaver.** The Fragment can reshape reality within 60 ft. as a lair action. This is not hostile — it's *instinctive*. The Fragment tries to "weave" mortals into the Tapestry because that's all it knows.

**Actions:**
- **Multiattack:** 2 Pattern Weave attacks.
- **Pattern Weave.** Ranged Spell Attack: +14, range 120 ft. Hit: 27 (4d8+8) radiant + 14 (4d6) force. Target must DC 19 CHA save or be Restrained as reality threads bind them (escape: DC 19 STR or CHA check).
- **Absorb into Tapestry (Recharge 5-6).** One Restrained creature within 60 ft. must DC 19 CHA save. Failure: transported to a pocket dimension inside the Tapestry. The creature is Incapacitated and takes 22 (4d10) radiant at start of each turn. Another creature can free them with DC 19 Arcana or by singing the Binding Song (Performance DC 16).

**Legendary Actions (3/round):**
- **Shift (1).** Teleport 60 ft.
- **Thread Burst (2).** 20-ft. radius, DC 19 DEX, 28 (8d6) radiant, half on save.
- **Reweave (3).** Reshape 30-ft. cube of terrain. Walls appear, floors shift, passages seal/open.

**CR Verification:**
- Defensive: 200 HP × 2 (immunities/resistances) = 400 effective HP → CR 14. + Magic Resistance + Legendary Resistance = CR 17. Adjustment for incorporeal -1 → CR 16.
- Offensive: ~41 DPR (2 × Pattern Weave) + Absorb into Tapestry (save-or-suck) → effective CR 15.
- Average: (16 + 15) / 2 ≈ CR 16 ✓

**XP Budget (1–3 Fragments):** 15,000–45,000 XP
**Difficulty:** Deadly (intentional — combat is the failure state)

**The Binding Song Resolution (preferred):**
- Performance DC 16 per round, sustained over 5 rounds
- Each success calms one Fragment. 3 successes = all Fragments de-escalate.
- The Seventh Verse (if discovered in Act III): automatic success on one round
- Sorrel's "Unfinished Verse" companion quest completion: automatic success on one round

### Encounter 13: Harmony Finale — The First Song

**Location:** Weavers' Cradle, Hollowing night
**Party Level:** 18–20

**The First Song — Extended Skill Challenge**
- **Successes needed:** 8 / **Failures allowed:** 4
- **This is the largest skill challenge in the game.**

**Round-by-round escalation:**

| Round | What Happens | Additional Effects |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| 1–2 | The Song begins. Speakers at Keystones harmonize. | Base DCs |
| 3–4 | Fray creatures assault the perimeter. Allies defend. | DC +1, combat interruption possible |
| 5–6 | The Weavers begin to respond. Threadlines stabilize visually. | DC -1 (the Weavers are helping), but CON DC 16 for sensory overload |
| 7–8 | The climax. The Fray presses hardest. The Weavers' full consciousness presses against all Speakers. | DC +2, WIS DC 17 or overwhelmed |

**Applicable Checks:**

| Check | DC (base) | What It Represents |
|-------|-----------|-------------------|
| Performance | 15 | Sustain the Binding Song |
| Arcana | 16 | Channel Threadcraft energy into the Song |
| Religion | 14 | Invoke the Loom-Born (they manifest and add their voices) |
| Persuasion | 16 | Rally faltering Speakers |
| Constitution save | 15 | Endure sensory overload from Weaver consciousness |

**Automatic Successes:**
- The Seventh Verse (Act III discovery): 1 automatic success
- Sorrel's "Unfinished Verse" (companion quest): 1 automatic success
- Wynn's "The Silence Speaks" (companion quest): 1 automatic success (the Weavers reply)
- Aldith's Fifth Verse (Act V NPC): 1 automatic success

**Combat Interruption (Rounds 3–6):**
| Creature | CR | XP | Count | Timing |
|----------|-----|------|-------|--------|
| The Unspun | 5 | 1,800 | 1 | Breaches perimeter in round 3 or 4 |

- The PC must defeat or drive off The Unspun without breaking the Song
- Breaking the Song to fight: -1 success from tally
- Fighting while maintaining Song: all combat rolls at disadvantage, but Song continues
- Companion handles it: if a companion's approval is Devoted, they automatically handle The Unspun (no PC action needed)

**Fray Exposure During the Song:**
- CON DC 15 save at end of rounds 5, 6, 7, 8
- Failure: 1 level Fray Exposure
- The Song itself provides partial protection: saves at advantage if the player has 4+ successes already

**Outcomes:**
- **8 successes:** The First Song completes. The Weavers and mortals sing together. The Threadlines stabilize. The Fray retreats. The world is fundamentally changed — Speakers can maintain the weave forever.
- **4 failures before 8 successes:** The Song fractures. Partial stabilization. The Weavers retreat deeper. The Thinning slows but doesn't stop. A bittersweet ending — the world survives but the conversation must continue. The next generation of Speakers will try again.
- **XP:** 20,000 (completion) or 10,000 (partial)

## RAW Deviations

| Deviation | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|
| Weaver Fragment uses CHA for Restrained escape | The Tapestry binds pattern, not physical form. Strength of personality resists absorption. Per 2024 FR, CHA saves resist planar banishment — analogous mechanic. |
| Time Pool "skip" mechanic | No RAW equivalent for temporal displacement. Modeled as a variant of the *Blink* spell effect but voluntary and location-locked. |
| Summit Debate as 7-round social challenge | D&D has no formalized political debate mechanic. Built on the Binding Song skill challenge framework (Mechanics Companion §7) with faction-specific DCs replacing fixed DCs. |
| Fray Exposure saves during the First Song at advantage with 4+ successes | Mechanical reward for skill challenge progress — the Song is literally protecting the singer. Grounds the narrative in mechanics. |
